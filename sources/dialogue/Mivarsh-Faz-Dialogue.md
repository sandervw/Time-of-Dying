# Mivarsh Faz - Dialogue and Internal Thoughts

Extracted from *The Worm Ouroboros* by E.R. Eddison

---

## IX: Salapanta Hills
(Lines 1119-1322)

### First Introduction - Interrogation by Juss

> "O devil transmarine, pity of thine ignorance sith thou know'st not Mivarsh Faz."

> "Woe worth the woe that is fallen upon all the land of Impland!"

> "Out harrow and alas for Fax Fay Faz and Illarosh Faz and Lurmesh Faz and Gandassa Faz and all the great ones in the land!"

> "Curse ye bitterly Philpritz Faz, which betrayed us into the hand of the devil ultramontane in the castle of Orpish."

### Describing Corund and the Northern Conspiracy

> "O devils transmarine, ye give me strong waters which comfort my soul, and ye speak me soft words. But shall I not fear soft words? Soft words were spoke by this devil ultramontane, when he and cursed Philpritz spake soft words unto us in Orpish: unto me, and unto Fax Fay Faz, and Gandassa, and Illarosh, and unto all of us, after our overthrow in battle against him by the banks of Arlan."

*When asked what Corund looks like:*

> "He hath a great yellow beard beflecked with gray, and a bald shiny pate, and standeth big as a neat."

> "A three days' journey north-west of this, at Tormerish in Achery."

*On what Corund was doing:*

> "Still devising evil."

> "Against Zeldornius, which is a devil transmarine."

> "With another devil from beyond seas; I have forgot his name."

*Describing Jalcanaius Fostus:*

> "He hath about my bigness. His eyes be bright, and he somewhat favoureth this one [pointing at Spitfire], though belike he hath not all so fierce a face. He is lean-faced and dark of skin. He goeth in black iron."

*Confirming the name:*

> "Ay."

> "And when they have Zeldornius slain, then must they help this bald-pate in his undertakings."

*On paying for counsel:*

> "Even so."

*On Corund's military strength:*

> "The greatest strength that he can make of devils ultramontane is as I think two score hundred. Many Imps beside will follow him, but they have but our country weapons."

## X: The Marchlands of the Moruna
(Lines 1323-1422)

### Warning Against the Moruna

> "Eastward, all ways lead to the Moruna. None may go thither and not die."

### Arrival of Corund's Forces - Warning the Demons

> "I have eat your salt, and moreover ye are against this naughty wicked baldhead that came over the mountains to oppress us. Therefore I would do you good. But I can little. For I am poor, that was rich in land and fee. And I am alone, that had formerly five hundred spearmen lodging in my halls to do my pleasure."

> "He must be upon you in an hour or twain."

*And he fell a-weeping.*

### On Eshgrar Ogo

> "This is so. But never heard I of any so witless as go thither. Here where we stand is the land fearsome enough; but Eshgrar Ogo standeth at the very edge of the Moruna. No man hath harboured there these hundred years."

*When asked if it still stands:*

> "For all I wot of."

*On its strength:*

> "In old times it was thought no place stronger. But ye were as well die here by the hand of the devils ultramontane, as there be torn in pieces by bad spirits."

### On the Passage of Time

> "Methought you should have been gotten clean away ere this. This is but nine or ten days' journey, and 'tis now the sixteenth day since ye did leave me on Salapanta Hills."

*[Internal state: Mivarsh slept not, for terror of hob-thrushes from the Moruna.]*

## XII: Koshtra Pivrarcha
(Lines 1627-1888)

### Refusing to Enter the Mountains

> "I was born in the high valley of Perarshyn by the upper waters of the Beirun in Impland. There boys scarce toddle ere they can climb a rock. This climb affrights me not, nor those mountains. But the land is unknown and terrible, and many loathly ones inhabit it, ghosts and eaters of men. O devils transmarine, and my friends, is it not enough? Let us turn again, and if the Gods save our lives we shall be famous for ever, that came unto Morna Moruna and returned alive."

*[Internal state: Mivarsh, bethinking him that if the mantichores of the mountains should devour him along with those two lords, that were yet a kindlier fate than all alone to abide those things he wist of on the Moruna, put on the rope...]*

### Fear of the Crocodile

> "Save me! let me fly! give me my weapons! It was foretold me by a wise woman that a cocadrill-serpent must devour me at last!"

*[He began to scream with terror.]*

> "It is an ill fish, and it sore dislikes me."

> "It was a wise woman foretold it me, that such a kind of serpent must be my bane. Yet be it according to your will."

### The Mantichore Fight

*[Mivarsh took his bow and set an arrow on the string.]*

*[Internal action: Mivarsh had saved him (Juss), seizing him by the foot and drawing him back to safety when the beast fell.]*

*[Mivarsh bound his wounds and laid him tenderly as he might back against the cliff, then peered down a long while to know if the beast were dead indeed.]*

### Continuing Despite Fear

*[Internal state: But Mivarsh put on a hardy face. Never a word he spake, but nodded his head, as who should say, "Forward."]*

### Surviving the Storm - On the Demons' Endurance

> "Ye be devils, but of myself I marvel. For I have dwelt by snow mountains all my days, and many I wot of that have been benighted on the snows in wild weather. And not one but was starved by reason of the cold. I speak of them that were found. Many were not found, for the spirits devoured them."

## Character Notes

**Full Name:** Mivarsh Faz

**Origin:** The high valley of Perarshyn, by the upper waters of the Beirun in Impland

**Former Status:** Rich in land and fee, with five hundred spearmen lodging in his halls

**Skills:**
- Climbing (learned from childhood in mountain country)
- Harp playing and singing
- Archery

**Character Traits:**
- Superstitious (believes in wise women's prophecies, fears "hob-thrushes" and spirits)
- Loyal (stays with the Demons despite terror, saves Juss's life)
- Brave when necessary (puts on "a hardy face" and continues despite fear)
- Practical and honest about dangers

**Prophesied Fate:** A wise woman foretold he would be devoured by a "cocadrill-serpent" (crocodile) - which came to pass at the Lake of Ravary.

**Notable Actions:**
- Warned the Demons about Corund's approach
- Played harp and sang before the fortalice walls
- Saved Lord Juss's life during the mantichore fight by seizing him and pulling him back from the edge
- Bound Juss's wounds after the battle
- Survived a night storm on Koshtra Pivrarcha's slopes
- Died attempting to ride the hippogriff
