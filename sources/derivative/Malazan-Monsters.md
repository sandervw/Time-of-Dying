## Azath

Azath is like a Deck of Dragons with each house providing access to warrens, Fiddler et al found an enormous spread of tiles inside Tremorlor with the whole Universe mapped, each house has a Guardian, houses of Azath appeared wherever they were most needed but no one knew how, Nameless Ones are the Servants of Azath though Kellanved tried to remove them from Malazan empire, Azath chose who to allow to enter

## Barghast

warring tribes from Genabackis; we have met Humbral Taur \- leader of White Face clan; Trotts in Bridgeburners was a Barghast; seem to be related to Toblakai; related to Moranths; hated and killed Tiste Edur; 

## Deck of Dragons

"a structure imposed on the power of warrens", each card a gate into a warren, its reading provides a sight into how various powers are placed, new cards appeared and some old ones disappeared when power shifted, House of Dark doesn't show on the Deck, Paran, as Jen'isand Rul, is new ruler

## Deragoth

Hounds of Darkness; thick long neck; broad flattened head; bulging jaw muscles; scarred black hides; dark eyes; as large as a big horse; they were present in Raraku's early memories that L'oric visited, they had domesticated early versions of humans; drove K'Chain Che'malle away from Seven Cities; there were seven of them and Dissembleckis made a pact with them to make them Seven Protectors against Nameless Ones; Seven Cities were named after them; one of them was killed in Raraku by Karsa; 

## Dragnipur

the sword forged in darkness, chains souls to the world that existed before the coming of light, wielded by Rake, black sword trailing smoky chains, forged by Draconus who was slained by Rake with this same sword, carries a always moving wagon within itself which contains the gate to the Kurald Galain, the wagon is constantly moving away from Chaos which is following it, that's why it needs souls to keep moving; no one ever escaped before Paran (is this what made Paran an Ascendant?); 

## Drift Avalii

a floating island off the coast of Quon Tali; Andarist and Rake's grandchildren guarded the true Throne of Shadow; only one possible landing area; structures on the island were built by Edur; After Andarist's death, Traveller becomes the guard; 

## Forkrul Assail

also known as Krussail; strong but disinterested; one of the founding races; very long arms with too many joints; in the conflict of T'lan Imass and Jaghut, Forkrul acted as impartial arbiters; they are better fighters than Toblakai and Tiste Edur; they have pale blue blood; their wounds self heal; 

## Gothos

Jaghut, wrote Gothos's Folly \- the oldest tome, Guardian of Deadhouse in Malaz City

## Jaghut

also known as Jhag or Shurl, individualistic, treated communities as source of corruption, one of the founding races, lived alone in remote dwellings, feared each other, used ice as a passive weapon

## K'Chain Che'Malle

Another of the ancient civilizations mentioned, but not described, one of the founding races, Morn was one of their ancient cities, first encountered attacking Gruntle and company in Book 3, they have flat, wide, fang-bristling faces \- sickly pale like snake bellies \- with empty pits for eyes, large black-pocked iron swords are fused to their wrists, they are reptilian and stride on their hind legs balanced by a long tapering tail, this race is more ancient than even Elder Gods, taloned three-toed feet

## Seguleh

## Silanda

ship Gesler's crew found in warren fragment called Nascent); three-masted black dromon from Quon, the only craft allowed to trade with the Tiste Andii, went missing on the way to Drift Avalii when Emperor's forces overran Quon; now headless bodies man the oars; before Gesler, Kesla had been onboard, killed all the Tiste Edur warriros and their mage; severed heads on board belonged mostly to Tiste Andii and a few humans; the ship passed through warren of dragons and delivered Gesler & company to Seven Cities, then collected injured of Coltaine's Chain and reached Aren; ship is made of wood from Drift Avalii; 

## Teblor

residents of Laederon Plateau; divided into clans, among them Uryd, Rathyd and Sunyd; descendants of Jaghuts (?) fleeing from T'lan Imass, these Jaghuts forgot their past as advised by Icarium; worship Seven Faces of the Rock; have four lungs; immune to infections; tall and well built; long lives; war-oriented people; could run as fast as a horse; treat humans as children; Uryd and Rathyd untouched by civilization; Sunyd have been betrayed by their greedy young generation; Uryd preferred spears and atlatl over bows; Teblor used blood-oil to harden their wooden blades, and to heighten their senses; naturally buoyant; Teblor are actually Thelomen Toblakai, their blood runs in Barghast and Trell (note: Fenn seem different); Toblakai are from the time T'lan Imass were still flesh; 

## Tiste Andii

## Tiste Edur

## Tiste Liosan

## T'lann Imass

known as Silent Host among Malazan, didn't speak, only Commander Logros did, Bonecasters stayed out of sight \- only one to ever appear was Olar Ethil who stood alongside clan chieftain Eitholos Ilm during battle of Kartool, Bonecasters could achieve much higher sorcery levels than Tiste Andii, killed by chopping body to pieces. Imass can cross water bodies but can only take shape on land, one of the founding races, became immortal to eradicate Jaghuts as a solution to the ice created by Jhags, Silverfox was their latest ruler, T'lann Imass created their own warren through the Ritual thus abandoning their old realm of spirits, they outlasted their gods through the Vow of Immortality and now feel alone
