# LORE:

The Time of Dying began 3000 years ago, when [seven great necromancers]{.underline} combined their power and their armies of the dead to wipe out the kingdoms, empires, and nations that would not serve them. Life was wiped out across the continent; only the [Road of Graves]{.underline} remained. The Road, it is said, runs in one main course without ends; over mountains and under rivers; through forests and caves and castles; sometimes curving, rarely straight. When any man dies, his gravestone is added to the cobbles of the road. The Road is ever expanding.

The necromancers' acolytes, servants, and slaves were to form the [dwellers]{.underline}; the basis of modern man; the ones who dwell upon this road. Only necromancy holds the world together, for only necromancy can persist after death. All other magics and gods have fallen in The Time of Dying.

The sun is a cold, slate color.

## Gods:

The seven gods of this world have only survived by means of their necromancy.

With no more external enemy to threaten them, the seven great necromancers turned on one another. [The necromancers fought; most have died at least once.]{.underline} Repeated deaths, confused doctrines, lingual drift; all contributed to a decline in the power of the seven. Once immortal gods, all seven necromancers are now cursed to different flavors of pseudo-immortality, living on the mountain they created. They are worshipped still, though their power is limited.

**The Thin Man (Sloth):** God of Sickness and Entropy

- An old man locked to his Sickened Cradle, who shared his dreams with his follows

- Bears the gift of perpetual youth united with perpetual sleep.

- The finding of his Sickened Cradle (tomb) is a goal of his followers - opening it will (supposedly) unleash a plague that brings final rest to all the living and the dead, inflicting final entropy on The Road of Graves

**The Old Man Below (Pride):** God of History and Age

- "Death stalks the land with a suit, a dapper smile, and silver laughter."

- A roving spirit that issues forth from [The End of the Road]{.underline} (possibly a metaphysical concept)

- Whispers instructions in shadowed corners, conniving and scheming to return to power

- Most powerful of the necromancers (in life)

**The Scratcher (Wrath):** God of Blood and Curses

**The Mother of Worms (Lust):** God of Decay

- A Vampire; lives off the blood of her consort-followers

- Beautiful on the outside, but rotten inside

- Inspired by Akivasha; always nude, always deadly

**Gallbladder (The Fat Man, 'His Grand Corpulency'; Gluttony):** God of Consumption

- Worshipped by the mutants of The Lake of Tomatoes

- Strongest servants are the Grootslang - many-trunked elephant-snakes

**Lady Horsehair (The Girl Doll; Greed):** God of Possession

**The Red King (Envy):** God of Persecution

### Necromancy:

**Necromancy Motifs**

- "Concerns the design of reusable magic in the field of necromancy."

- "By using the motifs described here in your incantation design, you will create spells and rituals that may be used in a variety of contexts."

<!-- -->

- **Shape Motifs (Creational)** - define how an undead creature is created. Summoning words define the what, who, how, and when of undead creation.

  - ***Classifiers** (Abstract Factory) provides an incantation for creating related or dependent undead without specifying their concrete type (IE. an EvilUndeadFactory, a GoodUndeadFactory, a sentientUndeadFactory)*

  - ***Constructors** (Builder) build complex undead by separating formation and representation (IE.. a ToeToScalpConstructor, an EssenceCollectionConstructor, a FromDustToLivingFleshConstructor a BloodSacrificeConstructor).*

    - *Creating a complicated undead creature directly within a spell is inflexible. It commits the spell to creating a particular type of the undead and makes it impossible to change the type later independently from (without having to change) the spell."*

  - ***Assimilators** (Factory method) creates undead without specifying the exact type to create.*

    - *Occasionally, it is desirable to summon an undead creature without specifying the specific type of that undead. Suppose I want to create a spell that conjures a servant with a specific capability: IE. patrolling a city road, or telling me what it knew in life. In this case, I am not concerned with the specific type - whether it is corporeal or incorporeal - only that it can perform the desired function. Assimilator Motifs allow this by allowing me to specify a function or attribute of the necromancy I wish to perform, without knowing the details of creature types.*

  - ***TODO Emulators** (Prototype) creates objects by cloning an existing object.*

  - ***True names** (Singleton) restricts summoned undead to one specific soul or not-soul.*

- **TODO Desire Motifs (Structural)** - concern undead capability. Desire motifs define ways of creating undead with new or enhanced functionality.

  - ***Attunments** (Adapter) allows a spell which imparts certain abilities to impart other abilities (IE. changing strength to speed; changing undead sight to sight-through-undead, etc).*

  - *Bridge decouples an abstraction from its implementation so that the two can vary independently.*

  - ***Amalgamators** (Composite) treat groups of similar magical effects or undead as a single entity, allowing control over larger groups of undead, or control of a variety of spell effects at once.*

  - *Decorator dynamically adds/overrides behavior in an existing method of an object.*

  - *Facade provides a simplified interface to a large body of code.*

  - *Flyweight reduces the cost of creating and manipulating a large number of similar objects.*

  - *Proxy provides a placeholder for another object to control access, reduce cost, and reduce complexity.*

- **TODO Nature Motifs (Behavioral)** - concerns the behavior-of, control-of, and communication-with undead.

  - *Chain of responsibility delegates commands to a chain of processing objects.*

  - *Command creates objects that encapsulate actions and parameters.*

  - *Interpreter implements a specialized language.*

  - *Iterator accesses the elements of an object sequentially without exposing its underlying representation.*

  - *Mediator allows loose coupling between classes by being the only class that has detailed knowledge of their methods.*

  - *Memento provides the ability to restore an object to its previous state (undo).*

  - *Observer is a publish/subscribe pattern, which allows a number of observer objects to see an event.*

  - *State allows an object to alter its behavior when its internal state changes.*

  - *Strategy allows one of a family of algorithms to be selected on-the-fly at runtime.*

# LOCATIONS:

## The Road:

The Road of Graves is endless interred, stone-after-stone, from its unknown beginning to its undiscovered end. The Road is surrounded by [The Cursed Forest]{.underline}, a wasteland of trees, and poison pools, and roaming un-dead.

Some graves - Tohmbs and Chrypts - are more elaborate that others. Not simple stones in the road, they are deep underground vaults. [Chryptsackers]{.underline} - plunderers - are professionals in the art of looting these valuable troves.

There are trees, but they sprout from the ground rotting and skeletal; there are leaves but they don't come from the trees - they come seemingly born on the Wind of some faraway plane of Forever-autumn, already fallen and colored with decay.

The only birds are: Ravens, Crows, whip-poor-wills, and vultures
