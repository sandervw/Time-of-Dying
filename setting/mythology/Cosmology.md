# Cosmology: The Road of Graves

## Overview

The world is the Road of Graves - an endless road paved with the gravestones of every person who has ever died. It runs in one main course without ends; over mountains and under rivers; across bridges of its own paving stones; through forests, caves, and castles; sometimes curving, rarely straight. The Road is ever-expanding. When any man dies, his gravestone is added to the cobbles of the Road.

The sun is cold and slate-colored.

The Road is surrounded by a wasteland of forests, poison pools, and roaming undead. Trees sprout from the ground rotting and skeletal. Leaves don't come from the trees; they arrive born on the wind of some faraway plane of forever-autumn, already fallen and colored with decay.

The only birds are ravens, crows, whip-poor-wills, and vultures.

Some graves - Tombs and Chrypts - are more elaborate than simple stones. They are deep underground vaults. Chryptsackers are professionals in the art of looting these valuable troves.

## Divine Weather Curses

Necromancer-gods can impose long-duration regional weather curses tied to calendar periods. A permanent rainstorm bound to commence each spring is one known example. Such curses can outlast the memory of their original purpose.
