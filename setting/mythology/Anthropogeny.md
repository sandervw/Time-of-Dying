# Anthropogeny: The Dwellers

## Overview

The necromancers' acolytes, servants, and slaves formed the dwellers - the basis of modern man; the ones who dwell upon the Road of Graves. Almost all civilization is built along the Road itself.

## Civilization

Almost all places of the living are built along the Road. Notable settlements:

- **Middlemoss** - lies in "The Middle of the Road," also in the middle of a cliff along which the Road runs; paper lanterns buzzing faintly with glowbugs inside; massive fungal conservatory with mushroom bonsai
- **Thistlecrown** - a motte and bailey where the Road runs over the tallest mountain; half-frozen cabins; lies in the shadow of a high, frozen cathedral to The Old Man Below
- **Tohmbtown**
- **Hornwater** - a major city south of the Meathook Mountains, anchored on Hornwater Lake; governed by three ruling guilds
- **Alsoburg** - a cave-town on the Scarab Mines route; functions as a supply stop
- **The Rat Farmer's Barn**

## Professions

- **Chryptsacker** - professionals who loot old tohmbs and chrypts in search of treasure
- **Tailor** - sells the clothes from graves (and any other goods)
- **Resurrecter** - men who dig up graves
- **Mance/Mancer** - necromancer
- **Squeaker** - a man who traps giant forest rats for meat; has a home-base plus several outlying safety lodges, warded against undead
- **Leech** - mundane healer

## Culture

- Spelling conventions: Chrypt, Grave, Tohmb (instead of crypt, grave, tohmb)
- People keep bugs (praying mantis), baby goats, pigs, and chickens as pets
- Clothing includes grave clothes
