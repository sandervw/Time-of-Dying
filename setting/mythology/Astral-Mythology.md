# Astral Mythology: The Slate Sun

## Overview

The sky of the Time of Dying holds a cold, slate-colored sun. The harridan's moon is yellow, and marks the night of witches. No stars are described in the known setting. The celestial bodies carry practical significance for necromancy and the undead -- certain creatures and spells are constrained by the presence or absence of sky and moonlight.

## The Slate Sun

The sun provides light but no warmth. It is cold and slate-colored. Certain necromantic creations are restricted by its presence:

- **Golems** (wax, glass, leather) cannot be created under the sky or sun.

The Sleep Tombs reference mancers who entered eternal slumber "to be awakened again in a better age, with a new sun dawned" -- implying a belief that the slate sun is a symptom of the current age, and that a different sun existed before, or may exist again.

## The Harridan's Moon

The harridan's moon is yellow. It marks the night of witches. Certain undead can only be summoned under its light:

- **Gallowspooks** may only be summoned under the light of a harridan's moon.

## Stars and Other Celestial Phenomena

No stars are described in the setting. The Holtsatr (Forest Elves), now extinct for roughly 600 years, were translucent and blind, with "innards of starlight" -- the only reference to starlight in the setting, and it describes a biological trait rather than an astronomical one.

**Will-o-wisps** produce glowballs so bright they blind any who look on them and steal all sound. Whether these are considered celestial or purely terrestrial phenomena is undefined.

## Relationship to Necromancy

The sky and celestial bodies serve as constraints on necromantic practice. The golem restriction (cannot be created under the sky or sun) and the Gallowspook restriction (requires the harridan's moon) suggest the celestial bodies interact with necromancy in ways not yet fully defined.

Among witches, necromancy is referred to as the "Words of Rotten Syllables." Whether witches derive particular power from the harridan's moon (which marks their night) is not yet specified.
