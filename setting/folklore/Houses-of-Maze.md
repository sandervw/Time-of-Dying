# Houses of Maze

## Overview

The Houses of Maze are a multi-city criminal guild network. Each House is a physical labyrinth -- not a building with rooms, but a crawlspace warren of dusty cavities between wall skins, nailed crawlspaces, and twisting stucco cracks. The architecture itself is the weapon: intruders are disoriented, enslaved, or driven mad by the maze.

## Structure

- Each House is governed by a **Master** -- a figure described as "Master of the Maze, Master of the Puzzle mask, Lord of the Heart-hiding Smile." The Master controls information flow and passage through the House
- **Rat couriers** serve as the guild's messengers, scurrying through walls in set patterns (always four, six, or twelve rats at scheduled hours). They steal paper and secrets -- especially documents
- The guild trades in **information brokerage**: the Master perceives more of what transpires in a city than perhaps any other person, but dispenses that knowledge sparingly and cruelly

## Practices

- **Maze-slavery**: intruders who enter a House without leave are trapped and enslaved. The maze itself breaks the will through disorientation, starvation, and exposure to maddening stimuli
- Houses vary in their defenses. One known House used mirrors -- reflection and refraction to dazzle the eyes. Another (in Middlemoss) relied on physical labyrinth complexity, rat swarms, and the Master's narcotic pipe-vapors
- The Master's pipe clouds inflict confusion, despair, hysteria, or terror on mazed senses

## Known Locations

- **Middlemoss** -- A House sits on one side of the Athidal gorge. The Middlemoss House is where the guild's presence is most documented
- Houses operate across multiple cities along the Road, implying a network rather than a single institution

## Killing the Master

The only known exit from a House is through the Master. Killing the Master opens the way out. This is whispered among long-term captives but never stated openly -- the maze punishes loose talk as readily as trespass.
