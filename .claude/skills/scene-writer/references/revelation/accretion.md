# Accretion

This is the main body of the scene — the longest phase, where fragments stack in deployment order and the protagonist circles closer to comprehension without yet arriving. If the user provided **scene priorities**, keep these in mind as you write.

Before writing, consult your working document's **POV Stance** and **Tipping Mechanism** declarations. The stance constrains how fragments are accessed (vantage, movement, social cover, sensory reliability). The mechanism dictates the trajectory the accretion bends along — what *kind* of pattern the protagonist is failing to complete.

## Texture Budget

Draw on **most of the remaining fragments** from your working document's inventory, deploying them in the order listed. Leave **1–2 fragments in reserve** for the epiphany — and you must always reserve the **TIP**. The TIP is never spent in this phase. Weave evidence into action, observation, or dialogue rather than piling it on as description.

## Guiding Questions

Use the following questions to shape your accretion. You do not need to answer "yes" to every one — they are priorities to weigh, not a checklist to complete. Let the scene's needs dictate which matter most.

1. **Fragment order**: Are the working doc's mid-sequence fragments deployed in the order listed, and is the TIP fragment unspent?
2. **Stance continuity**: Does the POV stance remain physically active — vantage maintained, social cover held, investigation continued, impairment ongoing — rather than fading into invisible narration?
3. **Reader pacing**: Is the reader allowed to outpace the protagonist on individual fragments?
4. **Reblocking**: Does the protagonist move, shift posture, or change vantage at least once within the stance's constraints?
5.  **Withholding the pattern**: Are connections between fragments left implicit — visible to the reader to assemble, but not narrated as a thesis?
6.  **Embedded delivery**: Are fragments delivered as lived experience (action, dialogue, sensory observation) rather than as lists, summaries, or expository blocks?
7.  **Mechanism foreshadow**: Is there at least one beat that hints at the tipping mechanism's *shape* (a flicker of scale, a near-recognition, a half-glimpsed loop, a noted anomaly) without delivering the actual tip?
8. **Friction**: Is there an in-scene reason the protagonist cannot simply leave — a physical, social, or psychological hook that holds them in place?

## Wordcount Constraint

The total finished scene should be approximately **[WORDCOUNT]** words. The accretion should account for **55–65%** of that total.

If your accretion exceeds 75% of the target wordcount, **rewrite it shorter**. Wordcount discipline is more important than addressing every guiding question. Cut the question, not the budget.
