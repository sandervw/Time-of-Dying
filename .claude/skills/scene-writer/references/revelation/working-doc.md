### POV Stance

Pick **exactly one**:

- **Hidden observer** — Protagonist watches unseen from a fixed, often precarious vantage. Movement is restricted to remain hidden. Sensory access is mediated by the vantage (gap, ledge, threshold). Tension lies between *needing to see more* and *needing to stay hidden*.
- **Trapped witness** — Protagonist is present in the scene's social space, not concealed, but cannot leave or intervene. Movement is permitted but constrained by captivity, hospitality, ritual, or social role. Sensory access is open, but the character must conceal their growing understanding from those around them.
- **Trespasser-investigator** — Protagonist moves actively through the scene, handling objects, examining evidence, asking direct questions. Sensory access is pursued, not received. Tension comes from what they choose to investigate next and what the search costs.
- **Disoriented witness** — Protagonist is impaired (dreaming, drugged, half-conscious, wounded, in shock) and receives the revelation passively. Sensory access is unreliable, fragmented, distorted. Tension comes from the gap between the character's compromised state and the reality being shown to them.

**Declared stance:** [one of the four]

### Tipping Mechanism

Pick **exactly one**:

- **Scale-recognition** — Fragments suggest something local or human-sized; the tip reveals the actual scope is vast, multiplied, or transformative. The character had the right *kind* of understanding but at the wrong magnitude.
- **Identity-confirmation** — Fragments suggest a person, creature, or actor whose identity is uncertain or veiled; the tip confirms who or what it is, usually through a specific personal trait. The horror is in the naming.
- **Modus-operandi** — Fragments establish that a power, force, or pattern exists; the tip reveals *how* it operates. The character knew of the threat but not its method.
- **Evidentiary-loop** — Fragments form a trail or chain of physical evidence; the tip is a contradiction or closure in the trail (a return, a missing link, an impossible path) that forces re-reading of all prior evidence.
- **Anomaly-contrast** — Fragments establish a baseline (a group, a behavior, a setting); the tip is a single element that violates the baseline in a small but decisive way. The whole interpretation flips on the anomaly.

**Declared mechanism:** [one of the five]
