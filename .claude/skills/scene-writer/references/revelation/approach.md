# Approach

This is the opening phase of the scene. You are writing only the **approach** — the entry into the comprehension space, where the POV stance is established physically, the protagonist's entry belief is framed, and the first peripheral fragments are sown. If the user provided **scene priorities**, keep these in mind as you write.

Before writing, consult your working document's **POV Stance** and **Tipping Mechanism** declarations. The stance dictates physical and sensory grammar (what the character can see, hear, move toward, conceal). The mechanism shapes what kind of comprehension the scene is bending toward, even though the tip is not spent here.

## Texture Budget

Draw on **only 1–2 fragments** from the front of your working document's inventory — the most peripheral and ambiguous ones. Do not spend mid-sequence fragments, and never spend the TIP. The approach should be lean; save the bulk of your evidence for the accretion phase.

## Guiding Questions

Use the following questions to shape your approach. You do not need to answer "yes" to every one — they are priorities to weigh, not a checklist to complete. Let the scene's needs dictate which matter most.

1. **POV stance grounding**: Is the declared POV stance established through physical staging — and the constraints the location imposes on it — within the first few paragraphs?
2. **Entry belief**: Is the protagonist's current (and ultimately incomplete) understanding of the situation visible to the reader?
3. **First fragment seeded**: Has at least one peripheral, ambiguous fragment from the working doc been placed in the prose without yet being interpreted?
4. **Interpretive restraint**: Does the prose withhold the protagonist's interpretive guesses enough that the reader can outpace them?
5. **Tonal foreshadowing**: Does atmosphere hint at the the coming revelation without spoiling its content?
6. **Pace signal**: Does the opening telegraph that this is a slow-burning comprehension scene rather than an action one?

## Wordcount Constraint

The total finished scene should be approximately **[WORDCOUNT]** words. This approach stage owns the establishing-vs-continuation swing for the whole scene; the accretion and epiphany stages do not adjust based on scene type.

- **Establishing scenes:** approach runs **15–20%** of total wordcount.
- **Continuation scenes:** approach runs **10–12%** of total wordcount.

If your approach exceeds the relevant cap (20% establishing, 12% continuation), **rewrite it shorter**. Brevity here is more important than addressing every guiding question. Cut the question, not the budget.
