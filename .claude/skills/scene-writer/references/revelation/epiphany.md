# Epiphany

This is the finale of the scene — the moment comprehension lands, followed by a short aftermath beat that points toward what comes next. If the user provided **scene priorities**, keep these in mind as you write.

Before writing, consult your working document's **POV Stance** and **Tipping Mechanism** declarations. The mechanism is especially load-bearing here: the TIP fragment must land with the logic of the declared mechanism (scale-recognition, identity-confirmation, modus-operandi, evidentiary-loop, or anomaly-contrast). The stance dictates what the protagonist can physically do next — flee, draw a weapon, hold position, recoil — when comprehension breaks.

## Texture Budget

Spend the **TIP** fragment and **all remaining reserves** from your working document's inventory. The pool must be empty after this phase.

## Guiding Questions

Use the following questions to shape your epiphany. You do not need to answer "yes" to every one — they are priorities to weigh, not a checklist to complete. Let the scene's needs dictate which matter most.

1. **TIP delivered**: Is the TIP fragment spent in the prose?
2. **Mechanism execution**: Does the tipping mechanism actually function as declared — a scale tip reveals magnitude, an identity tip names a specific being, a loop tip exposes a contradiction — rather than substituting a generic "and then they understood"?
3. **Comprehension lands concretely**: When understanding arrives, is it rendered in concrete, specific terms (the thing named, the magnitude shown, the trail closed) rather than abstract realization?
4. **Articulation restraint**: Does the prose avoid letting the protagonist explain the revelation in full sentences of internal monologue?
5.  **Outward hook**: Does the closing image or beat point toward what comes next — a threat to escape, an action to take, a question?
6.  **No retrospective explanation**: Does the prose avoid summarizing or re-explaining the prior fragments now that the pattern is clear?
7. **Stance-driven response**: Is the protagonist's first physical reaction shaped by the declared POV stance (flight from hidden vantage, social break from trapped role, drawn weapon as trespasser, recoil in disoriented state)?
8. **Entry belief echo**: Does the gap between the protagonist's entry belief (established in approach) and the new comprehension register either explicitly or by clear contrast?
