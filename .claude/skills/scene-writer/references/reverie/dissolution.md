# Dissolution

This is the finale of the scene — abrupt or quiet, carrying emotional residue forward without resolving it like a dramatic scene would. Waking, silence, fade, or hard cut back to ordinary register. If the user provided **scene priorities**, keep these in mind as you write.

## Texture Budget

Use **all remaining textures** from your working document's inventory. The pool should be empty after this phase.

## Guiding Questions

Use the following questions to shape your dissolution. You do not need to answer "yes" to every one — they are priorities to weigh, not a checklist to complete. Let the scene's needs dictate which matter most.

1. **No moral or thematic lesson**: Does the dissolution refrain from stating an explicit moral, lesson, or thematic conclusion (the reverie's meaning should remain felt, not declared)?
2. **Single closing image**: Does the dissolution land on a single specific closing image, sound, or phrase?
3. **Residue carried forward**: Does the dissolution leave a specific sensory or emotional residue that the reader will carry into the next scene?
4. **Question seeded**: Does the dissolution seed at least one open question (about what was real, what changed, what is coming) without answering it?
5. **No dramatic-arc reversal**: Does the dissolution avoid imitating a dramatic-arc resolution — no ironic reversal, no clean answer, no solved problem?

If your dissolution exceeds 20% of the target wordcount, **rewrite it shorter**. Do not worry about whether the *total* scene wordcount lands exactly on target here — focus on keeping the dissolution within its 10–20% share.
