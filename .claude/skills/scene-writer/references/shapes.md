# Scene Shapes — Selection Guide

Four shapes are supported. Pick the one whose structural logic best matches what the scene outline needs to do. When in doubt, default to `dramatic-arc`.

---

## dramatic-arc

**Pick when.** The scene has an active protagonist, a visible opposing force, and the outline calls for a concrete change by the close: fight resolved, deal struck, secret forced, escape made. Works in physical, verbal, or mixed registers.

**Placement.** Workhorse shape. Climaxes typically land here.

---

## revelation

**Pick when.** The scene's job is comprehension, not action. The outline accretes documents, fragments, overheard talk, or half-glimpsed objects toward a single epiphany. Conflict is between protagonist and what they are about to understand.

**Placement.** Pivots and climaxes. Often the closing scene; sometimes the inciting one.

---

## reverie

**Pick when.** The outline asks for atmosphere, scale, or dream-logic rather than events. The protagonist is observer or witness, not agent. Causality loosens; the scene moves by sensory drift, associative succession, or a procession of discrete tableaux.

**Placement.** Openings, transitions, hinges. Often just before a revelation or just after a dramatic-arc. Rarely a climax.

---

## embedded-tale

**Pick when.** The scene is dominated by a story-within-the-story: a manuscript read aloud, a letter quoted in full, an oral account delivered to a listener, a recording played back. Frame characters listen; the inner tale supplies the events.

**Placement.** Early to seed mystery, or mid-story to deepen it. Often the load-bearing scene enabling a later climax.

---

## Selection heuristics

- Outline foregrounds a **collision or change** → `dramatic-arc`
- Outline foregrounds an **understanding or reframe** → `revelation`
- Outline foregrounds **atmosphere, drift, or witness** → `reverie`
- Outline foregrounds a **told story, document, or account** → `embedded-tale`

If two shapes fit, pick the one matching the climax beat of the scene, not the opening.
