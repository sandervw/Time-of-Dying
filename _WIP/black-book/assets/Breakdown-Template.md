# The Liminal Diver

## 1. MEMORIALS (3)
- Wayfarer warning: body must stay tethered or soul wanders forever
- Tribute to a past diver whose name the water has worn smooth
- Oracular: count your drownings, do not trust the fourth

## 2. ESSAY
- Columbine water parts soul from body cleanly, unlike common drownings
- Three passes are needed because the tohmb folds itself between dives
- Speaker hedges on what a fourth folding would contain, if it exists
- Past divers reported a presence; he trusts none of their accounts
- Admits the true cost to the reader: memory thins with each drowning

## 3. LEMMA — The Columbine Pool
- Origin of columbine water and the god who first pressed the blooms
- Chemistry of clean soul-separation, distinct from murder-drowning
- Known pools still usable along the Road of Graves
- Canonical side effects: silvering of the tongue, thinning of memory

## 4. INTERVIEW (3) — The Remembering Drowned, between the second and third dive
- Ghost asks if he recalls their last conversation; he does not
- Ghost explains it trades memory for a name; he gave it one on dive two
- Ghost warns it will speak his name back to him on the next descent

## 5. VERSE
- Third plunge into the pool; the water takes him gladly
- Body sinks, ghost-self rises through the inverted maze layer
- The Remembering Drowned is waiting, wearing his face this time
- Strips of memory peel from him as he navigates the walls
- Finds the final glyph an instant before the entity speaks his name
- Surfaces gasping, one name poorer than before

## 6. ESSAY
- Body accepts the soul back unevenly; one limb tingles too long
- Gives the tally plainly: four names surrendered to the rite so far
- Hedges on whether the entity is teacher, predator, or both
- Decides he will return, and tells the reader why, and what it costs
