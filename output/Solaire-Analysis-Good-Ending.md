# CHARACTER TEMPLATE

## Purpose

_What is the character's name?_ Solaire of Astora

_Briefly describe the character:_ Cheerful Undead knight seeking his own sun

_What is the function of the character in the story?_ Foil and mirror to the Chosen Undead; embodies hope, cooperation, and the risk of obsession in a world designed to crush all three

_Does the character have an archetype/polarization (head/heart/gut), and do they avoid cliches?_ Heart archetype; avoids the "comic relief sidekick" cliche by carrying genuine pathos beneath his warmth

_How does the character compare/contrast with the hero?_ Shares the hero's Undead pilgrimage but pursues a personal ideal rather than a prophesied duty; where the hero endures silently, Solaire projects joy

_Does the character have some mystery?_ His true origin and connection to Gwyn's firstborn remain ambiguous; the nature of "his own sun" is never literally explained

_Does the character change?_ Yes, from buoyant pilgrim to increasingly desperate seeker, and finally (if saved) to fulfilled sacrifice who becomes the thing he sought

_Could the character undergo a range of change, and is the one they go through the most-fitting by the end?_ Could hollow into madness (default), or link the flame; linking is most fitting because it completes the sun metaphor

_What are the character's values?_ Cooperation, faith, joy in struggle, self-reliance

_Does the character have a symbolic animal and/or movement?_ Movement: arms raised skyward (Praise the Sun gesture); no explicit animal, but moth-like attraction to light

_Does the character have one or two of the following forms of symbols: action, object, name, or symbolic god/animal/machine?_ Object: hand-painted sun emblem (self-made faith); Action: the Praise gesture; Name: "Solaire" from Latin sol (sun)

_Do the symbols change with the character each time they are repeated?_ Yes; the sun emblem shifts from hopeful talisman to ironic marker of delusion (maggot path) or ultimate fulfillment (linking path)

## Believe

_Does the hero have a moment of humanity early on?_ His first meeting: laughing warmly at the sun, inviting a total stranger into jolly cooperation, openly admitting he does not have all the answers

_Is the hero defined by ongoing actions and attitudes, not by backstory?_ Yes; no flashbacks or exposition dumps, only his behavior at each bonfire encounter and his willingness to fight beside the player

_Does the hero have a well-defined public identity?_ The radiant, unshakeable sunlight knight who praises the sun and offers aid freely

_Does that public identity ironically contrast with a hidden interior self?_ Yes; beneath the cheer lies growing desperation and a loneliness so deep he crossed worlds to cure it

_Does the hero have a consistent metaphor family (drawn from his or her job, background, or developmental state)?_ Solar and luminous language: sun, light, warmth, grossly incandescent, rays, brightness

_Does the hero have a default personality trait?_ Relentless optimism

_Does the hero have a default argument tactic?_ Reframing despair as opportunity through cheerful rhetorical questions

_Is the hero's primary motivation for tackling this challenge strong, simple, and revealed early on?_ Yes; "I am in search of my very own sun" is stated at first meeting

## Care

_What will be the hero's self-revelation at the story's end?_ That the sun he sought was never an external object but the act of sacrificing himself to become light for others

_Does the hero start out with a shortsighted or wrongheaded philosophy (or accept a false piece of advice early on)?_ Believes the sun is something findable and possessable, an object to be located rather than a state to be achieved

_Does the hero have a false or shortsighted goal in the first half?_ Finding a literal, personal sun somewhere in Lordran

_Does the hero have an open fear or anxiety about his or her future as well as a hidden, private fear?_ Open: fear of never finding his sun; Hidden: fear that he is fundamentally incomplete without it, that his joy is performance

_What are the hero's psychological/moral weaknesses/needs?_ Psychological: fixation bordering on monomania, inability to accept purposelessness; Moral: risks abandoning companions to chase his obsession

_What is the hero's social problem and specific desire line?_ Social: isolation masked by friendliness, unable to form lasting bonds because he is always moving toward his goal; Desire: to possess a sun that validates his existence

## Invest

_Is the hero's greatest weakness (ironically) the natural flip-side of a great strength that we admire?_ Yes; his passionate idealism is what makes him inspirational, but the same fixation nearly destroys him

_Is the hero curious and resourceful?_ Yes; crosses between worlds, masters both miracles and swordplay, adapts to every environment from the Burg to Lost Izalith

_Does the hero have a plan?_ Follow the path of the sun through Lordran's depths until he finds his own

_Does the hero have general rules for living he or she clings to (either stated or implied)?_ Praise the sun; engage in jolly cooperation; never lose hope; fight beside those who need you

_Is the hero surrounded by people who sorely lack his or her most valuable quality?_ Yes; nearly every NPC in Lordran is cynical, despairing, hollowing, or hostile

_...And is the hero willing to let them know that, subtly or directly?_ Directly; he openly encourages the player and models joyful persistence without preaching

_Is the hero already doing something active when we first meet him or her?_ Yes; standing at the Undead Burg rampart, gazing at the sun, mid-pilgrimage

_Does the hero have (or claim) decision-making authority?_ Yes; acts entirely on self-directed faith, answers to no lord or covenant hierarchy

_Does the hero use pre-established special skills from his or her past to solve problems (rather than doing what anybody would do)?_ Yes; Sunlight Blade miracle, lightning spear, and exceptional swordsmanship mark him as elite among Undead

_Does the hero have an opponent (competing for the same goal, and able to hit the hero's weakest point)?_ The Sunlight Maggot (and by extension, the Chaos of Lost Izalith); it offers a false sun that exploits his desperate need to find one

_Does the hero conflict with the opponent(s) over strong beliefs/morals?_ Yes; the maggot represents surrender to delusion versus his belief in earned, genuine illumination
