# Dreamreel: Scene 7 Working Doc

## Pre-Scene Work

**Scene Type:** Continuation. Same Still-Room, same hour, picking up the instant after Weirmoth raises the cup. No re-orientation to setting.

### Purpose

**Audience:** A held, narcotic interior. The reader drifts inside Othelmedir's self-mythologizing memory while a poison is patiently brewed over a sleeping body. Cold dread and pity, not suspense.

**Story:** Plainly names Fellow Liddick, the lever, the sealed colleagues, Ostrobone the Rook. Establishes Othelmedir's cowardice-not-crime reframe. Plants the unnamed second decoction in his pocket for later payoff.

### Textures

1. Weirmoth's lips moving on names not his own. (Reserved for Dissolution.)

### Characters

1. **Othelmedir (Fellow Liddick):** Calm, slow, hours to spend. Wants the poison finished, the decoction hidden, and his own reframing of the breach to hold under his own gaze.
2. **Weirmoth:** Unconscious witness. Murmuring through the memory shown him. Wants nothing; he is the dreaming instrument.

### Location

The Still-Room, mid-brew. Slate worktop with ten reagents already measured. Iron sleeping-hound weights, oak bench where Weirmoth now lies. Alembic, diagram-walls. Light cold and pale; no fire near the bench.

### Crosscut (Optional)

The scene runs a soft parallel: poison-work on the slate above, memory-tableaux drifting underneath. One body of prose, two registers.

### Image-Sound Counterpoint (Optional)

Yes. Visual: precise, sober brew-work in cold light. Auditory: a sleeping mercenary murmuring the names of dead necromancers, including Othelmedir's own old name. The hand is steady while the room speaks his guilt.

## Draft

### Threshold

The cup's rim slackened between two of the mercenary's fingers and Othelmedir took it from him before it could fall, lifting it gently, like a dead moth from a sill. Othelmedir laid Weirmoth flat along the oaken bench, turned the mercenary face to one side for ease-of-breath, and drew the oxblood cloak from his own shoulders, settling it across the sleeping chest, the only warm colour in the cold paleness of the still-room. He listened, while the mercenary's mouth began to shape, very softly, names that were strange.

On the slate the ten mixed reagents waited in a great alembic, pale green and patient, the eleventh portion still unarrived; he did not touch them, did not lean to taste them, did not so much as straighten the row, for the brew had no further want of him until the breath came back out of the sleeper's mouth. The work that wanted his hand was smaller and stood apart at his elbow, the lesser mortar with its bitter copper-edged grain, which he wetted now upon a fingertip and brought to his tongue, tasting once and once again to be sure of the proportion; the taste was metal and dry rind, bitter, dry, faithful to its recipe. He took up the pestle of yew and ground in slow circles, turning the grain to oil, and beneath the small dry sound of the stone he listened, and beneath the listening he was elsewhere.

In an outer hall lit by green sconces a figure stood that was not a figure but a hornet's husk worn by a man, the carapace lacquered in bands of black and yellow, the eye-slits rimed pale with the breath of the wearer beneath; Ostrobone the Rook, who had come over the wall with his own people that night and brought his own necromancies, the Red Pew's brutal close work and Milkmoot's beside it, while in the inner stairwells the Fellows ran with their robes hitched and their voices wet. Fellow Sourbell had taken him by the white arm; Sourbell, whose voice was always wet, who had said only, *the lever, Liddick, the lever* and would not let go; and Fellow Alder the Lank had been at his other side, the good hand at the small of his back, steering him as a man steers a balking ox toward the lever-room and the lidded cauldron.

The white prosthetic remembered. There was no nerve in it to remember with and yet the limb carried the iron of that pull stored where pull-weight is stored, the slow giving of the counterweight, the dry clack of the catch; and after the catch the first thin pour of the gas, no thicker than a green thread, sliding down the cauldron's flank and along the floor to find the seam at the doors, the doors going then to their seats with the soft punctual sound of well-made doors closing on dreaming colleagues. Not a crime; a cowardice. He had not poisoned them; he had shut the wound the rivals opened. So ran the formula, and so it ran again now beneath the pestle's circling. He ground until the oil was even, tipped the lesser mortar into a horn vial, stoppered it with wax, and slipped the vial into the inner pocket of his coat.
