# Scene Audit — Scene 7

## Story Fit

- **Give the internal monologue moral weight** — The outline specifies Othelmedir treats the act as cowardice, not crime; the draft is tonally neutral and offers no self-condemnation.
  > "Fellow Liddick had stepped back from the lever then. He alone had not slept. He alone, farthest from the rising fume, had left."

- **Mirror the monologue to Weirmoth's dream perspective** — The outline says the monologue is "an exact reflection of what Weirmoth is witnessing"; the draft reads as Othelmedir's private recounting, not a shared vision.
  > "Othelmedir knew the gate... There had been the noise of a man coming through that gate..."

- **Make the murmured names identifiable as cabal members** — The outline requires the irony that Weirmoth is naming Othelmedir's colleagues; the draft obscures this with "names rinsed thin."
  > "the sleeping man had begun a slurred recital, names without their consonants, names rinsed thin"

- **Sharpen the lidd-decoction foreshadow** — The outline explicitly marks the second potion as setup for Scene 9's resolution; it is too easy to overlook here.
  > "he had set a second smaller vessel boiling while the sellsword slept; the oils inside bubbled without sound"

## Internal Quality

- **Fix two copy errors in the return-to-present paragraph** — "nwo" and "a he had" are plain typos that break the prose.
  > "The alembic nwo ticked as it cooled... a he had set a second smaller vessel boiling"

- **Cut the redundant setting re-description** — The slate worktop, cauldron, and oaken bench were already rendered in Scene 6; repeating them here adds nothing.
  > "the cold of the worktop coming up through the meat of his fingers... the cauldron at his elbow... the oaken bench caught his weight"

- **Bridge the memory-to-present transition** — The snap from deep memory back to the room is abrupt; no signal marks the shift, muddying cause-and-effect.
  > "The alembic nwo ticked as it cooled. Othelmedir had already moved."
