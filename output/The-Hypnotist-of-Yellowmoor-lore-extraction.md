# Lore Extraction: The Hypnotist of Yellowmoor

### Creatures

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Wooly Vampire -- full behavioral profile.** Feeds on dreams of sleeping victims. Weakens the waking hours of drained targets. Inhabits homes of great loss. Sustains itself off a small village for hundreds of years. Thralls lose all emotion and will ("flesh without inner will, domesticated swords").
  - Existing doc: one-line stub ("Feeds on imagination").
  - New additions: feeding mechanism (dreams), ecological niche (homes of loss), longevity, thrall nature.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Shadow-self as Wooly Vampire weapon.** The Vampire conjures the victim's own shadow as a physical combatant. The shadow-self is covered in shadow-thorns, mirrors the victim's fighting style, moves faster due to being unweighted. Each successful strike absorbs the victim's color. Seven hits fade the victim to nothingness. Cannot be blocked by conventional shields but can be struck by polished steel.

### Theogony

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Pilgrim Tohmbs as regional god-shrines.** Individual gods have dedicated shrines along the Road. At least four are confirmed in the northernmost Middlemoss heath. Each is architecturally distinct. Gods can grant or withhold a "sign of will" to pilgrims. Named examples:
  - **The Fourth Green** (Mother of Worms): beneath a gutted brothel; entry by riddle (answer: "Ginseng").
  - **The Leper Stone**: a menhir bearing the Thin Man's plaster mask; surrounded by a leper colony.
  - **The Wandering Barrow**: entry shifts between five sites each midnight; smell of old death is constant.
  - **The Chine Pit**: folk-shrine to a famous local butcher attached to a divine tradition without official sanction.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Mother of Worms -- in-world sin-domain named "wanting."** Folk or regional tradition names her domain "wanting" rather than lust or decay. May reflect doctrinal drift from the clinical "Lust" in existing docs.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **Thin Man iconography -- plaster mask with flaccid cheeks.** Shrine representation is a plaster mask described as looking "ill." Distinct from the silver mask in the Prophecy tradition, suggesting two separate iconographic lineages.

### Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Katabasis -- named hypnotic trance-descent.** "Katabasis" is the term for being consumed by a Wooly Vampire's trance. Described as cyclic ("Ouroboros is the trance"). The victim enters recursive dream-worlds and must identify real-world elements bleeding through to escape.
