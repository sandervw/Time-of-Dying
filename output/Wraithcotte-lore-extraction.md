# Lore Extraction: Wraithcotte

## Theogony

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Gallbladder's holy order** -- Gallbladder is served by "distended hermits," a monastic order. The Song-Singing Fungus (bright red mushroom) is sacred to this order. Existing doc names Gallbladder's followers as the Grootslang and Lake of Tomatoes mutants; this adds a monastic follower type.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Red King's confession racks** -- The Red King (Envy, God of Persecution) is associated with torture devices called confession racks. Existing Red King doc is a stub; this adds a concrete instrument tied to his persecution domain.

## Death/Afterlife

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Necromancy-suppressed zones** -- Landmine poison forces undead into dormancy and blocks all necromantic spellwork. Existing doc treats undead as universally active; this introduces a chemical suppression mechanism.

## Creatures

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Giant Slugs** -- Enormous black slugs inhabiting Landmine Forest. Repelled by firebug lantern light. Too large to enter narrow root-cave openings.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Bell Ringer** -- Thick skeleton with a bell for one hand and a cone around its mouth. Tolling 11 times induces sleep. Cries "Who calls at midnight?" on patrol.

## Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Anti-necromancy zones** -- Certain poisons nullify necromancy within a region. `setting/mythology/Magic-Systems.md` documents no environmental counters to necromancy beyond gagging; this is a new hard constraint.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Deadspeech vocabulary** -- "Vigyazz" means "Caution" in Deadspeech (from Hungarian "vigyazz"). Consistent with established Hungarian derivation.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **Cursing through ointments** -- The Reflective Ointment delivers a permanent, post-death curse via topical application. Implies necromantic curses can be administered through substances, not only spoken Deadspeech.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Autonomous skeleton behavior** -- Old-guard skeletons hold dormant guard-states; Fleshless Upright Bones run scheduled patrol circuits. Both operate without active necromantic direction, implying behavioral orders survive the issuing necromancer.

## Magic Items

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Firebug Lantern** -- Firebugs are living creatures capable of stinging and killing organisms. Opening the lantern door releases them permanently. Expands existing doc's "standard mancer field equipment" entry.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Song-Singing Fungus** -- Bright red, long-frilled mushroom sacred to Gallbladder's distended hermits. Completes songs you begin singing, induces deep sleep, then grows parasitic crimson roots over the victim to feed. Existing doc describes a humming mushroom with a small human mouth; this version is predatory and full-sized. May be a different organism or a fuller depiction.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Necromancer's Shovel** -- Enchanted shovel that cracks wood and marble coffins beyond ordinary digging function.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Reflective Ointment** -- Shiny apple-shaped bottle. Contents taste and look like metal. Grants mirror-like skin as a permanent curse that persists after death.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Poison Landmine** -- Device whose poison suppresses necromancy and puts the dead to sleep. Found in the Late Poet skeleton's knapsack. Defines the ecology of the Landmine Forest region.

## Astral Mythology

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Moon** -- Story describes a full moon casting silver light. Existing doc describes "the harridan's moon" as yellow and tied to witch-nights. The silver color and absence of the "harridan's" qualifier suggests either a normal lunar cycle alongside the harridan's moon, or the same moon in a non-witch context.
