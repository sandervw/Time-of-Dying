## A1: Negotiation Under Pressure

"You have named a sum, and withal I shall not dispute the principle of your entitlement, for the river is yours and the passage is yours and time, as all men know, carries its own exequial weight; and yet it may be that a reckoning more equitable than coin might serve us both, inasmuch as I carry upon my person an article of considerable spiritual consequence, whose provenance is such that those with learning in devotional matters would assay it at a value exceeding what you have proposed."

"The priest yonder, I perceive, is observing this transaction with an interest that strikes me as perhaps exceeding mere pastoral vigilance; and it may be prudent, ere that interest sharpens into inquiry, that we conclude our arrangement in the spirit of mutual discretion."

"I do not offer you money, for I have none; howbeit I offer you that which a mancer of my acquaintance would not hesitate to call a relic of the first order, and whose safe conveyance out of this jurisdiction would be, I ween, as much to your benefit as to mine."

"Let us depart, then, and settle the particulars in the hold, where the acoustics are less given to carrying."

## A2: Explaining Away a Disaster

"The stable, as those present are at liberty to confirm, was already in a condition of marked structural and organic deterioration; and it is my measured assessment, as one whose studies incline toward the taphonomic, that the conflagration represented less a disaster than an acceleration of what the rot-black timbers had, in their own slow and pestiferous fashion, been undertaking for some considerable period."

"The horse, as you observe, survives; and it is my further assessment, howbeit I advance it with the qualification that sphygmology is not my principal discipline, that a coat singed to this degree will, with the application of a suitable agaric-based poultice compounded from materials I have upon my person, recover a serviceable condition within the fortnight."

"I am not without ruth for your loss, and yet I must be plain: I was at no point in proximity to whatever source of ignition preceded the incident, and those who affirm otherwise would do well to consider the precision required of testimony in matters that may yet come before a proper assize."

"I have appointments of a funereal and professional nature that will not tarry; and I commend to your attention the horse, who is, in point of fact, the more valuable of your remaining assets."

## A3: Monologue While Alone

"And so it is given to me, at this dawn which breaks grey and indifferent over the lichen-grown ruins below, to learn what the dead, whom I have so long interrogated in their various states of dissolution, already know: that the living betray with a consistency that is, when apprehended without sentiment, almost admirable in its fidelity."

"I had accounted Severance, among all those who have passed through the Chrypt of my confidence, the least susceptible to the ordinary corruptions; and yet it may be that I was the fool of my own estimation, and that the very qualities I took for steadfastness were, from the first, the qualities of a creature whose allegiance followed the warmth of advantage, as the spore follows the drift of the morning air."

"Before this season of my error, I ruled here; and the city that now lies in diagenesis below carried, in its streets and its Tohmbs and its ghost-pale lamps, the impress of my governance; and I did not then believe that a single defection could unmake what three decades of covenants had built."

"And yet here is the battlement, and here is the ruin, and there, somewhere beyond the carrion-sweet morning, is Severance, carrying whatever was sold."

"Natheless I am not finished; and the dead, who are my oldest colleagues in this matter, have not yet rendered their full accounting; and it is my settled intention to pursue this inquiry through whatever number of exhumations and interrogations the case requires, until the ledger is balanced in a manner befitting the gravity of the original pact."

"Ere this next sidereal cycle has completed its arc, they will learn that a mancer whose working tome is still open is not yet a mancer whose work is done."

## B1: Rewrite Cugel's Con Artistry

"You would do well, I submit, to moderate the register of your address; for I am Othelmedir, arrived here at the express solicitation of Soldinck, whose communications with me on this matter were of a particularity that leaves little room for the ambiguity your tone appears to presuppose; and I should be grateful, withal, to be conducted to my quarters without further delay and with a civility more suited to the occasion."

"You may serve me, if you will, a collation of the lighter sort, inasmuch as I breakfasted at an hour sufficiently early that the morning's requirements have not yet been satisfied."

"I anticipate considerable comfort here; and the arrangements, to my initial assay, appear adequate to the purpose."

"Soldinck has charged me with the undertaking of certain tasks during the voyage, whose nature I shall disclose at the appropriate juncture and not before; and I trust that the household will extend the cooperation that the commission of a man of Soldinck's standing must be understood to warrant."

## B2: Rewrite Lord Gro's Philosophical Reflection

"It may be that common opinion, surveying my conduct in this present matter, will account me gone beyond the reach of sound judgement; for I have hazarded my life with a rashness that the prudent observer, acquainted with the sum of my labours and the covenantal patience by which I had, at last and against the persistent obstruction of fortune's fell dispensations, arrived at that which had been so long withheld from me, must regard as an act approaching the lunatic."

"And yet common opinion is, in my estimation, the fool and not I; for he that imagines, after the exhausting labours of his life, to arrive at lasting joy, as well may spend his remaining days beating water in a mortar, and will account himself, at the last, no wiser for the effort."

"Howbeit, because this dawn, with her bewitching grey-lit hours, has held me once in a certain thrall, it does not follow that I am bound to love her still when, glutted with her own triumph, she thickens into garish noon; and it is for this reason, among others whose elaboration would require a longer accounting than the present hour affords, that I turn now, as turn I must, toward that enterprise whose advent, in the sad sunset of an elder pride, yet carries more promise than all the won prizes behind me."

## B3: Rewrite Othelmedir's Remote Instruction

"It is Othelmedir who addresses you, and I would have you fix that name in your attention before I proceed, for what follows is a communication of some precision and I have no reserve of time in which to repeat it."

"Through the deadspeech, I have petitioned certain of the dead in your vicinity, among them, it would seem, sundry who were rendered to that condition by the blade you carry at your hip; and by their testimony I have located you with sufficient accuracy for this remote necromancy to function as you are at present experiencing."

"Know, then, that within the reservoir, both mineral and spiritual in its composition, which is your destination, there lies a scarab of particular consequence to me, being an ancestor of mine who has languished overlong in a condition of arrested dissolution, in a white-stone shelf within the Stope of the Ninth Ozone, enclosed in a red scarab whose outer casing you will recognize by its exceptional weight and its carrion-sweet emanation."

"Shatter that scarab, and all debts your Lady holds with me are, from the moment of that shattering, rendered balanced and discharged."

"Moreover, upon your examination of the shelf, you will observe a stone in close proximity to it, a stone of qualities sufficiently anomalous that I decline to elaborate them in this communication, save to say that it constitutes a further reward of a kind that will repay your attention."

"One closing matter, and I offer it not as counsel but as the notation of a practitioner who has studied the relevant diagenesis: be expeditious in your work there, for the reservoir contains rather more lifetimes than its apparent dimensions would suggest, and tardiness in such a place carries consequences of a kind that are, I assure you, prodigious and quite unpleasant."

## C1: The Flooding of New Londo

"I was present in New Londo in that season, and what I witnessed there was, even to me, the adept of necromancy, a source of horror and of something approaching scholarly awe, inasmuch as the remedicians of that city were confronted with a choice whose terms I have since recorded in my working tome as the most complete and unflinching exercise of covenantal logic I have encountered in my considerable study of such matters."

"The Darkwraiths had proliferated beyond the capacity of any conventional deadspeech intervention to contain; and the great remedicians, having conducted their assessments with a thoroughness I can only respect, arrived at the single conclusion available to them, which was this: that the city entire must be sacrificed, and its denizens drowned, and its streets and its Tohmbs and its lichen-grown towers surrendered to the water, so that the sealing of the water locks might effect a containment no other instrument could provide."

"The locks were closed; and the water rose; and the denizens of New Londo perished in a manner that was, by all accounts, neither swift nor clean; and their ghostly forms have swum in those flooded corridors ever after, which is a fact I report not for sensation but because it is germane to the question of what a properly conducted sacrifice of this magnitude actually produces."

"The remedicians yet stand guard over the locks, and stand guard still, as keepers of that watery seal, which is itself a form of devotion I find, withal, more honest than most; and the threat, I should add, is contained only in the partial sense that the word permits, for the Four Kings and the Darkwraiths have receded, through their covenant with the primordial serpent, into the Abyss, whence they have not, to the best of my current knowledge, returned."

"Whether they will remain there is a question upon which I would decline to wager anything I was not prepared to lose."

## C2: The Birth of the Chaos Flame

"Of the Witch of Izalith, and of that which she attempted and that which she produced, I have assembled the following account from such testimonies as survived, and I set it down here as notation rather than as narrative, for it is, in my estimation, less a story than a specimen of what occurs when a practitioner of the highest order permits compulsion to outpace method."

"The great city of Izalith stood adjacent to the archtree swamp, and stood nearest, of all places then inhabited, to the advancing dark of the Abyss; and the flames, which had been the condition of the world's continuance, were dying; and the Witch, who held mastery over the fire arts and carried within her the weight of a Lord Soul, felt herself compelled, in a manner that her surviving daughters described variously as devotion, as desperation, and as a kind of intellectual appetite she could not withhold, to attempt the recreation of the first flame."

"She had borne seven daughters by the time she undertook this work; and she carried, moreover, her first male child, which is a detail I note not for its sentimental character but because it bears upon what followed; and she proceeded nonetheless, which is itself the annotation I would have any student of the exequial arts read with the greatest attention."

"The result was a disaster of a completeness that has few parallels in the record I have kept: the flame she called forth combined with the Lord Soul in her possession, and became chaotic and ungovernable, consuming her, tainting her, pursuing her through the taphonomic stages of dissolution that ordinarily follow only upon death, while she yet lived and burned."

"She sought refuge in one of the archtrees, which is where she gave birth, in the rot-black hollow of that wood, to a creature disfigured beyond what I will describe here save to note the name by which it has since been known: Ceaseless Discharge, whose flesh bore the Chaos Flame's corruption as clearly as a spore print bears the image of its parent body."

"The Chaos Flame engulfed her, and the archtree, and the child together; and from that engulfment there emerged a new being called the Bed of Chaos, which is a name whose irony, I trust, requires no elaboration from me; and from the Bed of Chaos sprang forth uncontrollable fires that tapped into the chaotic nature latent in the residents of Izalith and in the swamp itself, and all save the strongest among them were tainted and turned, in a conversion that was neither swift nor reversible, into demons, whose lineage persists to this day in forms that any mancer with elementary training in undead taxonomy would recognize upon examination."

"The lesson, I have always found, is not difficult to read; howbeit it remains, in my experience of the profession, persistently unlearned."
