## Source Imprint

**Observed features:**

1. **Sentence-length distribution:** Long periodic sentences (30-60 words) carry exposition and atmosphere; short sentences (4-10 words) punctuate action beats. The middle range is sparse.
2. **Clause stacking:** Right-branching default; relative and participial clauses accumulate after the main verb. Periodic sentences held back with semicolons.
3. **Register:** Archaic-formal without being opaque. Latinate nouns ("turpitudes," "iniquities," "cachinnation") alongside plain monosyllabic action verbs. No contractions.
4. **Sensory channels:** Visual dominant; tactile second; kinaesthetic in combat passages ("dodging the thrusts," "clawing ferociously").
5. **Signature tics:** Paired adjectives before nouns; "as if" constructions (not "like"); temporal markers that reinforce dread ("as if by some hellish legerdemain," "even as").
6. **Repetition pattern:** Names and titles repeated at scene beats for weight; action verbs often doubled across coordinated clauses.

**Do / Don't:**
- Do: Use archaic Latinate nouns for atmosphere; keep action verbs plain and single-syllable.
- Do: Hold back periodic sentences for revelation moments; let short sentences drive combat.
- Don't: Use contractions in the narrative voice.
- Don't: Use "like a" similes; "as if" is the period-appropriate form.
- Don't: Front-load every sentence; let clauses pile at the tail.

## Speech Rules

### Othelmedir

**Positive mode:** Often restates the other character's last line before replying (~50% saturation). In the Generic-Witness lie sequence, the restatement anchors his deception cadence — he restates Weirmoth's suspicion before redirecting it.
**Register:** Archaic
**Sentence-length cap:** Minimum 15 words; no upper cap
**Signature construction:** Names the person he addresses (~50% saturation). In deception lines, naming + restatement may co-occur to project calm authority.
**Saturation cap:** Signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of his lines.
**Banned:** "What does it feel like" questions; fragment-as-style; any direct disclosure that the sealed colleagues are his own or that the dream is his own.

### Weirmoth

**Positive mode:** Speaks in declaratives about the past (fabricated origin tales) (~50% saturation). The mask-slip line before drinking breaks this pattern — it is neither banter nor fabricated past; it is unadorned present-tense.
**Register:** Thieves'-cant / criminal-slang
**Sentence-length cap:** 25 words maximum
**Signature construction:** Most lines reference a body part (~50% saturation). The mask-slip line may omit this; the absence marks the register shift.
**Saturation cap:** Signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of his lines.
**Banned:** "What does it feel like" questions; fragment-as-style.

## Forbidden Patterns

**Rolled bans:**
- No `like a` constructions
- No more than one simile per paragraph
- No anaphora across consecutive sentences
- No sentences that end on a single-word coda
- No comma splices
- No exclusively muted/grey color palette
- No philosophical asides from the narrator

**Scene-specific bans:**
- No fight choreography that describes only outcomes; every blow must have a specific body-part target or named maneuver
- No version of the Generic-Witness lie that hedges or italicizes the false claim — Othelmedir delivers it in the same flat register as the true claims
- No internal monologue from Othelmedir that names the dead colleagues or confesses guilt; the subtext must stay subtext
- No brewing sequence that reads as a recipe list; reagents must be embedded in action and sensation
