# Lore Extraction: The Sewer Under Khoroduhn

Source: `stories/The-CCK/The-Sewer-Under-Khoroduhn.md`
Setting: Time of Dying

---

## Theogony

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Red King mortal cultists** -- The Weige noble family are rumored cultists of the Red King, practicing "black rites and secrets in deep places."
  - Adds: a named powerful family as active mortal servants, with cult infrastructure rooted in the Lake of Tomatoes region.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Red King branded devotees** -- Worshippers are branded with his mark and described as "devoted, wrathful dreamers." Brand confirmed on a sewer-dwelling zealot from the Stitched Soul Men colony.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Gallbladder's title and prophetic function** -- Addressed as "His Grand Corpulency, Lord of Lake"; delivers daily portents via street crier.
  - Adds: the title "Lord of Lake," daily public portents issued in his name, and a formal crier-class.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Colloquial invocation of the seven** -- A character uses "pray to the seven necromancer-gods" as a casual expression of hope, confirming worship is ambient folk practice, not only organized ritual.

---

## Cosmology

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Necromancer-god weather curse** -- Khoroduhn suffers a permanent March rainstorm: a necromancy bound to a forgotten storm, marshalled by one of the seven to commence each spring.
  - Establishes: necromancer-gods can impose long-duration regional weather curses tied to calendar periods. Such curses can outlast the memory of their purpose.

---

## Creatures

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Silk Giant behavioral mechanics** -- Gigan Othok adds: master-bond psychology (madness-on-master's-death), near-invulnerability to cutting weapons ("saddle-like skin"), wall-climbing, and paralysis-by-concentrated-light as a confirmed combat mechanic.
  - Existing doc says: "A giant that likes to weave, can climb walls, and hates light."

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Stitched Soul Men** -- Colony of grey-eyed humanoids with stitched, variegated flesh. Live inside stone lanterns in a carved cave at sewer intersections. Attack on a single gong-note. Branded servants of the Red King.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Mermaid (sewer variant)** -- Bloated siren in the sewer cistern. Fish-eyed with a positional vulnerability: her eyes cannot look downward.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Penny Lizard** -- Enormous sewer salamander that collects objects falling through city grates, accumulating a copper-coin hoard. Encountered randomly throughout the sewer.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ratwall** -- Living wall of ratskin and ratheads, identified as "probably some mancer's work." Eats steel and any limb touching it. Destroyed by fire.

---

## Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Heart removal as anti-resurrection** -- Cutting out the heart of a slain person prevents "common necromancy" from resurrecting them. The qualifier "common" implies not all necromancy is defeated by this method.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Spoken-word portal activation** -- A clay kiln (Hasodeki's Crucible) in the Weige family mancer's tower functions as a portal to a hidden sewer chamber, activated by the spoken word "Bindweed." Establishes: portal enchantments exist, use verbal triggers, and can be disguised as mundane objects.

---

## Magic Items

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Song-Singing Fungus (detection variant)** -- Mushroom with a hidden mouth under its cap. Sings specifically in the presence of uncontrolled dead.
  - Existing doc says: ambient humming connected to incomplete Deadspeech spellwork. Adds: a specific detection trigger condition.

---

## Astral Mythology

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Named constellation: Amber Ox** -- A constellation chart shows all major constellations visible from the Lake of Tomatoes. The Amber Ox is specifically circled. This is the first named constellation in the setting.

## Prophecy

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Gallbladder daily portent-crying** -- A formal public crier delivers daily predictions attributed to Gallbladder in the streets of Khoroduhn. Distinct institution from the Thin Man's dream-prophecy tradition.
