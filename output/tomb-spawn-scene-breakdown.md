# The Tomb-Spawn - Scene Breakdown

*Clark Ashton Smith*

---

## Scene 1: The Storyteller in the Wine-Shop

**Summary:** A storyteller entertains merchants in a wine-shop with the legend of King Ossaru.

- **Setting:** Wine-shop near Faraad's northern gate, evening, end of a caravan day.
- **Narrative device:** Story-within-a-story. Oral delivery gives it mythic distance and unreliability.
- **Ossaru:** Wizard-king, half-immortal, ruler of half of Zothique. Served by Thasaidon, black god of evil.
- **Nioth Korghai:** Alien monster that rode a comet to Earth. Ossaru foresaw its arrival via astrology and met it alone.
- **The vault:** Housed beneath the throne-room. Sacrifices of women and warriors were sent down and never returned. Sensory details: muffled drumbeats, fountain-like regurgitation, mad cockatrice cackling.
- **Death and burial:** Nioth Korghai sickened and died first. Ossaru sealed the vault with a double enchantment, then was entombed beside it.
- **The two enchantments:** Inner circle preserves bodies against decay. Outer circle kills anyone who enters by the door, causing instant putrefaction.
- **The prophecy (Namirrha):** Travelers will find the tomb by accident, enter by a way other than the door, and witness "a strange prodigy" tied to Nioth Korghai's alien biology.

---

## Scene 2: The Merchants React

**Summary:** Two jewel-merchant brothers discuss the legend.

- **Characters:** Milab and Marabac, jewel-merchants from Ustaim.
- **Milab:** Philosophical acceptance. "The sands are full of lost tombs."
- **Marabac:** Mercenary. Wants treasure. Has seen tombs with gold walls and sarcophagi that "poured forth rubies like the gouted blood of vampires."
- **Character contrast:** Milab = wonder; Marabac = greed. Both traits are irrelevant to their fate.
- **Storyteller's closing line:** "They who are destined to find the tomb must tell the rest, if haply they return from the finding." A warning as a narrative shrug.
- **Dramatic irony:** The audience already knows these two will find the tomb.

---

## Scene 3: The Caravan Through Dying Lands

**Summary:** The brothers travel north through increasingly desolate, mummified desert terrain.

- **Route:** Northward toward Tasuun, then east to Ustaim by the orient sea.
- **Landscape as decay:** Hills "like recumbent mummies of giants." Dry watercourses, salt-leprous lake bottoms, dust columns moving "like fugitive phantoms."
- **The sun:** "A monstrous ember in a charred heaven." Destructive, not life-giving.
- **Foreshadowing:** The landscape mirrors the tomb: something grand reduced to husk, death at its center.

---

## Scene 4: The Ghorii Ambush

**Summary:** Ghoulish subhumans swarm the caravan in a narrow ravine; the brothers escape by accident.

- **The Ghorii:** Half-bestial, ghoul-adjacent anthropophagi. Drink blood instead of water. Live in hidden caves.
- **The ambush:** Midday, dead-calm. Ghorii pour from rocky slopes, fling themselves from ledges "like panthers." No war cries, only hoarse coughing.
- **Weapons:** Double rows of pointed teeth, sickle-like talons. Pure animal violence.
- **The carnage:** Camels gnawed alive. Pearls and rubies "weltering in puddled blood," worthless to the Ghorii. Jasper idols strewn in dust. The Ghorii's disregard for wealth inverts Marabac's fixation on it.
- **The brothers' escape:** Riding at the rear because Milab's camel went lame. Their mounts bolt from blood-smell and hyena-scent. Courage rendered moot by animal panic.
- **Pursuit:** A second Ghorii band appears. The brothers turn east into a side ravine.

---

## Scene 5: The Mirage City

**Summary:** Starving and parched, the brothers pursue a distant white city for two days.

- **Supplies:** Only dried apricots and a near-empty water bag. Pack-dromedaries lost with all provisions and jewels.
- **The terrain:** "Powdery terrain like the bituminous dust of mummies."
- **The city:** White walls and domes on a sunken plain. Recedes for two days like a mirage.
- **Milab's camel collapses** on the second morning. They share one mount.
- **Arrival:** An hour before sunset. They follow "far-thrown shadows of broken obelisks" into the streets. The city is real but dead.

---

## Scene 6: The Dead City

**Summary:** The brothers search the ruins for water and find nothing.

- **State:** Once a metropolis. Mansions are rubble. Sand has poured through triumphal arches and filled courtyards.
- **The search:** Systematic, failing. Dry aqueducts, cisterns "dry as furnaces," dust-choked fountains.
- **The palace:** Mighty walls defying erosion. Portals with green brazen hero-statues. Vast roofless hall, cyclopean columns, pavement mounded with debris.
- **The sound:** A low gurgling "as of some hidden stream or fountain" rising from underground. The first sign of something still alive beneath the ruins.

---

## Scene 7: Descent into the Vault

**Summary:** Following the sound of water, the brothers improvise a rope and descend through a cracked dais.

- **The opening:** A fallen wall block has cracked the black-veined marble dais. A jagged aperture leads down.
- **The sound:** Water-like regurgitation, "incessant and regular as the beating of a pulse." Biological rhythm, not mechanical.
- **Improvised descent:** Camel harness knotted into a thong, secured to the fallen block.
- **Below:** Ten to twelve feet down. Solid stone floor. A half-open cyclopean door with faint twilight from crypts beyond.

---

## Scene 8: The Horror Revealed

**Summary:** By burning-cloth light, the brothers behold the fused body of Ossaru and Nioth Korghai, still alive.

- **First impression:** Dim contours "like some enormous clepsydra or fountain surrounded with grotesque carvings."
- **The merged creature:**
  - Main body: urn-like, palish, pitted with small apertures, pedestalled on a tilted stone block.
  - Limbs: arm-like and leg-like projections in "swollen nightmare segments." Two members slope into an open gilded sarcophagus.
  - Head 1 (Nioth Korghai): beaked like a cuttlefish, lined with long oblique slits for eyes.
  - Head 2 (Ossaru): aged, dark, regal. Eyes like balas-rubies. Grizzled beard grown to "the length of jungle moss" on the porous trunk.
- **The prodigy explained:** Nioth Korghai's alien biology fused with Ossaru's preserved body. The inner enchantment kept both incorruptible; the alien organism used that preservation to merge and resurrect as a hybrid.
- **Escalation:** As the cloth burns out, the cuttlefish head's slits expand to "intolerably flaming" round orbits. Drum-like throbbing awakens.
- **Key detail:** The brothers do not recognize what they see. They have forgotten the storyteller's tale. Dramatic irony is complete.

---

## Scene 9: The Chase and the Red Line

**Summary:** The fused abomination lurches after the brothers, who flee into Ossaru's own death-ward and disintegrate.

- **The creature moves:** Reaches with Ossaru's brown, shrivelled hands. Cuttlefish beak cackles; the kingly head speaks "like some enchanter's rune" in an unknown tongue.
- **Movement:** Lumbering. "Trampling of elephantine pads and a stumbling of human feet inadequate to bear up their share of the blasphemous hulk."
- **Detail:** Tentacles withdraw from the sarcophagus trailing "empty, jewel-sewn cloths of a precious purple." Ossaru's wrappings, now hollow.
- **The red line:** Five paces from the doorway, Ossaru's outer circle of enchantment marks the dusty floor.
- **Marabac's death:** Checked in mid-air "as if by some invisible wall." Limbs and body melt beneath the burnoose. White bones gleam, then vanish. Empty rotting rags. Instantaneous decay compressed into seconds.
- **Milab's death:** The creature's hands reach his shoulders. He follows Marabac with "one swiftly dying scream."
- **The creature's end:** Does not pause. Enters its own death-ward. Melts "as of misshapen cloud, a falling as of light ashes." Darkness and silence.
- **Irony:** Ossaru's enchantment kills the thing Ossaru became. The wizard-king's own foresight destroys him, the brothers, and itself.

---

## Scene 10: The Ghorii Find Nothing

**Summary:** The Ghorii arrive at the palace, eat the camel, and find the tomb holds nothing to devour.

- **Sequence:** The Ghorii tracked the brothers across the desert. They kill and eat the camel at the palace entrance, then gather at the vault opening and sniff.
- **The ending:** "Baffled, they went away, their keen nostrils telling them that the scent was lost, that the tomb was empty either of life or death."
- **Final note:** Even scavengers who want only meat find nothing. The enchantment annihilated everything so completely that no scent remains. No treasure, no bodies, no monster, no proof.
- **Structural echo:** The story began with a legend no one could verify. It ends with evidence no one could find.
