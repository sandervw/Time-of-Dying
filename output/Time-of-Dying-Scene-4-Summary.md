# Time of Dying: Scene 4 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Othelmedir crosses the chrypt threshold and descends into pale gas pooling at knee-height. The antechamber is hung in spider-silk; preserved corpses of two cabals wake as the seal breaks. Weirmoth dispatches them in clean spear-work — first the Milkmoot breach-party (one of them masked in hornet-husks, the body of Ostrobone the Rook), then a pair of unarmed Red Pew tower-residents in red surplices reaching toward an unfinished killing. The dead recite silent breach-orders. Othelmedir passes books and a watch-ledger without slowing; Weirmoth notes his ease in the place. Othelmedir pockets a tin cauldron-and-eye pendant from the masked corpse. The corridor ends at a warm, breathing wall of plaited muscle that bars further descent.

## Ending Text

...Othelmedir stood there and did not answer at once, and the silent mouths behind them, in the chamber they had crossed, went on saying what they had been saying.

## Questions Posed

- How does Othelmedir get past the breathing muscle-wall?
- What does Weirmoth suspect about Othelmedir's familiarity with the place?
- What is the Milkmoot-pendant for, and what does Othelmedir intend with it?
- What were the dead saying with their silent mouths?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir:** Tall black man, oxblood cloak, white prosthetic left arm hanging limp. Walks the chrypt without hesitation; never fights. Speaks archaic, long-clauses, names Weirmoth often. Pockets a Milkmoot pendant with quiet purpose; withholds his history.

**Weirmoth:** Sideburns, cloak of patched shrouds; carries spear and the preserved canary Mister Ribbon. Unflashy, competent killer. Speaks thieves'-cant, references body parts, addresses the canary aloud. Beginning to needle Othelmedir about his ease in the place.

**Ostrobone the Rook (referenced, dead):** Leader of the Milkmoot breach-party. Wore a brown-stained leather mask with hornet-husks stitched into the cheekplate. Killed at the breach long ago; preserved by the gas; now dispatched again. Wore the cauldron-and-eye pendant.

## Reflection

**What worked:** The two-cabal layering (Milkmoot vs. Red Pew) sharpens the outline; the cumulative tissue-appraisal sentence carries Othelmedir's clinical interiority; the muscle-wall ending holds.

**What was cut in review:** The "slow as a man waking under heavy bedding" simile (forbidden pattern) and Othelmedir's two-word "Walk on" fragment (violated his 15-word minimum).

**What's missing:** The villagers' overwriting (ticking clock from brief) is unsurfaced; could surface obliquely next scene.
