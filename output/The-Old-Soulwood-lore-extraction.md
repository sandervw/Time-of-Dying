# Lore Extraction: The Old Soulwood

## World Geography

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Soulwood Widow origin myth (expanded)** -- The demon king who owned the field willed it to his human slave concubine on death. The necromancer-gods could not kill her (being human) but drove the Road of Graves through her land. She planted magic juniper berries atop the first epitaphs; these rooted into stone and spread into forest that no felling could stop.
  - Existing doc says: "a garden created by a demon king's widowed mistress." This story adds: she was a human slave who inherited by will; gods were constrained from killing her; origin mechanism is magic juniper berries on epitaphs; spread was unstoppable.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Road of Graves buried under the Soulwood** -- The Road's epitaphs lie beneath loam and roots throughout the Soulwood but appear often enough to confirm a traveler is still on course.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **The Old Soulwood is roughly four days east-west** -- The Road exits the Soulwood's eastern limit after four days and three nights on foot.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Unnamed tall forest at the Soulwood's eastern edge** -- Immediately east of the Soulwood stands a thin forest of arch trees rising "beyond the dreams of the highest clouds."

## Creatures

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Periwinkle Forest Cat -- corrected scale** -- Length 4.3-4.5 m, shoulder height 2.2-2.3 m, reach ~3 m, weight exceeding two tons. Unusually long sinewy limbs; flat face with a comparatively weak jaw. Habitat: the Soulwood and its Underpass.
  - Existing doc says "lynx-sized." This entry is elephant-scale. Existing doc must be revised. Story also adds: Underpass as primary hunting ground, giant tunnel-rats as primary prey, and that cats do not kill-hunt in the forest proper except for play or vengeance.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Lampagogue, The Maker of Wisps** -- A sentient pool in the Glowing Glade. Water is dry brittle grey; refuses to reflect the moon. Speaks in a boneless, sinusless voice. Drives victims to become wisps by forcing combat in one of three chosen forms: Form of the Eyeball (slow 20-foot armored millipede with a socketless eyeball that spews yellow fluid), Form of the Glass Wasp (near-invisible wasp; sting curses flesh to glass), Form of the Padfooted Woodsman (fleet axeman in heavy pelt who cuts through nearly anything).
  - Note: Will-o-Wisps already exist in `settings/mythology/Creatures.md` as glowballs that blind and steal sound. Lampagogue is their apparent source or manufacturer.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Black Fairie and Walnut Fairie** -- Two faerie types found under Soulwood gravestones, flushed out by pouring water on specific epitaphs. Black Fairie hides under gravestones, beached scallops, and manure piles; swallowing one grants three days of prosperous financial dealings. Walnut Fairie begs to be escorted to the nearest frog pond; if escorted, it shrinks to yam-size and rides in a pocket.
