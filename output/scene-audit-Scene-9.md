# Scene Audit — Scene-9

## Story Fit

- **Add pendant-withholding beat** — The outline specifies Othelmedir keeps the pendant secret as a deliberate act; the draft only references it as passive sensation, with no moment of concealment visible to the reader.
  > "The pendant of hammered tin lay cold against his sternum beneath the oxblood cloak; he felt the flat-tear edge of it shift a finger's breadth with the motion and settle."

- **Deepen the "realizes too late" irony** — The outline calls for Othelmedir to realize Weirmoth's barter logic mirrors his own; the draft registers mild surprise but does not land the self-implicating weight.
  > "He had taken Weirmoth for a road-tough. He inclined his head once."

## Internal Quality

- **Fix typos: "At", "gass", "IT", "soldified"** — Three typos in one paragraph break immersion.
  > "At the decoction met the boiling gass... IT had soldified into mundane muck."

- **Fix typo: "broke nspear"** — Word split error.
  > "The tipped end of his broke nspear was in his hand."

- **Fix casing: "he did not"** — Sentence begins lowercase after a full stop.
  > "he did not."

- **Cut prosthetic re-description** — Othelmedir's prosthetic arm is established early in Scene 1 and visible in every scene; invoking it here only as a simile for a wound adds no new information.
  > "his other arm hung limp as Othelmedir's own prosthetic"
