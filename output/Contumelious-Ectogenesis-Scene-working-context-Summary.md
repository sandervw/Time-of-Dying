# Contumelious Ectogenesis: Scene 1 (working-context) - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Othelmedir, fronting as a wandering scholar, arrives at *The Hooded Pillow*, the only boarding house in Sulky Wend. The door has been rehung wrong, the lintel set too low for him; inside, residents sleep upright, half-starved of rest. He observes a strong-necked boy as a butcher would, marking neck and teeth. While the bedmeister works the queue in clipped commands, a barefoot child stands in a cold that does not extend beyond her body and recites numbered lines of a funeral rite. Othelmedir crosses to the counter, recognises the attercop-silk ledger from a former life he has not named, and asks for the highest room. The bedmeister's thumb moves along the bead-tally. Nothing has been resolved; the village's sickness has been confirmed.

## Ending Text

..."You ask, master bedmeister, whether I take bed or bench. Let it be a bed, if any room remains, and let it be the highest room you keep, as I prefer the quiet of an upper floor." The bedmeister's thumb moved along the cord.

## Questions Posed

- Who is Othelmedir, that the wrong-hung door and the silk-bound ledger are familiar?
- Whose death does the rite mark, and why does the child carry it?
- What count was Othelmedir taking at the threshold, and what answer did the room give?
- Why is each name in the ledger struck through?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir:** Tall as a redwood, thin as a reed; iron eyes, broad dark face, cropped curls, salt-and-pepper beard. Oxblood velvet cloak conceals a full white prosthetic left arm hanging dead from a shoulder cord. Speaks in archaic, formal cadence. Fronts as a modest scholar; appraises living villagers as material.

**The Bedmeister:** Operator of *The Hooded Pillow*. Stands at a plank counter beneath an iron bead-tally on a nailed cord. Speaks only in imperatives and questions, six words or fewer, ending each line on a noun. Distracted, transactional, runs the night like an inventory.

**The Sleepwalking Child:** Girl of seven or eight, barefoot, in a coarse grey shift. Eyes open and unfocused. A small cold lives a hand's-breadth from her body. Speaks numbered lines of a funeral rite (*The Little Mass of Failing Lamps*) she cannot possibly know.

**The Strong-Necked Boy:** Around fourteen. Asleep upright on a bench, jaw slack, head against plaster. Strong neck, even white teeth. Untouched pewter cup of small beer at his elbow. Material for Othelmedir's gaze; non-speaking.

## Reflection

**What worked:** The crosscut between bedmeister's transactional commands and the child's numbered rite; the dead arm's chill registering Othelmedir's guilt without naming it; the wrong-hung door as architectural unease.

**What was cut in review:** Tightened bedmeister lines to keep imperative/question form and noun endings.

**What's missing:** The plastered niche; the boarding house's historical role only implied, never confirmed for the reader.
