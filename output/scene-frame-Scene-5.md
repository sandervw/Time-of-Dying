# Scene Frame — Scene 5

## Scene Parameters

**Senses (3):** touch · kinaesthetic · taste

Touch and kinaesthetic suit the library's physical intimacy — Othelmedir handling a book he helped write, the texture of rotted codices, the prickling awareness of the door behind which something stirs.

**Rhythms (2):** run-on · cumulative

Run-on sentences carry Othelmedir's absorbed, circling attention; cumulative rhythm lets details accrete as he moves through the space, building dread by addition rather than punctuation.

**Scene shape:** escalation

Steady pressure from cold open to the sound at the door — each beat adds weight until Othelmedir's quiet instruction to Weirmoth closes the scene on held breath.

**Color palette:** calico, monochromatic-grey

The dead false-sun globe drains the library of warmth; grey dominates — pale mold on spines, pale gas, dim stone gallery — with only the treatise's ink as a darker anchor. As if the room is badly washed in bleach

## Source Imprint

**Sentence-length distribution:** Long, weighted sentences (25-50 words) dominate; short sentences (5-10 words) appear as pivots or closures, never strung together.

**Clause stacking:** Right-branching, heavily parenthetical; subordinate clauses extend a single thought outward rather than resolving it.

**Register and vocabulary:** Latinate and slightly archaic; no contractions; abstract nouns carry equal weight to physical ones (*melancholy, significance, erudite encumbrances*).

**Dominant sensory channels:** Visual with strong proprioceptive undercurrent (posture, limb position, bodily stillness).

**Signature tics:** Accumulated apposition ("not so much … as …"); negation as description ("it was never this … nor anything tangible"); simile deferred to clause-end.

**Do:** let physical observation trail into interiority; use negation to qualify emotion; let sentences double back on themselves mid-clause.

**Don't:** resolve a thought cleanly in under 15 words; use exclamation or rhetorical urgency; name feeling before showing its physical correlate.

## Speech Rules

### Othelmedir

**Positive mode:** Usually restates the other character's last line before replying (~50% cap)
**Register:** Archaic
**Sentence-length cap:** Minimum 15 words; no cap maximum
**Signature construction:** Usually names the person he is addressing (~50% cap)

**Saturation cap (constant):** signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of the character's lines.

**Banned:** "What does it feel like" type questions; fragment-as-style; any direct disclosure of builder-knowledge (he does not yet name his role in the tower's sealing)

---

### Weirmoth

**Positive mode:** Speaks in declaratives about the past (his fabricated origin tales) (~50% cap)
**Register:** Thieves'-cant / criminal-slang
**Sentence-length cap:** 25 words maximum
**Signature construction:** Most lines refer to a body part (~50% cap)

**Saturation cap (constant):** signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of the character's lines.

**Banned:** "What does it feel like" type questions; fragment-as-style

---

## Forbidden Patterns

**Rolled bans:**
- No metaphors built on weather or seasons
- No `X like Y` similes where Y is domestic or food
- No tricolon (rule of three) lists
- No triadic sensory catalogues (smell/sound/texture)
- No rhetorical questions in narration
- No modern slang

**Scene-specific bans (PREDICTIVE):**
- No direct statement that Othelmedir recognises or remembers the library; render familiarity through behaviour only (hands finding a shelf without looking, feet taking the correct stair)
- No explicit naming of the treatise's significance; the title lands and is left alone
- No catalogue of book titles or spine-descriptions that reads as inventory
- No dramatic pause or beat inserted immediately before the sound at the door; let the awakening interrupt mid-action
