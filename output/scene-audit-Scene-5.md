# Scene Audit — Scene 5

## Story Fit
- **Add oblique authorship signal** — Outline requires the subtext that Othelmedir authored the treatise; the draft gives no trace of it, leaving the subtext with nothing to anchor to.
  > "It is a treatise, Weirmoth... *Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents* — would repay more study than most of the volumes which have kept it company."

- **Plant Othelmedir's foreknowledge of the still-room** — Outline specifies his knowledge of that door is subtext; the draft shows no hint, however oblique, that he recognises where it leads.
  > "a door half-hidden behind a shelf — a door which Othelmedir, walking past it, did not look at directly, and yet whose threshold his shoulders had already squared themselves against."

## Internal Quality
- **Cut re-described gas sensation** — Scene 4 already rendered the gas as spoiled meat settling at the back of the throat; Scene 5 repeats the same sensory beat in the same anatomical register.
  > "A sour taste — given off by the ankle-high gas — suffused the library; something at the edge of rancid, which settled at the back of the throat and lingered there as if it had business with the soft palate."

- **Simplify opening sentence** — The triple-stacked subordinate clauses make the cold-open physically hard to parse on first read.
  > "the false-sun globe suspended above the central gallery having long since emptied of whatever luminance it once held, its dead face casting light that was not so much light as the negation of shadow, flat and without warmth or direction, so that Othelmedir's boot found the first flagstone's raised corner before his eyes had adjusted."
