# Solaire of Astora -- Description

_Dark Souls by FromSoftware_

## Full Description

Solaire of Astora is a broad-shouldered knight clad in a suit of iron armor that sits heavy on his frame, chain mail visible beneath the plate at the joints and collar. The armor is plain, unadorned iron -- functional, not ornamental -- with the notable exception of the large sun emblem painted across the chest in faded pigment, hand-done by Solaire himself, the brushstrokes rough and uneven as if applied in the field rather than a workshop. Green cloth pauldrons drape over both shoulders, their fabric sun-bleached and fraying at the edges, fastened with simple leather straps. A single red feather juts from the right side of his bucket-style helm, which encases his head entirely -- a flat-topped iron helmet with a narrow T-shaped visor slit that reveals nothing of the face beneath, only shadow. The helm gives him a blunt, almost comical silhouette, somewhere between a crusader and a tin can.

His gauntlets are iron, articulated at the knuckles, worn smooth at the grip from years of swordwork. In his right hand he carries the Sunlight Straight Sword, a featureless longsword, well-forged and kept in good repair but wholly unremarkable in design -- no etching, no gilt, no ornamentation. His left arm bears the Sunlight Shield, a medium iron kite shield bearing another hand-painted sun symbol, again without any divine power or enchantment. A Sunlight Talisman hangs from his belt, a small casting medium decorated with yet another of his hand-illustrated sun symbols. His armor, shield, talisman, and sword all share this recurring motif: a crude, earnest sun painted by his own hand, powerless but persistent. His equipment exhibits no special traits. His stance is upright, arms slightly spread, weight settled. He floats a small distance above the ground, giving the impression of being taller than those around him. Every piece of his kit speaks to rigorous training and self-reliance rather than birthright or enchantment -- a self-made knight whose prowess comes from discipline alone.
