# CHARACTER TEMPLATE

## Purpose

_What is the character's name?_ Solaire of Astora

_Briefly describe the character:_ Devout sunlight knight questing for impossible radiance

_What is the function of the character in the story?_ Foil and mirror to the Chosen Undead's journey

_Does the character have an archetype/polarization (head/heart/gut), and do they avoid cliches?_ Heart-polarized idealist; avoids cliches through genuine madness beneath the cheer

_How does the character compare/contrast with the hero?_ Both are Undead seeking purpose; Solaire seeks external meaning while the player forges their own

_Does the character have some mystery?_ His true origin, possible divine parentage, and what "his own sun" truly means

_Does the character change?_ Yes, from joyful pilgrim to parasite-maddened hollow shell

_Could the character undergo a range of change, and is the one they go through the most-fitting by the end?_ Could find his sun or be saved; corruption is the most thematically resonant outcome

_What are the character's values?_ Cooperation, faith, perseverance, loyalty

_Does the character have a symbolic animal and/or movement?_ The sun itself; his movement is the upward reach of "Praise the Sun"

_Does the character have one or two of the following forms of symbols: action, object, name, or symbolic god/animal/machine?_ Action: Praise the Sun gesture. Object: Sunlight Straight Sword and sun-painted tabard

_Do the symbols change with the character each time they are repeated?_ Yes, the sun becomes the Sunlight Maggot; worship becomes delusion

## Believe

_Does the hero have a moment of humanity early on? (A funny, or out-of-character, or compassionate, or oddball, or comically vain, or unique-but-universal "I thought I was the only one who did that!" moment?)_ "If only I could be so grossly incandescent!" is absurd, endearing, and immediately iconic

_Is the hero defined by ongoing actions and attitudes, not by backstory?_ Yes, defined by relentless cooperation and sunny demeanor across every encounter

_Does the hero have a well-defined public identity?_ The jolly sunlight knight who aids all fellow travelers

_Does that public identity ironically contrast with a hidden interior self?_ Beneath the cheer lies desperate existential need and creeping doubt

_Does the hero have a consistent metaphor family (drawn from his or her job, background, or developmental state)?_ Solar metaphors throughout: sunlight, radiance, incandescence, warmth, grossly incandescent

_Does the hero have a default personality trait?_ Unwavering optimism

_Does the hero have a default argument tactic?_ Earnest enthusiasm that disarms through sheer sincerity

_Is the hero's primary motivation for tackling this challenge strong, simple, and revealed early on?_ Yes, stated plainly at first meeting: "I am in search of my very own sun"

## Care

_What will be the hero's self-revelation at the story's end?_ The Sunlight Maggot is his "sun," a parasitic counterfeit he embraces as truth

_Does the hero start out with a shortsighted or wrongheaded philosophy (or accept a false piece of advice early on)?_ Believes an external object can grant him the purpose and radiance he craves

_Does the hero have a false or shortsighted goal in the first half?_ Finding a literal, personal sun in a world where the First Flame is dying

_Does the hero have an open fear or anxiety about his or her future as well as a hidden, private fear?_ Open: fears appearing foolish, "a blind fool without reason." Hidden: fears his quest is fundamentally hollow

_What are the hero's psychological/moral weaknesses/needs?_ Psychological: fixation that blinds him to parasitic corruption. Moral: cannot distinguish genuine purpose from desperate self-deception

_What is the hero's [social]{.underline} problem and [specific]{.underline} desire line?_ Social: isolation despite constant companionship. Desire: to possess his own source of meaning separate from borrowed light

## Invest

_Is the hero's greatest weakness (ironically) the natural flip-side of a great strength that we admire?_ His faith and devotion are the exact qualities that make him vulnerable to the Maggot's false light

_Is the hero curious and resourceful?_ Curious enough to cross worlds; resourceful in combat through pure training

_Does the hero have a plan?_ Journey through Lordran, seek the sun, aid those he meets along the way

_Does the hero have general rules for living he or she clings to (either stated or implied)?_ Praise the sun. Engage in jolly cooperation. Never abandon the search.

_Is the hero surrounded by people who sorely lack his or her most valuable quality?_ Yes, Lordran is populated by cynics, schemers, and the hollow; almost no one shares his warmth

_...And is the hero willing to let them know that, subtly or directly?_ Directly and without apology, offering help and covenant fellowship

_Is the hero already doing something active when we first meet him or her?_ Yes, standing at the Undead Burg bridge gazing at the sun

_Does the hero have (or claim) decision-making authority?_ Claims spiritual authority as Warrior of Sunlight; acts independently on his quest

_Does the hero use pre-established special skills from his or her past to solve problems (rather than doing what anybody would do)?_ Lightning miracles and rigorous combat training, no magical shortcuts

_Does the hero have an opponent (competing for the same goal, and able to hit the hero's weakest point)?_ The Sunlight Maggot itself, which offers exactly what he seeks and destroys him through it

_Does the hero conflict with the opponent(s) over strong beliefs/morals?_ The Maggot exploits his belief in sunlight, turning devotion into possession and faith into insanity
