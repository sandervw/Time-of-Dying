# Lore Extraction: The Canopy of the Ents

### Creatures

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- definition and placement** -- Oldest surviving trees in the setting. Found only in the remotest parts of the Old Soulwood: mountain peaks or ravines deep enough that canopies sit below the standard forest line.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- communication** -- Communicate with each other through branch-to-branch contact; language manifests as creaking and groaning. Rarely, a smaller beast or plant may understand their intent.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- locomotion** -- Sway and twine branches with one another but do not uproot. Movements compared to whales, elephants, and ten-thousand-year-old birds: slow undulations.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- internal anatomy: the Four-Pronged Heart** -- Each Ent contains a tetrahedron-shaped heart-chamber at its core. Veiny roots loop through floor and walls; every seventh root undulates with sappy red light. A giant stag's head is suspended at the apex. The heart beats erratically.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- the heart-mite** -- A thief who wounds the heart without applying a clotting salve becomes trapped and transformed: body encased in chitin, one side of the face replaced by a single large eyeball with a rectangular iris. Cannot be slain by conventional means.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- the petrifying eye** -- A corrupted Ent's stag-head can emit an eye-beam that petrifies any target standing in its light for more than a few seconds. The heart also controls chamber roots defensively.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- named afflictions** -- Nine named conditions that may kill or sicken an Ent: Petrification, Red King's Curse, Stone Curse, Slumber, Wilting Disease, Millipedes, Curse of Soft Roots, Pulping, and Shakes. Red King's Curse implies the necromancer-god the Red King can afflict trees as well as men.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ents -- cultural lore source: The Third Song of Splywater** -- A named cultural text documenting that Ents dislike all men who touch their bark. First named song or lore-text cited as a setting source.

### Magic Items

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ent-Eye -- function clarified** -- Previously listed as a rare optical component of unstated function. This story adds: deflects the corrupted Ent stag-eye beam when held aloft. Price: attracts the heart-mite. Origin confirmed: found in The Emerald House (Lady Horsehair's temple).

### World Geography

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Old Soulwood -- Ent zone placement** -- Ents occupy the elevated stratum above the cloud line, accessible by climbing the great arch trees. The Ent-land has a defined boundary transitioning to ordinary forest. Adds: a tundra with distant white mountains (implied: the Meathook Mountains) stretches beyond that border, through which the Road of Graves runs as a faint line of paving stones.

### Astral Mythology

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The fifth star -- behavioral timing marker** -- When the fifth star blinks into place in the dusk sky, Ents become unsafe to stand on. First named stellar event that governs creature behavior rather than ritual or undead restriction.
