# Lore Extraction: The Soul-Sense

## Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Soul-to-Sense Tether** — The motif of desire bridges soul, sense, and body. Repeated death and resurrection wears the motif thin until it breaks. Othelmedir: "The motif of desire, which bridges the gap between soul, sense, and body -- it is worn thin."
  - Expands existing doc: desire motifs govern soul-sense-body integrity in living and resurrected persons, not only undead capability. The connection is subject to physical attrition.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Kustodevite Sect and the Indigo Lodge** — A named mancer sect (Kustodevites) models the soul as a multi-story painted lodging house. The living self occupies the roof, descending through occupants and functions to the coffin-maker in the cellar. Called the "Indigo Lodge" cosmology.
  - First named theoretical school within the mancer tradition.

## Cosmogony

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Pre-Road Era: Demon Kings** — Five thousand years ago (two thousand years before the Time of Dying), demon kings ruled by burning and salting each other's kingdoms. Their slave-race were half-demons. The Road did not yet exist; it was ordinary mortared cobblestone.
  - Existing doc frames the world as beginning 3,000 years ago with "no before." This story reveals the "no before" reflects cultural amnesia, not objective history. The demon-king era predates the Road by two millennia.

## Magic Items

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Far Speaking Necklace** — Thin silver necklace enchanted with a "spell of Far Speaking." Activated by touch. Allows contact with a mancer across long distances. First catalogued distance-communication magic item.

## Hero Cycles

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Dueling Ceremony: Seven Specialist Roles** — Noble-clan duels use a formalized ceremony: Four Burdened Ones (bear away the body), Scholar of the Arid Art (draws water from the body, returns it to the clan), Wringers Twain (soak up and wring the foe's blood into a silver-gilt iron ewer).
  - Victor protocol: sun-colored handkerchief drawn along the blade, tucked into a black pouch, sword sheathed over exactly three seconds, then turn to face the high table.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Post-Duel Oratory Formula** — Winning lord speaks a two-part declaration: lionizing the family through the servant's sword, then celebrating the dead opponent's courage.
