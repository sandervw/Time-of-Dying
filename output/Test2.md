"So: the word has come, and the messenger is dust beneath the gate, and the matter is proven that I had long surmised and longer refused to entertain, that he whom I called first among my seconds has this sennight past contracted his allegiance to the enemy, and that the coin of my ruin, struck from such a die, is already in circulation among the lesser courts.

Below me the city lies in its scarred and exequial hush, rooftops blackened where the engines took them, the river choked with the leavings of an evacuation that was, in the end, no evacuation at all, and the dawn lies upon it like a cerement drawn back for a last, unprofitable viewing.

How common a turn, this, and how often recorded in the older chronicles: the confidant whose hand at the hem of the robe was mistaken for a hand upon the shield; I ought to have read it in his pauses, in the over-precision of his accounts, in the small tribute he declined at Lammas and accepted, too readily, at Michaelmas.

Yet I find in myself, to my own mild surprise, no fury commensurate with the occasion, only a cold and professional curiosity as to the mechanism of the thing, as to which lever was first touched, and by whom, and what promise was held out that weighed, in the balance of his night-thoughts, heavier than the twenty years we kept together.

I shall not pursue him. Pursuit is the tactic of the wounded, and I am not wounded; I am merely informed.

I shall, instead, withdraw by the eastern gate before the sun has fully cleared the stones, and I shall carry with me such of the working journals as are not yet copied, and the small scarab that was my grandmother's, and the ring which is not, whatever the heralds say, a bauble.

Let him have the city. The city was already lost; he only now discovers the date of its losing.

And as for myself: I have, howbeit belatedly, one further study to pursue, and the Road, I am told, is long."

### C2: The Birth of the Chaos Flame

**Instruction:** Dramatic monologue. Witness/scholar describes events at Izalith. Convert exposition into speech; preserve sequence.

---

"Hearken, then, to the matter of Izalith; I have rehearsed it seldom, and I shall rehearse it, I believe, but this once more.

The Witch, whom the older texts name simply so, and whom the later chroniclers have bedight with every epithet that terror and hindsight could furnish, was not, in her own estimation, either rash or ambitious; she was, rather, a sovereign who had read, upon the face of the fires, an augury which she took, as any prudent mistress of her art would take it, for a summons.

The flames were dying. I use the word advisedly. And her domain, the great city of Izalith, which sat upon the lip of the archtree swamp and whose farthest gardens were already pale with the Abyss that crept against her southern wall, lay in the first hour of that long evening which the dark, uninterrupted, meant to make its whole estate.

She had mastery over the fire-arts, as none before or since; and she bore, moreover, a Lord Soul, which is a thing I shall not here attempt to anatomize, save to say that it weighed upon her as a crown weighs upon a fevered brow.

Seven daughters she had borne, and the eighth, who should have been her first son, was then within her; and it was at this juncture, with the dark at her wall and the child within her and the flame failing, that she resolved upon the working which the chronicles are too prudent to name, and which I, being past the age at which prudence repays its keeping, will name: the recreation of the First Flame.

The working failed. I say it plainly, for the fastidious circumlocutions of the ordinary histories serve here only to flatter the catastrophe. The flame she kindled took, upon contact with the Lord Soul, such an aspect as neither she nor any of her order had foreseen: chaotic, self-propagating, and immedicable; and it fell upon her, in the first moment of its kindling, as a tide falls upon a shore that had presumed to name itself.

She fled, tainted and consumed, into the hollow of an archtree; and there, in a travail which the mind refuses to entertain for more than the moment required to record it, she gave birth to the first of her sons, Ceaseless Discharge, disfigured past the compass of mercy, and already, at the hour of his birthing, the unwilling vessel of that same chaos which had undone his mother.

And the Chaos Flame, having consumed her, consumed the archtree that sheltered her, and consumed the child also, not in annihilation but in fusion; and the three, by a kind of grammar which our order has since learned to parse but never to forgive, became a fourth, which the remaining scholars of Izalith, in what one must call either piety or despair, named the Bed of Chaos.

From that Bed the flames sprang forth uncontrollably, and, finding in the residents of Izalith, and in the swamp itself, a chaotic nature not wholly unlike their own, they took hold; and all, save such few as were strong enough in their own proper fire to resist the taking, were tainted, and by that taint were turned, sennight upon sennight, into demons.

This is the account as I had it, and as, withal, I have verified it, insofar as verification is possible in a matter whose chief witnesses are either ash, or demons, or myself."
