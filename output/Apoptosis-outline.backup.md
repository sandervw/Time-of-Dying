# Apoptosis — Outline

*First entry in a planned connected series. Target: ~4-5k word story.*

---

## Premise

Two neutral-aligned allies — **Othelmedir** the necromancer and **Rayche**, a half-assassin / half-barbarian axe-man — descend into a sealed Oldvault for plunder. They crack a seal they should not have cracked. Something far worse than expected is rising up the stairs. They retreat to the shrine-room at the top. The shrine's hereditary caretakers are still there. They know what the duo did. They demand the duo fix it.

The bulk of the story is **single-room**: one chamber, one stairway descending into black, escalating waves of horror climbing toward the top.

## Setting

- **Time-of-Dying** continent. A frontier holding off the Road of Graves; specific holding-type TBD.
- The shrine-room sits atop the stairwell descending into the Oldvault — an ancient sealed necromancer-laboratory whose contents have been forgotten for centuries.
- The seal was the holding's reason for existing. The shrine-dwellers are its hereditary keepers.

## Protagonists

### Othelmedir the Mancer
- Tall, reed-thin, dark-skinned, oxblood velvet cloak, salt-and-pepper beard.
- Left arm: living-tissue prosthetic of his own make; brutal super-biological strength held in reserve. Most people see "maimed cripple."
- Spellcraft: small effects only — woven-light orbs, deadspeech whispers, minor enchantments. Real power is **lore, components, and craft**.
- Travels with his golem **Grot** (chained crate), singing mushroom, firebug lantern, banshee-wax, bone dust.
- Cerebral, pragmatic, oath-stickler, reveals terms only from advantage.
- In this story: descended for scholarly gain and plunder. Weighing what he can extract from the breach, the keepers, and Rayche before dawn.

### Rayche
- Half-assassin, half-barbarian. Roman-styled armor, crested helm, heavy war axe. Pronounced "RAYch."
- Driven by hunger for **status and rule over men**.
- In this story: came as muscle and for plunder. Now sees the breach as a **stage**: the keepers are witnesses, and a man who holds the gate against the dark earns a seat to rule.

## Setting Cast

- **The Two Keepers** — a small remote sect of the **Thin Man** (Sloth; god of Sickness and Entropy; locked in eternal sleep in his Sickened Cradle, shares dreams with followers). Their theology: the buried thing must sleep; we watch, we do not wake it. The **Thin Man** doctrine forbids them from descending. They have dreamt this breach for years.
  - **Elder Keeper** — old, gaunt, silver-masked echo of the Thin Man iconography. Speaks for the cult. Slow, watchful, non-acting; will not lift a weapon. Has been waiting for the duo without knowing their faces.
  - **Young Apprentice** — not yet ossified into stillness; can move, can fight a little. Counter-voice to the elder. The story's human heart-rate.
- Stage population: 4 people total — Othelmedir, Rayche, elder keeper, apprentice. Plus **Grot** available offstage as a late-act asset.

## Plot Frame

- **Defense scenario**, horror tonality, single-room episode.
- **Time pressure**: one night under the harridan's moon (witches' night, yellow moon).
- **Othelmedir's stakes**: first claim on whatever rises from the breach — lost Deadspeech artifacts, rare components, the maker's notes. Plus oaths extractable from the keepers as the price of protection.
- **Rayche's stakes**: delivering the killing blow on the Master in front of witnesses earns him the authority he wants. The keepers are the audience that makes men kneel.
- **Horror engine**: 3-wave escalation up the stairwell.

### The Maker's Obsession — Fragmentation

The ancient necromancer who built this Oldvault was obsessed with **creations that cannot be destroyed because they divide**: creatures that regrow, split, expel copies, detach parts that act independently. The Master is his **magnum opus of fragmentation** — modular, partable, with a severable core. As a flesh-shaper, Othelmedir recognizes the school and reads what is missing from the body that falls.

### Three-Wave Structure

1. **Wave 1 — The Discards.** Failed half-flesh attempts, plentiful and weak. Volume horror; axe-work establishes the duo's competence and the blood-economy of the chamber.
2. **Wave 2 — The Catalogue.** Three named successes, escalating, each forcing different improvisation:
    - **Halflung.** A humanoid flesh-thing that expels a half-formed copy of itself from each wound. Cutting produces more enemies. Forces the duo to burn instead of cut — Othelmedir's banshee-wax candles get spent here.
    - **Kept Smile.** A figure cloaked in **overlapping flayed faces**, each a layer it has consumed; mimics voices it has eaten. Among its faces: keepers from generations past. The apprentice **recognizes a face** and is lost here — killed mid-recognition or undone enough that the duo must do something brutal in front of the elder. Drives a wedge between the keepers and the duo.
    - **Wedlock.** A body that **writes the maker's name in blood across the chamber floor** as it climbs. Each completed letter detaches as a small witness-creature, alive until the next letter is written. To kill the Wedlock requires destroying the writing-arm before the signature finishes — while fighting the letters already crawling. Othelmedir recognizes the hand and learns whose vault this is: his first private clue toward the Master's true nature. Costs him a bone-dust working to seize the arm.
3. **Wave 3 — The Master.** A single great horror climbs last. Cannot be won by force alone. Victory requires **Headstone the Mouth** plus Othelmedir's hidden cards (prosthetic, Grot) plus Rayche holding the threshold long enough to deliver a credited killing blow.

By the end of Wave 2 the duo has lost the apprentice, most of Othelmedir's reserves, and the keepers' last optimism. They face the Master bloodied, depleted, and alone with the elder.

### Headstone the Mouth — How the Vault is Re-Sealed

Setting cosmology: an epitaph on the Road of Graves shields what lies beneath it from necromancy. When a person dies, their gravestone joins the cobbles. The seal is not a rite — it is an **interment**. The Road eats the breach.

The elder has carved his own road-stone for years (since the dreams began), epitaph already cut. He descends to the topmost step, lies on his back across the stairwell threshold, sets his hereditary **silver mask** over his own face, and the duo **lower the headstone over him**. He is crushed into the stair; the stairwell mouth becomes a new cobble of the Road. What is below cannot pass under an epitaph.

- No chant, no glow. Death and stone.
- The duo must lay the slab with their own hands while the Master arrives.
- At dawn the silver mask glints up from beneath the new cobble.
- The cobble where Rayche delivered the killing blow becomes a **named stone** on the Road — cosmological credibility for his future lordship.

### The Master — Frankenstein's Monster

The Master is the ancient necromancer's **magnum opus**: unfinished, unkillable, sealed centuries ago because the maker could neither complete nor destroy it. Semi-intelligent. Its want is **freedom**. It has been in the dark a long time. The duo and the keepers are not its prey — just an obstacle in the choke.

The **"destroyed-but-not" trick** is the story's final beat: the Master takes what reads as a killing blow — credited to Rayche in front of the elder — and collapses. The headstone is lowered, the breach is sealed. Most leave the night believing the Master is dead. **Othelmedir alone knows better**.

### Othelmedir's Tell

When the Master falls and the headstone is lowered, Othelmedir kneels beside the body "to confirm the kill" and reads two truths only a flesh-shaper of his caliber could find:

- A **hollow cavity** in the chest where, by the maker's school, the core should sit. The body is a vessel without its primary organ.
- A **recent extrusion seam** along the underside — fresh tissue, not yet sealed — where something extruded itself from the body in the last moments before the killing blow.

The body is a **shed husk** — apoptosis, programmed and engineered. The Master's core slipped its own skin during the chaos and is gone. Othelmedir reads it in a heartbeat. He stands, wipes his hand on his cloak, and says **"It is done."** True and false at once. He pockets a sliver of the seam and walks out at dawn carrying the elder keeper's debt and the Master's secret.

## Series-Spine

The Master is loose on the Road of Graves, free, and beyond the duo's reach. Othelmedir alone knows it lives. Future stories track its consequences across the continent. Rayche, having claimed the killing blow and a holding of his own, becomes a rising power — and Othelmedir's continuing accomplice.

## Structure & Voice

- **POV:** Third-person limited, anchored on Othelmedir. His interiority is required for the closing trick to land. Rayche reads from outside.
- **Opening:** ~200-word atmospheric prologue from the Elder Keeper's POV — vigil, the shared dream, the wrongness rising up the stair for days — then cut: the chamber doors burst inward and the duo are here, bloodied, dragging plunder, slamming the inner door.
- **Scene budget (rough):**
  - Prologue (Elder POV): ~200w
  - Chamber arrival, accusation, demand, seal scene: ~500-700w
  - **Wave 1 — The Discards** (mass action): ~700-1000w
  - **Wave 2 — The Catalogue** (named horrors, casualties): ~1000-1300w
  - **Wave 3 — The Master + the interment**: ~1000-1500w
  - Closing beat (killing blow credited, headstone laid, Othelmedir's private knowledge): ~300-500w
