# Scene Scene 9 Brief

## Premise

Near Sulky Wend, the sealed Tower of the Attercop leaks a necromantic gas that replaces villagers' dreams with the death-visions of the dead inside ('The Dreamreel'). Othelmedir caused this: years ago, under the name 'Fellow Liddick', he pulled the lever that released the gas on his own mancer cabal (The Red Pew) to buy time against the invading cabal (Milkmoot), then he walked out alone. He has hired a sellsword, Weirmoth, and fought through both reawakened cabals to reach the cauldron.

## Scene Structure

- **POV:** Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir)
- **Tense:** Past
- **Target wordcount:** 600

## Scene Outline

The Cauldron Chamber. Othelmedir and Weirmoth approach. Before Othelmedir can speak, Weirmoth tells him that he will not interfere, allowing Othelmedir to perform his task. Othelmedir empties a third bottle (a potion he brewed in the laboratory: 'The Lidd Decoction') into the cauldron. The cauldron hisses, but he intones deadspeech over it, and it eventually turns to a cauldron of muck. Weirmoth has spear ready when Othelmedir turns; he ponders killing the mancer. Othelmedir argues that his cabal did no evil deed; that the plight of the townfolk was a result of the rivals' disruption; that he kept the secret to himself to avoid persecution caused by ignorance of these circumstances; and finally, that he himself is guilty only of that former cowardice, which he has now rectified. Weirmoth assents, but forces Othelmedir to turn over the alchemy reagents he pocketed back in the still-room (a set of cinerous crystals among them). Othelmedir agrees to this fair bargain, and hands over the various ingredients - but he keeps secret the stolen cult pendant he took earlier from a dead-dreaming milkmoot invader.
- (Othelmedir) Closing speech in formal, archaic cadence; not pleading, stating terms
- (Othelmedir) Realizes too late that Weirmoth's barter logic mirrors his own; he had taken the merc for a simple road-tough

## Carry-Forward State

Othelmedir and Weirmoth have just left the upper dining hall / ritual-circle chamber, having killed Alder the Lank and Sourbell (whose voice rose from beneath the boards). Weirmoth's spear was split lengthwise; he finished the fight with his short knife. His forearm was opened to the bone in the earlier fight. He has just openly heard Othelmedir named 'Fellow Liddick' twice by the dead. Othelmedir denied it. The way down is through a half-open door behind the overturned table, past Alder's corpse. The cauldron chamber is just beyond.

## Upstream Revelations

- Othelmedir's true former name is 'Fellow Liddick'. Weirmoth now knows it.
- Liddick/Othelmedir pulled the lever that released the gas; his own cabal (The Red Pew) ordered it; he walked out alone, instead of accepting his fate - and he never returned to save his comrades (until they were already dead and dreaming).
- The stolen pendant (hammered tin, lidded cauldron with a single open eye, flat-tear shape) was taken from a Milkmoot invader in Scene 4. Weirmoth has not seen Othelmedir take it.
- Othelmedir stole multiple alchemy reagents from the still-room in Scene 6; Weirmoth saw this and grew suspicious.

## Closing Beat

End on the asymmetry: Othelmedir surrenders the named reagents in good faith, registers that Weirmoth bargains like a mancer, and the unnamed pendant rests against his ribs.
