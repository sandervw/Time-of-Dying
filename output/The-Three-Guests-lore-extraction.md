# Lore Extraction: The Three Guests

## Creatures

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Periwinkle Forest Cats** -- Lynx-sized predators (bear-wide, salamander-long) patrolling The Old Soulwood. Not always hostile; sometimes befriend children and women.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Bouldsatr origin myth** -- Bouldsatr giants settled The Old Soulwood, were hunted to extinction, then possessed native wild cats to become the forest's eternal guardians. First substantial lore for this race; populates the TODO entry in `Mythology/Creatures.md`.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Doppelganger Skeletons** -- Immortal undead mancers. When the host body ages, the skeleton rips free, tears bones from a new host by the spine, and inhabits the vacant flesh. Several work in The House of Maze.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Mummies** -- Undead type with hypnotic gaze capable of involuntary enthrallment. Retain active necromantic ability. Beauty persists through death as a feature of the type.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ghouls** -- Grey-skinned dead with long tongues and streaming spittle. Swarm in packs. Repelled by Lemon Cider.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Scorpion Stoats** -- Long-bodied, chitin-plated creatures with weak heads and tails. Guard tree hollows in The Old Soulwood.

---

## World Geography

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Old Soulwood** -- Named forest on the Road of Graves. Contains Periwinkle Forest Cats, soul-trapping trees, Blind Eye ponds, and Scorpion Stoats.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Middlemoss: The Athidal** -- A sulfurous gorge physically dividing Middlemoss, crossable on foot. The House of Maze sits on one side.
  - Existing doc: Middlemoss is the geographic center of the Road, between two white peaks.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Old Soulwood: Blind Eyes** -- Eggshell-colored ponds, waist-deep, with one hole large enough for a person. The hole leads to an ocean in another place. Faces may appear on the surface.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Soul-trapping trees** -- Trees in The Old Soulwood that trap souls and leak them as sap-wisps to lure travelers. Contact shrinks the victim until small enough for the tree to digest.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLICIT]` **The Old Soulwood: origin myth** -- The forest was created as a garden by the widowed mistress of a demon king. The Bouldsatr later settled it. Implies the Demon King era predates the Time of Dying.

## Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Elegy of the Equinox** -- Named necromantic song used by a mummy. Allows the caster to see in the cold when stars shine over her. Implies attunement between undead perception and stellar or seasonal conditions.

---

## Magic Items

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Speech Cheese** -- Consumable. Temporarily grants ability to understand and speak in the voice of wisps or other tiny nonhuman intelligences.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Bloodstone** -- Detection curio. Turns pale and shivers when a nearby body is occupied by multiple people (i.e., a Doppelganger Skeleton host).

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Lemon Cider** -- Protective draught. Grants immunity to ghoul attack when taken secretly beforehand.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Coldsoil** -- A pot of special soil keeping undead worms in an alive-dead preserved state. Looted from Celadust Castle.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Inside-seeing Statue of a woman** -- Enchanted statue. Looks through a person's skin to reveal internal structure. Detects Doppelganger Skeletons.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Feral Pickled Fairie** -- Dead fairie in lime-colored brine. Retains residual magic despite death. First mention of fairies as a creature type in any story.
