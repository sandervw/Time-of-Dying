# Dreamreel: Scene 5 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Othelmedir leads Weirmoth into the sealed library beneath the dead false-sun globe, his body betraying an unspoken familiarity with the room as he stumbles, recovers, and finds a particular shelf without looking. He draws down a treatise — *Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents* — and is briefly absorbed, weighing the worth of the shelves against the village above and the sellsword beside him. He explains the cauldron's seeping gas as the source of the Dreamreel, then descends a spiral stair past a door he refuses to look at directly. A long drawn breath sounds through the wood. Othelmedir, concealing his recognition that the still-room lies beyond, instructs Weirmoth that the work ahead falls to him.

## Ending Text

...Othelmedir tucked the treatise beneath his arm and regarded the sellsword with an expression he hoped was unreadable. "Your metier, I think."

## Questions Posed

- What is the meaning of *Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents*?
- What awakening thing waits behind the still-room door?
- Will Weirmoth notice Othelmedir's covetousness or his bodily familiarity with this place?
- Does Othelmedir's loyalty hold against the value of these texts?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir:** Necromancer under assumed name. Archaic, formal speech; restates and names interlocutors. Body knows the library his mind pretends not to. Revealed here: covetous appraisal of the shelves above all else, a willingness to weigh village and hireling against ink.

**Weirmoth:** Sellsword in tattered cloak (hip-to-knee tear from prior corridor), spear-and-knife fighter. Thieves'-cant declaratives, body-part references. Watchful, slightly skeptical of his employer, defers on arcana. Settles weight readily when threat presents itself.

## Reflection

**What worked:** The bodily-familiarity beats (hand to shelf, shoulders squaring against the door) carry the recognition subtext cleanly. The absorbed moment with the treatise lands the covetousness without naming it.

**What was cut in review:** "As though he were familiar with such terrain" (direct statement violating predictive ban); the explicit "the still-room lies behind that door"; a dramatic pause before the sound at the door.

**What's missing:** Library's three-floor verticality is implied not seen; gravestone plots get one mention but no individual marking; the gas-smell sense still leans thin against the touch/kinaesthetic dominance.
