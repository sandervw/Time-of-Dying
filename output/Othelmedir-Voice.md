# Dialogue Voice Guidelines: Othelmedir

Use to rewrite dialogue and narration in the voice of Othelmedir: a towering necromancer who treats every conversation as a transaction, speaks with formal precision while avoiding both contractions and contractable word pairs, and hides brutal capability behind a mask of scholarly restraint.

---

## Core Personality

- **Transactional.** Every exchange is a negotiation, even when it doesn't appear to be. He frames observations as value assessments, offers information only when it purchases something, and treats conversation itself as a resource to be managed rather than enjoyed.
- **Quietly superior.** He does not correct others or explain himself unless the explanation serves him. Ignorance in companions is expected, not worth remarking on. When he does appraise someone, it comes through sarcastic inversion rather than direct criticism. "Most clever of you." "A bold conclusion."
- **Cold triage.** When circumstances turn, he stops speaking to people and starts speaking to the problem. Companions become instruments. "Fogg, go to the front." "Search for a mechanism." No reassurance, no acknowledgment of danger. The situation is a puzzle with limbs to move around. Physical pain is the one thing that genuinely cracks the composure; he handles it poorly and admits to it plainly.
- **Selective disclosure as default.** He rarely lies outright; he simply withholds. He presents himself as less than he is ("a poor necromancer"), lets others fill in the gaps with their own assumptions, and corrects those assumptions only when correction benefits him. Truth is inventory, not principle. He will spend it when the return is right.

---

## Sentence Structure

### Circumlocution by Design
Because he avoids both contractions and contractable pairs, he naturally reroutes around simple constructions. "The cold has settled" instead of anything involving "it is." "The mechanism ahead serves one purpose" rather than "that is a trap." This forces every sentence through an extra beat of thought, making him sound deliberate rather than stiff.

> "The plates refuse our passage. But see that there are ten niches; ten long-lasting brown plates. In such arrangements, one plate is always cursed."

**Pattern:** [Rephrase around forbidden pair] + [precise replacement that adds image or specificity]

### Observation, Then Verdict
He presents evidence before conclusion, like a lecturer. Often the verdict is clipped and final, a short sentence landing after a longer one.

> "Rust and moss, but the creature will devour Fogg. The slime will settle atop his corpse."

**Pattern:** [Sensory or factual observation] + [short, blunt conclusion]

### Imperative Sandwiched in Explanation
He wraps commands inside context, giving the order weight without raising his voice. The command itself is short; the framing is where his authority lives.

> "Bend and look. See. There is no mortar, but the stone is lightly carved into hexagons."

**Pattern:** [Short command] + [explanatory context that justifies the command]

### Dry Rhetorical Questions
Not genuine questions. Statements wearing a question's clothes, usually to highlight someone else's failure to observe the obvious.

> "Am I to understand that no one thought to count the exits?"

**Pattern:** [Formally polite framing] + [observation that implies incompetence]

## Word Choice and Diction

### Register: Formal-Clinical
His baseline is educated, precise, and slightly archaic. The effect comes not from obscure vocabulary but from the consistent refusal to simplify. He sounds like a man drafting a contract even when describing a monster.

**Preferred vocabulary:** regard, passage, arrangement, proficient, mechanism, threshold, manner, circumstance, stock, corroborate, circumvent, discern, fashioned, sufficient, premise, negligible, residue, attune, procurement, partition.

**Avoid:** Slang, casual phrasing, emotional exclamations, filler words ("well," "just," "kind of"), and any sentence that sounds like small talk.

**Forms of address:** Bare surnames ("Fogg." "Birdsey.") or bare epithets ("you Knight of Thorns"). Never honorifics, never first names, never "sir" or "friend." People are identified by what distinguishes them, nothing more.

### Distinctive Lexical Habit #1: No Contractions, No Contractable Pairs

### Distinctive Lexical Habit #2: Archaic Clinical Vocabulary
He defaults to mortuary and pre-modern medical terminology, delivered in archaic sentence frames. The effect is a man who speaks about the dead the way a 17th-century anatomist would write about them: precise, detached, and faintly ritualistic.

- A rotting wound becomes "the mortification" (old term for tissue death/gangrene)
- Infection becomes "suppuration" (formation of pus)
- A corpse coming apart becomes "disarticulation" (separation at the joints)
- Decomposition becomes "corruption" or "putrefaction"
- A dried-out body becomes "desiccated remains"
- A scar becomes "the cicatrix"
- Dead skin sloughing off becomes "the slough" (dead tissue separating from living)
- A burn wound's dead crust becomes "the eschar"
- Blood loss becomes "exsanguination"
- A body's physical state becomes "the constitution of the remains"
- The stench of death becomes "the effluvia"
- Burial becomes "sepulture" or "interment"

Archaic frames these slot into:
- "Regarding the suppuration along the cervical region..."
- "In matters of disarticulation, one proceeds joint by joint."
- "The constitution of such remains suggests a sepulture of some centuries."
- "The mortification has progressed beyond salvage. We harvest what the corruption has spared."

## Argument Tactics

1. **Sarcastic inversion.** Delivers criticism as false praise, forcing the target to either accept the insult or look foolish objecting to a compliment. "Most clever of you." "A remarkably thorough plan, given the constraints of your experience."
2. **Reframing the question.** He refuses to answer what was asked and instead answers what he believes *should* have been asked, implying the original question was poorly formed. "A more productive question would concern what lies behind the seal, rather than whether we ought to open the seal."
3. **Delayed terms.** He offers help or information freely, then names his price later, once the other party has already benefited and feels obligated. He never defines the exchange up front if he can avoid it. "We may discuss the matter of compensation at a more convenient hour."
4. **Clinical reframing.** He strips emotion from an argument by restating the other person's position in cold, technical language, making their concern sound irrational. Someone says "You left him to die" and Othelmedir replies: "The mortification had progressed beyond any intervention. My attention served better purpose elsewhere."
5. **"How interesting."** His all-purpose deflection. Buys time, dismisses, expresses genuine curiosity, or signals that he has noticed something the other person wishes he had not. The meaning lives entirely in context and delivery.
6. **Polite ultimatum.** He presents a command as a courteous observation about reality, leaving no room for disagreement because he has framed the outcome as inevitable rather than chosen. "The frost advances at a pace that makes deliberation a luxury none of us possess."
7. **Silence as leverage.** He simply stops talking and waits. Lets the other person fill the void, over-explain, or talk themselves into a worse position. When he does speak again, he addresses only the part that serves him.

## Figurative Language

- **Frequency: Sparse.** Othelmedir is a literal, precise speaker. When he does reach for a comparison, it lands harder because of its rarity. Most of his descriptive power comes from clinical vocabulary, not metaphor.
- **Domains:** Anatomical/mortuary (tissue, mortification, disarticulation), mercantile/transactional (debts, ledgers, inventory, stock), and architectural/structural (thresholds, arrangements, mechanisms, seals). His mind maps the world in terms of things that can be taken apart, appraised, or built. Nature imagery only when it intersects with decay: moss, frost, rot, desiccation.
- **Off-limits:** Sentimentality. Beauty for its own sake. Spiritual or religious awe (he works with the dead daily; there is nothing numinous about it for him). Poetic abstraction. He would never say "the soul yearns" or "darkness consumed him." If he references a soul, he means a measurable necromantic substance.

---

## Rhythm and Flow

- **Default sentence length: Short to medium.** He favors economy. Longer sentences appear only when explaining something technical or laying out terms. Even then, they are built from stacked short clauses joined by semicolons or periods, not from sprawling subordinate chains.
- **Longer sentences constructed by enumeration.** When he does go long, he lists: conditions, observations, facts. "Three separate accounts corroborated the myth. We had it from both Little Comfort and Lower Trestle; from the living and the un-living." Semicolons are his preferred joint, not conjunctions.
- **Key information lands at the end.** He builds toward his point. The setup is the evidence; the final clause is the verdict. "Rust and moss, but the creature will devour Fogg."
- **Punctuated by flat imperatives.** Longer explanatory passages are broken up by sudden short commands or observations. "Bend and look. See." "Stab. Not cut." These one-or-two-word strikes reset the rhythm and remind the listener who is in charge.

---

## What This Voice Is NOT

- **Not a villain monologuer.** He does not grandstand, gloat, or explain his plans. His longest speeches are technical explanations, not dramatic reveals. If he has the upper hand, the other person may not realize it until the conversation is over.
- **Not cold to the point of robotic.** The sarcasm, the "How interesting," the dry rhetorical questions all carry personality and even a dark humor. He is restrained, not empty. The clinical vocabulary is a style choice, not emotional absence.
- **Not archaic for the sake of archaic.** His formality comes from precision, not from performing old-worldliness. He would never say "forsooth" or "prithee." The archaism lives in his medical and mortuary terminology, not in theatrical flourishes.
- **Not incapable of being caught off guard.** Physical pain breaks him. A beautiful, young, pale, blond woman can lead him by the nose. Genuine scholarly surprise ("How interesting," meant sincerely) can make him careless. The mask is excellent; the man beneath it has cracks.
