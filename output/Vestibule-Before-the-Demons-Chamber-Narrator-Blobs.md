# Vestibule Before the Demon's Chamber — Narrator Blobs

## 2. NARRATOR

*Every grunt, every war-cry, every whispered prayer between sword strokes -- Guilliam has been listening. Not as an enemy listens, studying your weaknesses. As a composer listens, transcribing your rhythms. Your breath patterns are cataloged. The cadence of your killing arm is scored and set to pipes. These silver-dressed dead are his prized choir, and every note you have sung since entering this tomb is already threaded into its walls. I tell you this not from kindness: every corpse you cut down from here forward feeds his song exponentially. You cannot fight quietly. He has made certain of that.*

## 9. NARRATOR

*You thought this was a barricade. It was a concert hall. Every corpse was a tuning fork calibrated to harvest a different frequency of your faith -- the pitch of your conviction, the timbre of your rage, the hum beneath your exhaustion. Even now, your breathing feeds it. Walk forward. The final chamber is where the song is loudest, and Guilliam is waiting to hear you sing.*
