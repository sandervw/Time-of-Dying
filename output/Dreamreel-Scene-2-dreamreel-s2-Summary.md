# Dreamreel: Scene 2 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

In *The Hooded Pillow*, the bedmeister Noseman explains to Weirmoth, a wiry adventurer-for-hire, that Sulky Wend's nights are eaten by the Dreamreel: an unrelenting vision of strangers' deaths from the sealed Tower. He pleads for a rider. Othelmedir, listening from the plank counter, presents himself as a specialist in dream-mancy, names no fee, binds the work by barter and oath. Weirmoth boasts his spear-myth and lets slip that he never dreams. Noseman lays three coppers, glances at Othelmedir with something other than sleeplessness, then looks down. Two horses are agreed for first light. Othelmedir crosses to a worn bench and sits, the prosthetic side of him hanging quiet beneath the cloak. The Dreamreel is named; the bluffer-and-architect partnership is struck; Noseman's silent recognition stays unspoken.

## Ending Text

...Othelmedir lowered his head a finger's breadth. He crossed to the bench whose edge had been worn shiny by the shoulders of generations of sleepers, and sat.

## Questions Posed

- What does Noseman recognize in Othelmedir, and will he name it?
- Can a bluffing grave-carter ride to the Tower without being unmasked?
- Why does Weirmoth claim never to dream, and does the Dreamreel reach him?
- What does Othelmedir's "barter and oath" cost the village in the end?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir:** Tall, deliberate; oxblood velvet cloak with the left side hanging quiet over a hidden white prosthetic arm; iron eyes, dark broad face, salt-and-pepper beard. Speaks in long archaic periods; restates the other speaker, often names his addressee. Composed, listening, withholding identity.

**Noseman Under Eaves (the Bedmeister):** Sleep-starved; lower lids broken with red lacework, irises lost in yellowed white; runs his thumb along the fraying attercop-silk cord of his ledger. Speaks in clipped six-word fragments, mostly imperatives and questions. Ground-down; quietly recognises Othelmedir; will not say so.

**Weirmoth:** Wiry, mid-thirties; sideburns to the jaw, half-grown mutton-chop on the lip; immaculate quill-knife nails on grave-carter knuckles; cloak of funeral-shroud patches with reversed seams; signature spear (oiled-twine butt, hairline-split haft). Chipper picaro, declaratives-about-the-past, body-part patter; bluffs the mystic work; claims he never dreams.

**The barefoot child:** Asleep against the bench-leg, mouth open, eyelids running with what runs behind them. Background presence; her even breathing carries the scene's image-sound counterpoint against the Dreamreel description.

## Reflection

**What worked:** Noseman's clipped 6-word cadence against Othelmedir's long periodic clauses; the child's even breath as counterpoint to the Dreamreel description; the prosthetic asymmetry kept implied, never named.

**What was cut in review:** Tradesman-vehicle simile ("tally of stabling"); roadman simile in the closing measure-of-height; "never close his eyes" coda (too explanatory).

**What's missing:** Bedmeister's near-recognition could land harder; Weirmoth's hand-washing tic only registers once.
