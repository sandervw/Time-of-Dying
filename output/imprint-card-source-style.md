# Source Imprint: source-style

**Sentence-length distribution:** Long periodic sentences (25-50 words) dominate; short anchors (4-8 words) used for blunt statement or dialogue. No middle range.

**Clause stacking:** Right-branching and periodic; subordinate clauses stack after the main verb, withholding resolution.

**Register:** Archaic-formal; no contractions; Latinate abstraction mixed with plain Anglo-Saxon nouns. Thee/thou in dialogue.

**Dominant senses:** Temperature and sight; cold as dread signal; visual detail rendered as moral data.

**Signature tics:** Repetition of subject-noun at clause head ("And he... And she... And Yadar..."); coordinating "And" to open sentences; participial absolute ("Forgetting the presence of...").

**DO:** Open clauses with "And"; stack right-branching subordinates; use cold/heat as emotional register; repeat subject nouns.

**DON'T:** Contractions; modern connectives (but/so/because as openers); interior monologue in present tense; mid-length filler sentences.
