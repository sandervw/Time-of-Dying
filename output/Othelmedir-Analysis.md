# Othelmedir - Character Analysis

## Description

Othelmedir is a necromancer of extraordinary height, compared to a redwood, who towers a head above tall men yet rises thin as a pond reed. His appearance recalls Keith David: iron eyes set in a broad, dark face, short cropped curls, a salt-and-pepper beard, and a deep authoritative presence that fills a room before his voice does. He wears a thick velvet cloak the color of oxblood. His left shoulder and arm are gleaming white: a full prosthetic, fabricated-marble-tissue arm that hangs still and lifeless at his side. He carries no visible weapon.
On the road he travels by wagon with a singing mushroom, firebug lantern, banshee-wax, and bone dust. Chained in a crate he hauls Grot, his golem: an enormous horror of stitched tissues, three man-arms, three skewed eyes, and a face of cured human skin, the foremost example of his craft as a shaper of living and undead flesh.

*Most assume he is maimed by his prosthetic. In truth the arm is one of his own tissue-based creations, capable of animating with brutal, super-biological strength; a secret weapon held in reserve, because Othelmedir will always try the cunning approach first. His actual spellcraft produces only small effects: orbs of woven light, whispered deadspeech, minor enchantments. His real power is knowledge, lore, and the necromantic tools and substances he has built or acquired.*

## Personality

Othelmedir is measured, cerebral, and ruthlessly pragmatic. Not evil, but far from good: cold, self-serving, and driven by intellectual ambition above all else. He speaks in precise, formal language and commands respect without raising his voice. His nose crinkles at others' laughter; he keeps his own sarcasm internal. He feels no grief for the dead beyond their value as raw material, and will kill without hesitation to replenish his stock. Yet he will deal fairly if others do the same. He is a stickler for bonds, debts, pacts, and oaths, operating almost entirely through barter: no service without return, always. He rarely defines terms outright, preferring to wait until he holds the position of advantage and then set both sides himself. His terms are rarely unfair or malicious, just self-serving. Scholarly curiosity can distract him to the point of carelessness, yet his mind works constantly, drawing on deep arcane knowledge to solve problems others cannot perceive. He uses deception as readily as spellcraft, presenting himself as a "poor necromancer" when it serves him. His one true weakness is beautiful, young, blond, pale women. Not that he harasses them; but an innocent, pretty face can lead him by the nose where threats and sorcery cannot.
