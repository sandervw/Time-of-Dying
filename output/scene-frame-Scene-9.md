## Source Imprint

**Extracted features:**

1. **Sentence-length distribution:** Long establishing sentences (20-35 words) alternate with very short declaratives (3-8 words); the short sentences land as blunt facts, never as atmosphere.
2. **Clause stacking:** Right-branching with light coordination ("and … and …"); periodic constructions appear only for moment-of-consequence sentences.
3. **Register:** Plain-to-archaic blend; formal diction without Latinate inflation; contractions absent in narration, rare in dialogue.
4. **Dominant sensory channels:** Visual and tactile; sensation reported as observed fact, not as simile.
5. **Dialogue form:** Short, declarative; no attribution beyond "said" and "cried"; dialogue stands on its own without tag embellishment.
6. **Signature tic:** Justice / consequence stated as impersonal rule ("To each who comes, justice is done") — moral weight delivered in flat, repeated formula.
7. **Repetition:** Key phrases repeat verbatim across the scene for structural weight, not for lyrical effect.

**Do:** short declaratives for consequence moments; right-branching coordination; moral/thematic weight delivered flat. **Don't:** simile chains, sensory metaphor, interior effusion, tag verbs beyond "said".

## Forbidden Patterns

**Rolled bans:**
- No `as one [verbs] a [noun]` similes
- No metaphors built on light or darkness
- No `not X, but Y` constructions
- No scene-ending negation coda (`and X did not Y`)
- No participial dialogue tags mid-sentence
- No glowbug, lantern, or torchlight throwing shadows imagery
- No archaic register in narration (archaic is reserved for Othelmedir's direct speech only)

**Scene-specific bans (PREDICTIVE):**
- Do not let Othelmedir's argument arrive as a list — no enumerated or bullet-shaped rhetoric in the prose; his case must feel cumulative, not catalogued
- Do not render the pendant's concealment as a moral judgment from the narrator; the asymmetry must land through placement and silence only
- Do not have Weirmoth express surprise at Othelmedir's logic; his counter-bargain should read as already decided, not as a reaction
