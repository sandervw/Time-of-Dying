# Vestibule Before the Demon's Chamber — Description Blobs

## 1. DESCRIPTION

The vestibule opens wider than it should, the vaulted ceiling climbing until it vanishes into a dark that swallows torchlight whole. Along both walls the dead sit upright in two facing rows, dozens of them, hands folded on laps draped in silver cloth sewn thick with garnets and river pearls. Not one jewel has been pried loose. Not one body slumped or toppled.

The air hums. It presses against your teeth, your sternum, the thin bones behind your ears. Every pipe in the walls carries it, a harmonic vibration sourceless and constant, and threaded through it you catch something familiar. Your own melody, the marching hymn you sang three corridors ago, returned to you wrong. Slower. Flattened into a key that does not belong to any chapel you have known.

## 8. DESCRIPTION

Silver cloth and shattered bone cover the floor in overlapping heaps. Your blade is notched in four places. The tabard across your chest hangs in strips, and sweat has soaked through the gambeson beneath until the wool clings cold against your ribs.

You stand still and wait for silence. It does not come. The walls carry a sound fuller than anything the dead produced while moving, every surface thrumming with a song built from your own breath and steel. It is louder now than when the congregation stood. Louder, and growing.
