# Scene Frame — Scene-4

## Scene Parameters

**POV / Tense:** Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir), past tense. (User-locked; not rolled.)

**Senses:** kinaesthetic, touch, taste
- Kinesthesia grounds the fight in effort and body-weight. Touch gives the spider-silk and stone surfaces texture. Taste delivers the rot and meat-smell

**Rhythms:** fragmented, cumulative
- Fragmented rhythms for violence: short bursts as Weirmoth works. Cumulative for Othelmedir's internal appraisals: stacking clauses.

**Scene shape:** circular
- The scene opens outside the threshold and ends at the muscle-wall — a new barrier replacing the door, sealing the same kind of passage.

**Color palette:** muted earth-tones
- Pale green-grey gas, spider-silk grey, old-wax flesh.

## Source Imprint

**Observed features:**

1. **Sentence length:** Short declaratives (5–10 words) alternate with mid-length action chains (20–35 words); no long periodic sentences.
2. **Clause structure:** Right-branching, additive ("and she…, and so…"); minimal subordination.
3. **Register:** Plain, slightly formal; no contractions; no slang; Latinate words avoided except for specificity ("pinioned").
4. **Sensory channels:** Predominantly visual and kinaesthetic; no smell, almost no sound.
5. **Tics:** Comma-and-conjunction chains for action sequences; dialogue is blunt, declarative, emotionally compressed.
6. **Pacing:** Action reported flatly, without adverbial color; emotion surfaced through physical detail, not interiority.

**Do / Don't:**
- Do: flat declarative action verbs, right-branching chains, clipped dialogue.
- Do: let physical detail carry the emotion; don't name the emotion.
- Don't: subordinate-clause nesting, adverbial hedging ("almost," "seemed to"), or reflective pause mid-action.
- Don't: internal monologue that paraphrases what just happened.

## Speech Rules

### Othelmedir

**Positive mode:** Usually restates the other character's last line before replying (~50% cap)
**Register:** Archaic
**Sentence-length cap:** Minimum 15 words; no cap maximum
**Signature construction:** Usually names the person he is addressing (~50% cap)

**Saturation cap (constant):** signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of the character's lines.

**Banned:** "What does it feel like" type questions; fragment-as-style; any direct disclosure of builder-knowledge (he does not yet name his role in the tower's sealing)

---

### Weirmoth

**Positive mode:** Speaks in declaratives about the past (his fabricated origin tales) (~50% cap)
**Register:** Thieves'-cant / criminal-slang
**Sentence-length cap:** 25 words maximum
**Signature construction:** Most lines refer to a body part (~50% cap)

**Saturation cap (constant):** signature construction and positive mode each fire on no more than ~50% of the character's lines.

**Banned:** "What does it feel like" type questions; fragment-as-style

---

## Forbidden Patterns

**Rolled bans:**
- No `X like Y` similes where Y is domestic or food
- No similes comparing the dead or decaying to objects
- No staccato comma-list cadences (e.g., `throat, chest, skull`)
- No `as if` constructions
- No glowbug, lantern, or torchlight throwing shadows imagery
- No sententious closing lines (one-line moralisms)

**Scene-specific bans:**
- No lines that cause Weirmoth (or the reader) to register that Othelmedir is recognized by the corpses
- No staging where Othelmedir's knowledge of the layout reads as surprise or discovery; his familiarity should be wordless
- No gas described as fog, mist, or smoke — it is heavier than air, pooling at knee-height
