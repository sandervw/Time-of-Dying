# Dreamreel — Memorials Blobs

## 4. MEMORIALS (3)

Halgrim Snathe, cartwright of the Sixth Verge, axle-true and oath-true, died of a wheel his own hands trued. The buyer in Lowmarrow waited three days, then bought another cart, then forgot the name on the bill of sale.

---

Slacken your reins, rider. The dead listen for hooves, and they have learned the difference between a passing trot and a stopping one. Do not stop.

---

I am one of seventeen the bluff would not hold. The mason carved no name because no kin came to give one. If you read this and felt a draft, that was me. I have been waiting for someone to feel a draft.

## 12. MEMORIALS (3)

Fellow Hain, of the Red Pew. Last to leave the lecture, first to set the kettle. He died with chalk under his fingernails and the door bolted from inside, as he had asked.

---

I kept the ledgers. I kept the silences. The first was harder. Vespertine, sealed in by her own ink, sealed in by her own tongue, sealed in by her own choosing. Read the figures on my stone; they balance.

---

Wayfarer, the marker beside this one wears no face. The attercop has stitched silk over the carving these forty years. Do not brush it clean. The bedwarden asked for the dark and the dark was given.

## 22. MEMORIALS (3)

Here lie the cabal of the Tower of the Attercop, who set their hands upon the lever and their eyes upon the door, and pulled the one while watching the other, and were not seen again by friend or foe. Their work survived them. Their names did not.

---

Sleeper, may your dreams be your own again. The cauldron above the village is stoppered. The reel that turned behind your eyes has been unwound. Sleep deep. Sleep without witness. Sleep as the lucky dead sleep.

---

Some debts travel with the debtor. The tin at my throat was hammered by a fellow whose face I will not give you. He kept faith. The one who took it from me has not yet learned what faith costs.
