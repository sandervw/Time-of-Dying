# Lore Extraction: The Graveside Inn

### Creatures

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Living Bones -- named category of conversant skeleton undead** -- "Living Bones" is a proper noun for a walking, conversant skeleton capable of sustained speech and rhyming verse. Distinct from Gasps and generic Restless Upright Bones. No existing named category of conversant skeletal undead is documented.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Sewer Worm -- creature with supernaturally strong silk** -- Sewer Worm silk is used as rope and is explicitly stronger than iron. Not in Creatures.md. Note: also belongs in Magic-Items.md as a material source.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Vampirism as a harridan's curse** -- Kriecher of Graveside was transformed into a vampire by a harridan cursing him "under her moon." He is described as "living but not, frozen far away," suggesting vampirism produces a suspended, displaced state. Creatures.md lists vampire types but records no origin mechanic. This establishes harridan-curse as at least one origin path.

### Magic Systems

- `[REINFORCES]` `[IMPLIED]` **Written Deadspeech as summoning medium** -- Scroll fragments bearing Deadspeech found at a murder scene imply Deadspeech can be recorded in written form and used in ritual work, not only spoken aloud. Existing doc says: "Spells are spoken aloud -- gagging a mancer prevents casting." Written Deadspeech is not independently functional; it is a notation system.

### Magic Items

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Sewer Worm silk -- material stronger than iron** -- A rope of Sewer Worm silk holds a fully armored knight beyond his ability to break free. Not in Magic-Items.md.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ill Omen's Heart -- named necromantic substance** -- A labeled vial in a doctor's satchel. Name implies ominous or predictive properties used in medical or necromantic practice. Not in Magic-Items.md. May relate to Prophecy or Astral Mythology categories.
