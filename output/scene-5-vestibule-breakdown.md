# Vestibule Before the Demon's Chamber

## 1. DESCRIPTION
- Vestibule opens wide, vaulted ceiling lost in shadow
- The dead here wear jewel-sewn cloths of precious silver, untouched by looters
- Corpses seated upright along the walls like a silent congregation
- Air thick with harmonic vibration, the pipes humming from every direction
- Igon's own melody faintly audible in the resonance, distorted

## 2. NARRATOR
- Announces: these are Guilliam's most prized servants, dressed for ceremony
- Taunts: each note Igon has sung or grunted is now threaded into the tomb's architecture
- Warns the reader: every kill from here forward amplifies the demon's song exponentially
- Reveals: Guilliam has been cataloging the knight's fighting rhythm, breath patterns, war-cries

## 3. ACTION
- First wave rises slowly, silver-clad dead peeling from the walls
- Engage them before they form ranks or the choral voice synchronizes
- Target the jaw first on each corpse to cut off the singing
- Breaking jaws only redirects the sound through the pipes, does not silence it
- If surrounded, use the vestibule pillars to funnel attackers into single file

## 4. DIALOGUE — Guilliam
- Asks what it feels like when courage leaves the body
- Compliments Igon's swordsmanship, calls it "exquisite percussion"
- Observes that faith sounds different when the faithful are afraid
- Thanks the knight for each swing, each grunt, each prayer muttered between blows

## 5. TABLE (4)
- Four notable undead threats in the vestibule, each distinct in combat behavior
- Mix of armored warriors, robed figures, and one fused double-corpse
- Each carries or wears a different resonant object amplifying the demon's song
- Difficulty escalates as Guilliam feeds more stolen melody into each successive corpse

## 6. ACTION
- Second wave rises en masse, the full congregation animate
- Igon hacks through them with escalating brutality, specific dismemberments
- Detail: how armor dents against bone, how silver cloth tangles in the blade
- The bodies do not stop moving when cut, they vibrate and hum from the floor
- Igon begins shouting over the noise, feeding the demon without realizing

## 7. ACTION
- The crescendo: corpses press in from all sides, singing in stolen harmony
- Specific maneuvers: shield-bash, pommel strike, sweeping cuts to clear space
- Igon fights to reach the far door, carving a corridor through the dead
- Each kill adds a new voice to the choral swell behind him
- The vestibule rings like the inside of a bell

## 8. DESCRIPTION
- Aftermath: shattered corpses strewn across the floor in heaps of silver cloth
- Igon panting, drenched, blade notched, tabard shredded
- The silence he expects does not come; the walls sing louder than ever
- Every surface vibrates with his stolen song, amplified and triumphant

## 9. NARRATOR
- Declares: the vestibule was not a defense, it was an instrument
- Reveals: the dead were tuning forks, each one calibrated to harvest a different frequency of faith
- Mocks gently: even Igon's exhausted breathing feeds the architecture now
- Directs the reader forward into the final chamber, where the song is loudest
