# Dreamreel: Scene 7 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Weirmoth drinks the witness-draught and goes under on the oak bench in the still-room. Othelmedir stands over him, listening to the mercenary murmur the names of the old Red Pew cabal in a slurred roll-call. While Weirmoth dreams, Othelmedir runs a sustained interior monologue through the night of the breach: Ostrobone the Rook's attack, the two cabals' necromancies filling the hall, the Red Pew's verdict, and his own hand pulling the lever that released the gas and sealed his colleagues in endless sleep. The monologue is self-mythologizing throughout — framing the act as an instruction carried out, not a crime committed. While Weirmoth sleeps, Othelmedir quietly brews a second, smaller vessel. Weirmoth wakes carefully. Othelmedir greets him and asks what he dreamed.

## Ending Text

Weirmoth's breath caught, then quickened. The mercenary's eyes moved beneath their lids, and the lids rose. He sat up on the oaken bench, carefully, like one nursing a broken rib. "Friend Weirmoth," Othelmedir said. "You have dreamed well. Will you tell me what were those dreams?"

## Questions Posed

- What is the second, smaller vessel Othelmedir brewed while Weirmoth slept — and what does he intend to do with it?
- Weirmoth now holds Othelmedir's own memory of the breach. Will he recognize whose eyes he looked through?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir (Fellow Liddick):** Wears a prosthetic arm, dark cloak, close beard. Archaic, formal register; addresses people by name; restates lines before answering. Inward, self-concealing. This scene reveals his capacity for sustained self-exoneration — he frames mass killing as institutional compliance.

**Weirmoth:** Sellsword; sideburns, shroud-cloak, immaculate hands, spear, poniard. Criminal-cant register, declarative about the past. Appears here only as a sleeping body, murmuring names — passive, subject to Othelmedir's purposes.

**Ostrobone the Rook:** Named only in the interior monologue. Led the Milkmoot invasion wearing a mask of hornet husks. Arrived through the iron-banded gate; the breach is his doing.

**Fellow Sourbell / Fellow Alder the Lank:** Red Pew colleagues, named in the monologue. Sourbell answered the breach with a Cope of Blue Wasps; Alder the Lank with bass necromancies. Both sealed inside the tower.
