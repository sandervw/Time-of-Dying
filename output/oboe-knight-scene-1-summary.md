# The Song You Carried In: Scene 1 Summary

## Scene Summary

Igon, a wind-worshipping pilgrim-knight, descends a sandstone entry into an ancestral tomb sealed by necromancers for three centuries. He talks to himself, revealing his self-appointed title and reliance on faith over magic. A crystal door at the bottom has no visible mechanism. Physical force fails. Listening at the door, he hears only oppressive silence. He hums for comfort. The crystal vibrates, lights from within, and slides open. Igon attributes this to luck. He tests the door from the other side, confirms it responds to his hum, draws his blue glass claymore, and advances. He does not realize the tomb opened because it wanted what he carried in.

## Ending Text

...he unsheathed the blue glass claymore. The blade caught the lantern's glow and burned cold in the dark. Igon faced the chamber ahead, settled his grip, and walked forward.

## Open Questions

- What waits in the chamber beyond the crystal door?
- Why did the tomb respond to Igon's voice specifically?
- What is the relationship between Igon's personal song and the oboe melody he seeks?

## Characters

**Igon (the Oboe Knight) of Ald Soad:** Scuffed iron plate over chain mail. Iron full-face helm with slitted visor and silver-feather plume. Tabard bearing a hand-painted oboe. Hip-mounted glowbug lantern. Warm, jovial voice. Talks to himself constantly. Relentlessly optimistic and self-reliant.
