# Dreamreel: Scene 6 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Weirmoth kills the still-room's guardian dead-dreamer in a brief, vicious exchange — countered at the throat, he pivots to a poniard and drives it home. Othelmedir begins brewing the eleven-reagent compound using ingredients inventoried with tactile precision. Weirmoth, stitching his torn shroud-cloth from a bench, grows suspicious as Othelmedir pockets a handful of unfamiliar grey crystals. Othelmedir deflects with the Generic-Witness lie: a witness-draught must be drunk to retrieve the eleventh reagent from a preserved dream-breath in the gas; any practitioner would burn to ash drinking it; the dream belongs to an anonymous trespasser. The second half is true. He offers the cup. Weirmoth, mask slipping for one quiet line, drinks anyway. As he falls unconscious, Othelmedir lays his own cloak over the sleeping man and watches.

## Ending Text

...Othelmedir caught the cup before it fell. He set it on the slate and stood a moment over the sleeping man. The cauldron's heat rose at the small of his back; the gas chilled his shins. Between those two weathers he waited. Then he drew the oxblood cloak from his own shoulders and laid it across Weirmoth, and watched.
