# Scene 6 Brief

## Premise

Othelmedir, a necromancer under an assumed name, returns to the sealed Tower of the Attercop with hired sellsword Weirmoth to destroy a stoppered cauldron whose gas has replaced villagers' dreams with the death-visions of his old cabal — colleagues he sealed inside years ago by his own hand. The gas preserved them as dreaming undead, and they wake as he enters.

## Already Rendered (DO NOT RE-DESCRIBE)

Othelmedir's appearance (tall, iron-eyed, oxblood velvet cloak, white prosthetic left arm), Weirmoth's appearance (wiry, sideburns, shroud-cloak, spear, immaculate hands), the green knee-deep gas, spider-silk patina on surfaces, the dead false-sun globe, the library/scriptorium with its dead-garden grave-plots. The treatise *Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents* has already been retrieved by Othelmedir and is tucked under his arm.

## Carry-Forward State

Cold open in action: Weirmoth confronts the still-room's guardian dead-dreamer as the scene begins. Othelmedir stands holding the treatise; his last spoken line in Scene 5 was "Weirmoth. Your metier, I think."

## Upstream Revelations (known to Othelmedir, not Weirmoth)

- Othelmedir built the seal and the cauldron; the dreams are his old colleagues' deaths.
- The treatise is his own authored work (the eleven-reagent recipe is his).

## Downstream Setups

- The eleventh reagent — *Respiration from a Silver Dream* — must be named and framed as the one missing piece so Scene 7 can deliver it.
- The "Generic-Witness lie" must land cleanly: Weirmoth must believe the dream is an anonymous invader's, and that any practitioner would burn to ash drinking it. The second half is true; the first is the deception. The dream is in fact Othelmedir's own.
- Othelmedir pocketing unfamiliar reagents seeds Weirmoth's distrust and Scene 9's bargain.

## Scene Outline

Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir), past tense. Target wordcount: 900.

As Weirmoth returns his attention to the room, Othelmedir has brewed a different potion first; a potion to enter that memory. Only one unhabituated to necromancy can drink it safely. Weirmoth qualifies.
