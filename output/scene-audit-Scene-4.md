# Scene Audit — Scene-4

## Story Fit

- **Expand the fighting** — Outline requires foes from both cabals (Milkmoot invaders AND Red Pew tower residents); the draft shows only three Milkmoot guards, no Red Pew dead appear at all.
  > "Three Milkmoot stood as they had stood when the gas had taken them"

- **Deepen the fight specificity** — Outline calls for "vicious, excruciatingly-specific fighting … numbers of foes, how bodies are affected"; three guards dispatched in one paragraph is too thin.
  > "The cudgel-man came first … He set him down on the cudgel-man and pulled the spear out."

- **Gas colour continuity** — Scene 3 establishes the gas as "green-grey heaviness"; Scene 4 calls it "pale green," creating a minor continuity gap between scenes.
  > "the gas had pooled knee-deep there, pale green, smelling of meat stood through a summer"

## Internal Quality

- **Anatomy muddled** — A spear driven through an eye-socket pulling free with "a tendon coming with it" is physiologically odd; tendons don't run through the orbital cavity.
  > "drew the spear free with a wet pop and a tendon coming with it"

- **Minor hyphenation error** — "back-wall" should be two unhyphenated words in this context.
  > "drove it through to the back-wall of the skull"

- **Re-description of prosthetic** — Acceptable; Scene 4 renders it as "dead at his side" (functional detail), not a physical re-description. No flag needed.
