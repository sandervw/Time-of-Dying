# Lore Extraction: The Lake of the Lost

Source: `stories/Lake-of-the-Lost.md`

---

## Cosmology

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Road of Graves crosses water via bridges of gravestone paving** - The Road includes bridges over lakes, and these bridges use the same inscribed memorial stones as the rest of the Road. *("The huge paving stones each bore a deceased's fading inscription, just like the stones in the rest of the road. Yet the bridge spanned a wide, cold, pale lake.")*
  - Existing doc says the Road goes "over mountains and under rivers" but does not describe bridges or clarify that gravestone paving continues over water. This story adds the specific mechanic of bridge-crossings and raises the unresolved question: where are the dead interred when there's no soil beneath the stones?

---

## Death/Afterlife

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **Vengeful ghosts as a consequence of careless killing** - Killing someone without proper precaution risks creating an avenging ghost. The bandit chief specifically avoids murdering the necromancer for this reason: *("No need to make a vengeful ghost.")* This suggests a folk belief (or genuine mechanic) where the manner of death influences whether the deceased becomes hostile undead.

---

## Creatures

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Gasps - additional physical detail** - The story adds that Gasps retain scraps of flesh *("only some small pieces of flesh still clung like tattered sailcloth to their bones")*, that ice spreads specifically from their toe-phalanges, and that they can rise in coordinated groups from the same body of water.
  - Existing doc covers: rise from water with a gasp, form ice, throw rocks from neck pouches. This story adds flesh remnants, toe-phalanx ice formation, group behavior, and tactical intelligence (they pause, they surround, they coordinate barrages).

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Golems - stitched-tissue variety and behavioral programming** - Othelmedir's golem "Grot" is made of stitched human tissues with three arms, three eyes, and a cured-skin face. It operates on standing behavioral orders (*"He has standing orders for my protection"*) and can receive real-time verbal commands (*"Kill the Gasps," "Kill those as well. Delicately."*).
  - Existing doc lists Wax, Glass, and Leather golem varieties and notes they cannot be created under sky or sun. This story adds a tissue/flesh variety, demonstrates command hierarchies (standing orders + live directives), and shows golems can have named identities.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Singing Mushroom** - A fungus with a tiny human mouth on the underside of its cap, capable of humming songs. Used as a necromantic tool; its humming appears connected to incomplete Deadspeech spellwork. *("The fungus's tiny human mouth had started humming a soft song.")*

## Magic Systems

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Motifs in practice - spell composition requires multiple motifs** - The story names Desire, Nature, and Shaping as motif categories used in a single spell, confirming the motif system is combinatorial. *("He had not begun the motifs of Desire or Nature; had he completed those of Shaping?")*
  - Existing doc defines Shape, Desire, and Nature motifs as separate categories. This story confirms a single spell can incorporate motifs from multiple categories and that a caster works through them sequentially.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Spell interruption produces unbounded effects** - Interrupting a spell mid-cast doesn't simply fizzle it; a partially completed spell can become an uncontrolled open invitation. *("It was less of a spell, more of an unbounded, open invitation in Deadspeech.")* Othelmedir's interrupted spell drew the Gasps from the lake - the partial Shaping motif without Desire or Nature meant no targeting, no behavioral constraints.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **Verbal component requirement - gagging prevents spellcasting** - Necromantic spells require spoken words. Stuffing a necromancer's mouth physically prevents casting. *("Stuff his mouth. That'll stop spells.")* This is common knowledge among non-practitioners.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Necromantic substances and tools** - Three new items: **banshee-wax** (purpose unspecified), **bone dust** (carried in a flour sack), and a **firebug lantern**. These are part of a working necromancer's field kit alongside flints, a shovel, and a steel basinet.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Fabricated tissue as necromantic material** - A white, marble-like mass of artificially created biological tissue, worn as a pauldron over Othelmedir's shoulder. The term "fabricated tissue" suggests necromancers can grow or construct living-adjacent material. *("A gleaming white mass over Othelmedir's left shoulder, like a marble pauldron. 'Fabricated tissue,' said Othelmedir.")*

---

## Otherworld Geography

- `[REINFORCES]` `[IMPLIED]` **Lakes along the Road of Graves** - A wide, cold, pale lake exists along (or astride) the Road, bridged by the Road itself. The existing doc states Cursed Forest lakes are "poison pools," but this lake is directly integrated into the Road's path, not off in the wilderness. It harbors Gasps beneath its surface.
  - Existing doc covers the Cursed Forest's poison-pool lakes. This story establishes that significant bodies of water can sit directly on the Road's route, bridged by the Road's own paving stones, and that these waters serve as undead reservoirs.

---

## Summary

"The Lake of the Lost" contributes most heavily to **Magic Systems** lore, demonstrating the motif system in live practice and introducing the critical mechanic of spell-interruption-as-uncontrolled-invitation. It also enriches **Creatures** with a flesh-and-tissue golem variety and the singing mushroom, and provides the first narrative demonstration of Gasps in combat. The story's secondary contribution is to **Cosmology**, raising the unresolved architectural question of how the Road of Graves handles interment when it crosses water.
