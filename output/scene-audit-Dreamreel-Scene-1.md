# Scene Audit — Dreamreel Scene 1

## Story Fit
- **Add Othelmedir's physical description** — Outline calls for iron eyes, broad dark face, cropped curls, salt-and-pepper beard; scene only conveys height and the hidden arm. The reader has no face yet.
  > "He drew the cloak closer at the throat and let the dead weight of his left arm settle against his ribs beneath the velvet, where no one need see it."

- **Restore the "lived near Sulky Wend before" reflection** — Outline lists this as a required beat that primes the whole story; it is absent from the draft.
  > (no corresponding line in scene)

- **Make the cloak read as oxblood** — Outline specifies oxblood velvet; scene says only "velvet." A small but specific costume detail the outline calls for.
  > "the dead weight of his left arm settle against his ribs beneath the velvet"

- **Consider naming (or near-naming) "The Little Mass of Failing Lamps"** — Outline withholds the rite's *significance*, not its title. Hearing the name would let the reader carry it forward; right now it is only an unlabeled chant.
  > "'One. Lamp of the unlit hour.'"

## Internal Quality
- **Opening "forgetting" beat is muddled** — There is no porter established in scene, and the four-day walk arrives as a parenthetical the narrator simultaneously claims he has forgotten. The double negation reads as throat-clearing.
  > "Forgetting the presence of any porter, forgetting indeed that he had walked four days to reach this room, he stood a moment in the doorway..."

- **"Found the count answering a question" is opaque** — The reader has no anchor for what is being counted (bodies? staff? beads?) or what the question is. Either name the count concretely or cut.
  > "he stood a moment in the doorway and looked, and counted, and found the count answering a question he had not yet permitted himself to ask."

- **Geography of the room shifts mid-scene** — First we have "benches against the long wall"; then the child stands "between two of the small tables." Tables appear without setup.
  > "The cold was a hand's-breadth from a child of seven or eight, barefoot, in a grey shift of coarse wool, who stood between two of the small tables..."

- **"A small cold opened" is striking but spatially confusing** — Reader cannot tell whether the cold emanates *from* the child, near her, or independently. The follow-up clarifies range but not source.
  > "From the corner of the room, where Othelmedir had not yet looked, a small cold opened."

- **"The wrong-hung door" callback risks blocking error** — Othelmedir was last "at the plank counter"-bound; the line returns him to the doorway for the metaphor, then the next paragraph has him cross the tables. Tighten the staging or move the metaphor earlier.
  > "And Othelmedir stood beneath the wrong-hung door and listened to the rite as a builder listens to the cracking of his own beam..."
