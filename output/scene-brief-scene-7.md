# Scene Scene 7 Brief

## Premise

In the road-village of Sulky Wend, the living dream the deaths of a sealed cabal of necromancers in the Tower of the Attercop. Othelmedir, who once helped build and then betrayed that cabal under the name Fellow Liddick, has hired the sellsword Weirmoth to escort him in and destroy the cauldron producing the gas. In the Still-Room, having tricked Weirmoth into drinking a witness-draught, Othelmedir now stands over the sleeping mercenary, brews a quieter second potion alongside the staged eleven-reagent poison that awaits its eleventh breath, and drifts through the memory the sleeper is being shown.

## Scene Shape: Reverie

Drift, tableaux, observer/witness, slack time. No fight, no negotiation, no plot ratchet inside this scene. The action is internal; the external frame is two bodies in a still room. Sentences may stretch. Time should feel suspended between Weirmoth's swallow and his eventual stirring. End on a held note.

## Scene Outline

Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir), past tense, target ~600 words. 

Cold open: Weirmoth drinks the cup and goes under. Othelmedir takes the emptied cup from his slackening fingers, lays him out, draws the oxblood cloak from his own shoulders and covers the sleeper. The mercenary begins to murmur. Othelmedir internal monologue runs underneath, an exact mirror of what Weirmoth is now witnessing:

- The night of the breach. The hornet-husk mask of Ostrobone the Rook in the outer hall. The Red Pew's necromancies and Milkmoot's, in brutal close work.
- His former colleagues — Fellow Sourbell, Fellow Alder the Lank, others — instructing him toward the lever. Their faces named plainly in his thought.
- His own hand on the lever of the lidded cauldron. The pull. The first thin green pour of the gas. The doors sealing on the colleagues he left dreaming.
- The reframe: cowardice, not crime; the disruption was the rivals'; he was the one who shut the wound.

The brew-work in this scene is the second, smaller potion only. The staged eleven-reagent poison sits already-measured on the slate; no further reagents go into it here, since the eleventh (the breath from Weirmoth's dream) has not yet arrived. While Weirmoth dreams, Othelmedir secretly brews the second potion alongside the staged main brew. He does not name it for the reader. He sets it aside in an inner pocket. (This is the Lidd Decoction, for the cauldron later; foreshadow only, no payoff here.)

Close: the second potion is stoppered and slipped into Othelmedir's inner pocket. The eleven-reagent brew still stands open on the slate, awaiting the breath that has not yet come. Weirmoth has not yet stirred. Othelmedir stands a moment over the sleeper, listens to the last names being murmured (his own old name among them), and waits.
