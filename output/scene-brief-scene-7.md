# Scene scene-7 Brief

## Premise

Othelmedir, a necromancer under an assumed name, has returned to a sealed crypt (the Tower of the Attercop) above Sulky Wend, where residents' dreams have been replaced by death-visions of an old cabal he once belonged to. Years ago, as Fellow Liddick, he released the necromantic gas that sealed his colleagues inside as dreaming undead, to buy time against a rival cabal's breach. He has hired the sellsword Weirmoth to help him destroy the cauldron still producing the gas, while concealing his authorship of the disaster.

## Scene Structure

- POV: Limited 3rd-person, Othelmedir
- Tense: Past
- Target wordcount: 600

## Carry-Forward State

- Still-room. Weirmoth has just drunk the witness-draught ("I am not so clever as you take me for, friend") and is going under on the oak bench.
- Cauldron on the worktop holds the ten-reagent brew, lacking only the eleventh (*Respiration from a Silver Dream*) Weirmoth must fetch from the dream.

## Upstream Revelations

- Othelmedir's old name was Fellow Liddick. His former colleagues (Fellow Sourbell, Fellow Alder the Lank, others of The Red Pew) ordered him to release the gas; he pulled the lever.
- The invader cabal was Milkmoot, led by Ostrobone the Rook in a mask of hornet husks.
- The witness-draught gives Weirmoth Othelmedir's own memory of the breach, not an anonymous invader's. Othelmedir lied.

## Tonal Notes

- Most of the scene is Othelmedir's interior monologue: archaic, formal. He frames the lever-pull as cowardice, not crime.
- The monologue tracks Weirmoth's dream beat for beat: breach, instruction, lever, sealing.
- Minimal external dialogue.

## Scene Outline

(Cold open optional; scene picks up from Weirmoth raising the cup.)

Weirmoth drinks the witness-draught and goes under. Othelmedir stands over the sleeping body, listening to the mercenary murmur the names of the old tower cabal's members. **While Weirmoth dreams, Othelmedir runs an internal monologue through most of the scene: the night of the breach**; his hand on the lever that will unseal the lidded cauldron, releasing the gas; the colleagues he sealed inside. His monologue is an exact reflection of what Weirmoth is witnessing, and what the mercenary will know when he wakes.

- (Internal monologue, Othelmedir's memory) Weirmoth relives the original attack through Othelmedir's eyes; the two mancer cabals and their necromancies in brutal combat.
- (Internal monologue, Othelmedir's memory) Othelmedir's former colleagues instruct him to release the gas.
- (Internal monologue, Othelmedir's memory) Othelmedir pulls the release lever, dooming the original occupants to endless sleep.
- (Othelmedir) Internal monologue in archaic, formal, self-mythologizing register; treats the original act as cowardice, not crime.
- Weirmoth awakes; while he has dreamed, Othelmedir has secretly brewed a second potion (*subtext* this one is not to dissolve the muscle-wall, it has another purpose).
