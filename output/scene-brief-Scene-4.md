# Scene Scene 4 Brief

## Premise

Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir), past tense, target ~1,100 words.

In the road-village of Sulky Wend, residents' dreams are being replaced by the death-visions of an old necromancer cabal sealed inside a nearby chrypt, the Tower of the Attercop. Othelmedir, a necromancer who was once a member of that cabal under another name, hires the sellsword Weirmoth and returns to destroy the cauldron producing the gas, before the villagers' memories are entirely overwritten. The same gas that traps the dead has preserved them; they wake as the seal breaks.

- Othelmedir: oxblood cloak, white prosthetic left arm hanging limp, tall black man.
- Weirmoth: sideburns, cloak of patched shrouds, preserved canary "Mister Ribbon" (one glass-bead eye, one empty socket).
- The chrypt door (oak-black, iron banding, brass seal) and its unsealing.
- The pale green-grey gas already pouring over the sill, heavier than air.
- Cabal names "Red Pew" (tower) and "Milkmoot" (invaders).

## Carry-Forward State

- Gas spilling down the outer steps; entering means walking into the source.
- Weirmoth seated on a cold stone block, Mister Ribbon in hand. Must rise and arm before combat.
- Weirmoth has just admitted he slept badly (contradicting his "never dreams" brag); his uneasiness about Othelmedir going in unarmed is fresh.

## Ending Text (Scene 3)

"Mister Ribbon," he said to the canary, softly, "this meed we're earning today is more than square gold pieces in the pocket, eh, more than pocketed squares." Othelmedir watched the mercenary, but said nothing.

## Scene Outline

Outer chambers. Old invaders, long-preserved in the gas, rouse and attack. **Vicious, excruciatingly-specific fighting** takes place between the two reanimating cabals of necromancers (invaders and tower residents), and Othelmedir/Weirmoth — describe specific maneuvers, movements, numbers of foes, how bodies are affected. Weirmoth notes how Othelmedir seems to know every corridor. They encounter a "muscle-wall," a barrier of flesh woven over the passage to the lower sanctums of the tower; preventing the further release of the gas, and further progress.

- Gas pools knee-deep in low corners, pale green
- Every surface wears a velvet patina of spider silk; footsteps leave dark bruises
