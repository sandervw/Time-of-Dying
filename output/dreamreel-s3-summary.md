# Dreamreel: Scene 3 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Othelmedir and Weirmoth climb the bluff to the Tower of the Attercop. Weirmoth admits he slept poorly, contradicting last night's brag. Othelmedir lays his palm on the seal, whispers the deadspeech _Öltesoldzo sjhaj_, and the wax lifts; greenish gas pours from the seam, cobwebs quiver, a spider stirs. Weirmoth lets slip an oath by "the Pew's own teeth"; Othelmedir corrects it to the Red Pew. While they wait for the first fume to thin, Othelmedir warns that if the gas keeps pouring the villagers' memories will be replaced entirely. Weirmoth produces a stitched canary, Mister Ribbon, and addresses it as "Milkmoot weather"; the second self-betrayal cracks his grin. The cabal names Red Pew and Milkmoot are spoken for the first time.

## Ending Text

..."Mister Ribbon never did take to Milkmoot weather in the throat," Weirmoth said into the bird's stitched neck, soft as a man at a confessional, and then, hearing himself a second time, looked up and tried for the grin again. It did not quite come.

## Questions Posed

- Why did Weirmoth's old oath name the Red Pew, and how much does he know that he is not saying?
- What lies past the threshold once the fume thins, and will the cauldron still be feeding?
- What did Weirmoth dream?

## Characters Introduced or Referenced

**Othelmedir:** Older man in a long grey stole worn folded over the forearm, hands ungloved and chalk-greyed, no visible weapon. Quiet, archaic in speech, names whomever he addresses, restates their words back. Carries the calm of a man returning to work he himself once sealed.

**Weirmoth:** Sellsword in a shroud-cloak (an old cassock by the cut), left bootheel worn from a kneeling habit. Brags in thieves'-cant about a fabricated past; touches body parts when speaking. Twice catches himself letting old liturgy slip out. Carries Mister Ribbon, a stitched canary, in altar-felt.

**Mister Ribbon:** A preserved canary, throat sewn shut in tiny black stitches, wings folded as if perched. Kept wrapped in old altar-felt inside Weirmoth's cloak. Addressed as a confidant.

## Reflection

**What worked:** Twin self-catches by Weirmoth (oath, canary) earn the reversal without spelling out his past. Othelmedir's "another year, another name" claim plants the historic-knowledge thread.

**What was cut in review:** A horse-ear simile (animal-vehicle, blacklisted); a fragment line for Weirmoth.

**What's missing:** A sharper smell of the cauldron beneath the gas; a future scene can pay this off.
