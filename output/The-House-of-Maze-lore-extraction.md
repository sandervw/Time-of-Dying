# Lore Extraction: The House of Maze

## World Geography

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Middlemoss occupies two canyon walls** cut through yellow-heather heath. The canyon floor was never settled beyond the reach of refracted sunlight.
  - Adds to existing doc: canyon geometry, yellow-heather heath detail, and the architectural rule limiting settlement to refracted-sunlight reach.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Middlemoss is entirely built from repurposed tohmbs.** White marble facades on sandstone terraces were originally layered tohmbs descending into the canyon. Upper tohmbs became living space over generations; the town grew downward within sunlight's reach.
  - Adds to existing doc: the entire built fabric of the oldest town is repurposed funerary architecture.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Middlemoss is the oldest town in living and dead memory.** Even the conversantly-dead (Living Bones and similar) recall no older settlement. This anchors Middlemoss as a cosmological center-point, not merely geographic.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **The Holt of Old Souls** is a named wilderness landmark more than a hundred leagues from Middlemoss. Used as a waypoint reference for frontier territory. Not in existing `setting/Mythology/World-Geography.md`. New entry needed.

## Creatures

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Dust Slimes** are non-sentient debris animated by ectoplasm suffused with scrap soul fragments. Six varieties: hunchhobble, wriggling mass, green, brownish-green (venomous), dropper, and false puddle. Most dangerous in confined spaces; must be drawn to open ground to fight. Not listed in `setting/Mythology/Creatures.md`. New entry needed.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Cankarnis** is a vein predator: rotting man's head on a living stone statue body. Stone limbs and torso are impervious to mundane steel. Skull softens under maze-madness. Body regenerates at moonrise unless a mancer intones hidden spells from midnight to the crow's call.
  - Adds to existing doc: regeneration mechanic, combat anatomy, "vein predator" categorization, lamprey tongue, and basalt fingernails.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Giant rats** in wild territory reach bulldog proportions, are territorial, and hunt men. Frontier trappers called "Squeakers" catch them in deadfalls and guillotine boxes and sell the pelts. Not mentioned in `setting/Mythology/Creatures.md`. New entry needed.

## Magic Items

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Scroll-Armor** is metal plate lined inside with yellow vellum scrolls covered in a mancer's script. Most ghosts will not trouble the wearer. Establishes that written Deadspeech inscribed on worn objects can passively deter undead -- a distinct application from spoken spellwork. Not in `setting/Mythology/Magic-Items.md`. New entry needed.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Sleep toxin formula** includes: condensed sunflower juice, oxidized copper dust, columbine petals, sulfur, tohmbdust, and crushed fireflies, stewed together. Significant for the inclusion of tohmbdust, confirming it carries active necromantic or alchemical properties and is a tradeable commodity.

- `[NEW]` `[IMPLIED]` **Ent-Eye** is a rare artifact harvested from a creature called an Ent, sought more than a hundred leagues past the Holt of Old Souls. Described as a "true shiner." Nature and function are unstated; implied to be a rare optical or magical component.

## Proposed New Category: Guilds and Criminal Institutions

Houses of Maze form a named, multi-city guild network with documented mechanics: rat couriers, information brokerage, maze-slavery of intruders, and a Master figure. This is distinct from Hero Cycles and uncovered by any existing category. Proposed scope: named organizations, criminal networks, and trade guilds operating across the Road with their own rules, hierarchies, and mythological weight.
