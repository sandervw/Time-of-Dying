# Lore Extraction: The Tunnel of the Stillbreather

## Magic Systems

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Winter Wind as Necromantic Raw Material** -- First-age necromancers borrowed Winter's breath, installed it in undead hosts, and buried them to create permanent self-sustaining cold. This technique is not lost; it remains active. *"From Winter, which has always been Necromancy's season, they borrowed breath... They installed this breath within their un-living hosts. They buried the hosts in deep mountain tohmbs."*

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Ice as Intrinsic Necromantic Element** -- Ice carries inherent affinity with necromancy; some undead are stronger in cold, and ice recurs as a motif in preservation and spellwork. *"There lives an inherent facet of necromancy in the element of Ice."*

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Homunculus Creation (Updated Detail)** -- Burial substrate is cow entrails (not dung). Feeding substance is explicitly mammal blood; greater mammal yields greater homunculus. *"A homunculus is created when a mancer grows a fetus in a stoppered flask buried in cow entrails. He then uncorks it and nurses it on mammal blood."*
  - Prior doc: cow dung, fed blood, greater blood = greater power. This story adds: cow entrails as burial medium, specifies "mammal blood."

## Creatures

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Fimbulwolf (Additional Context)** -- Ancient lineage predating the current age. Gaze described as prehistoric. Rideable by small creatures. Breath described as "ancient petrichor."
  - Prior doc: "A Winter Wolf." This story adds: ancient brood, rideable by small beings.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Hueless (Named Homunculus Type)** -- Old homunculus, rides a Fimbulwolf. Wax-textured, milk-colored, elongated forehead, four thin hairs, bloodless, four eyes. Speaks in a voice "like water trickling in a library."

## Theogony

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Old Man Below Built in the Meathooks** -- Stopped in the Meathook Mountains on his journey from the bottom to the top of the Road. Built the lost temple The Massy Fane there. Left "reflections of death" in the ice.
  - Prior doc: roving spirit from the End of the Road. This story adds: physical temple in the Meathooks, directional cosmology (bottom-to-top journey).

## Death / Afterlife

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Real Death is Cold; Real Life is Warm** -- Cosmological axiom structuring ice, death, and necromancy. *"Real death is cold, real life is warm."*

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Core Glacier (Eschatological Clock)** -- At the core of Time is a perpetually sweating glacier. When it becomes a lake, Time ceases and all drown. *"At the core of Time is a glacier that is always sweating. When the glacier has become a lake, Time will cease, and all will drown in the flood."*

## World Geography

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Meathook Mountains (Expanded)** -- Road-side settlers and giant rat trappers; ten thousand undead legends; glaciers are frozen undead exhaling Winter breath; lost temple The Massy Fane; quicksnow hazards; glacier-cut canyon; Lost Glaciertop as named sub-location; Crackling Spiders in canyon.
  - Prior doc: named far landmark. This story adds: inhabitants, undead presence, glacier origin myth, named sub-locations, environmental hazards.

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Tunnel of the Stillbreather (Greatly Expanded)** -- Interior: double doors (bronze and moonstone), ice walls with green ambient glow, frozen servants in walls, petrified lake, frozen castle with limestone undercroft housing sarcophagi, Stillbreather's burial chamber as green-light source. Two entrances; passable via glacier route or canyon route.
  - Prior doc: named route, associated with a Lich. This story adds: full physical description, castle structure, Stillbreather as active Lich in open feretory.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Mellowdepth** -- Alternate Road of Graves route: echoing pipe under the sea, lined in slime-covered marble and polyps.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Leviathan Fields** -- Named alternate Road of Graves route, avoided by the protagonist. No further description.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Hornwater** -- Named far city, travel destination. Reachable via three routes from the Meathooks: Road of Graves south through Limbichorous Dunes, the Scarab Mines, or the Forest of Candles and Skulls and Roaches and Fungi and Ruptured Capillaries.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Limbichorous Dunes** -- Thousand-mile stretch of frigid dunes on the southern Road of Graves loop between the Meathooks and Hornwater.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Scarab Mines** -- Underground route under the Waxy Maize Fields, with a stop at cave-town Alsoburg.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Alsoburg** -- Cave-town on the Scarab Mines route; functions as a supply stop.

- `[NEW]` `[EXPLICIT]` **The Waxy Maize Fields** -- Impassable surface obstacle between the Meathooks and Hornwater; circumvented by the Scarab Mines.

## Astral Mythology

- `[REINFORCES]` `[EXPLICIT]` **Aurora (Green Firebeetle Legend)** -- Aurora is an established celestial phenomenon. Meathook folk attribute it to the Green Firebeetle's flight. Story-end aurora (white, blue, purple, gold swirls) lacks the green tint of Stillbreather's undead light, implying a distinction.
  - Prior doc: no aurora. This story adds: named phenomenon with folk-mythological explanation. *"Swirls of white, blue, purple and gold; like a burning phoenix on the firmament."*
