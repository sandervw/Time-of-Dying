# Scene Scene 5 Brief

## Premise

Othelmedir, a necromancer working under an assumed name, has returned to the sealed Tower of the Attercop above Sulky Wend to destroy the cauldron whose gas has been replacing villagers' dreams with the deaths of the cabal he abandoned. He hired the sellsword Weirmoth as muscle and cover, concealing that he himself built and fled this place. The preserving gas is also waking his old colleagues as he enters.

## Scene Outline

Limited 3rd-person (Othelmedir), past tense, ~450 words.

(Cold Open) Othelmedir leads them into a library of rotted codices. Othelmedir retrieves a treatise he helped author. (*subtext* he does not reveal he authored it). They hear the sound of an awakening dead-dreamer down another door; Othelmedir knows it leads to the still-room (*subtext*), and tells Weirmoth to "get ready" (but use a better line than that).
- Library Layout: Three floors with open central balcony overlooking a dead garden; gravestone plots for former cabal members; false sun globe dead and dim
- Books moldering, many unreadable, most have no titles
- Othelmedir explains the gas and the cauldron: elements seep outward even though the cauldron is stoppered, producing the Dreamreel
- (Othelmedir) Loses a moment absorbed in the treatise; it is named 'Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents' (*subtext* the meaning of the name is withheld until next scene)
- (Othelmedir, Internal/*subtext*): these texts are worth more than the village, certainly more than Weirmoth

## Carry-Forward State

Othelmedir and Weirmoth have just encountered muscle-wall at the end of a corridor through which they must pass. The wall cannot be cut through; they must find another route. Weirmoth's cloak is torn hip to knee from a hooked sword. Weirmoth's spear is recovered.

## Already Rendered (DO NOT RE-DESCRIBE)

Spider-silk velvet on every surface; pale green meat-smelling gas; the dead-dreamers' silent mouths still shaping breach-songs. Weirmoth's spear-and-knife combat style.

## Upstream Revelations

Othelmedir has read the breach-cabal's silent chant (Milkmoot) and recognized Ostrobone the Rook's hornet-husk mask among the dead. He is concealing authorship of texts in this house. Weirmoth treats him as a slightly suspect employer but defers on arcana.

## Downstream Setups

The treatise title 'Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents' must land plainly without explanation; next scene reveals its meaning. The still-room's location, accessed through a separate door, is the next destination. Othelmedir's appraising covetousness toward the books seeds the barter over reagents in a later scene.
