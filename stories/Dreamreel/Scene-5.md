# Dreamreel: Scene 5

The door opened onto grey, not darkness — the false-sun globe suspended above the central gallery having long since emptied of whatever luminance it once held, its dead face casting light that was not so much light as the negation of shadow, flat and without warmth or direction; Othelmedir's boot found the first flagstone's raised corner before his eyes had adjusted. He stumbled, yet straightened without comment and without breaking stride, his second foot already correcting for the descent his first had not anticipated, the hand at his side dropping to the rail without his needing to look for it.

Weirmoth moved along the balcony rail, one gloved hand running along the stone — cold even through the leather, the drop to the dead garden below shifting in and out of view through flat pillars as he walked — while Othelmedir examined the shelves. His hand moved from spine to spine and then stopped, the third shelf from the floor, second column from the corner, as though the shelf had been waiting for it. The spine gave under his fingers, soft and yielding, its cover having absorbed so many seasons of damp that it had become a texture rather than a structure. He drew it carefully free of its neighbors. The volume was heavier than one would expect from its mold-thinned cover. He opened it to a page at the middle; the ink, at least, had been preserved. This pleased Othelmedir

"That one worth reading?" Weirmoth had stopped at the rail, watching his companion.

"It is a treatise, Weirmoth," Othelmedir rejoined. He closed the book. "Which is to say it repays study rather than light perusal, and this particular treatise — *Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents* — would repay more study than most of the volumes which have kept it company."

"The gas," said Weirmoth. "You were going to tell me how it works."

"The cauldron in this tower is stoppered, yet elements seep regardless, as a sealed vessel of brine will still sweat salt through its seams, and those elements dispersing outward are what produce the Dreamreel. The deaths the villagers have been dreaming are not visions but residue."

Othelmedir moved to a short spiral stair. Weirmoth followed a few steps behind. They descended to the library's lower floor. Othelmedir turned, and proceeded across the open central space. Weirmoth, uncertain of their direction at first, eventually percieved a door on this lowest level, half-hidden behind a shelf.

Then they heard a sound. It came from somewhere beyond the door; a low, sustained sound of something drawing breath after long stillness came through the wood.

Othelmedir did not move at once. He stood holding the treatise. Weirmoth stood a little farther back, in the center of the open central space below the dead sun, among the garden plots: small humped islands of pale, grassless soil amidst the ankle-deep sea of gas - and each island marked where the cabal had buried one of its mancers.

Othelmedir tucked the treatise under his arm and regarded Weirmoth with an unreadable expression.

"Weirmoth," he said. "Your metier, I think."
