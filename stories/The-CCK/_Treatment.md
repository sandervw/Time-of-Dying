# The Cracked Cup Knight - Chapter-by-Chapter Treatment

## Part 1 - The Spit of Mool

The Cracked Cup Knight, a resurrected warrior indebted to the mancer Othelmedir, is dispatched to the ancient tomb of Mool - a long-dead grot creature - to retrieve its spit as payment for his resurrection. Armed with plate mail, a pinecone shield, and a polished steel longsword, the Knight enters the tomb and navigates its labyrinthine upper halls by following jellyfish-themed carvings, descending into deeper catacombs guided by a gearslave ape automaton.

The tomb's interior is populated by dozens of mechanical ape-constructs of various types, all serving the will of the sleeping mancer Mool. The Knight observes their strange industry from a ledge above a vast underground trench: the apes harvest glowing crystals and tend to colonies of bioluminescent jellyfish in an underground lake. When an ape falls into the lake the entire population erupts into mechanical hysteria. The Knight attempts to escape along a narrow ledge and across rope bridges but is discovered, fights off multiple apes, and is ultimately dragged into the lake by a collapsing bridge.

He wakes in the Heather Vault - a lower level blooming with magenta shrubs - caged by the apes, who intend to boil him alive. He escapes by goading a nearby ape with the forbidden symbol of The Sisterhood of the Yellow Smile, ripping free the creature's iron tail to use as a pry bar, then recovering his sword. He hides, waits for the pursuit to pass, and eventually finds Mool's inner sanctum, where he retrieves his shield, pockets a frozen streak of Mool's black bile spit, and possibly captures a small jellyfish. Mool itself awakens and gives chase. The Knight flees through mirrored moonlight tunnels, cuts through a wire grate, and emerges onto the grass above the tomb - exhausted, but alive, and in possession of his payment.

## Part 2 - The Sewer Under Khoroduhn

The Knight arrives in Khoroduhn, a mountain town under eternal March rain and the shadow of the Weige family - a powerful, cultist-connected clan. His target is Pigburne, the family's shaved-headed Majordomo, a calculating poisoner responsible for the assassination of thirteen children in rival families. The Knight infiltrates the keep's eastern wing in disguise, locks Pigburne in his chamber, strangles him, and excises his heart to prevent necromantic resurrection. As guards converge and the keep bells begin to ring, he abseils down the castle wall.

On the street below he encounters R the Killer, a lean, dark-faced mercenary just broken from the Weige gibbet. The two form a wary alliance and race through the city's rain-slicked streets to a rented room, where the Knight's Blue Lady waits - a noblewoman whose family the Weige clan has destroyed. With the gates locked and militia flooding the streets, the group descends into Khoroduhn's sewer system to escape.

The sewer is a treacherous labyrinth of merging tunnels, sentient flotsam, and hostile creatures: a living wall of rat hides, a bloated mermaid, a coin-hoarding salamander, and a colony of stitched-soul fanatics marked with the Red King's brand. After hours underground, the three emerge into a vast cistern where a Silk Giant named Gigan Othok - former Weige weapon, now maddened recluse - attacks. The Knight defeats him by blinding the light-sensitive giant with the Cat's Eye Spear. A hidden silk-woven wall gives way beyond Othok's lair, leading to a final tunnel.

They emerge outside the city into dawn rain, free of Khoroduhn. R the Killer, too notorious to remain around the Lake of Tomatoes, parts ways and advises them to head for Middlemoss. The Knight and his Lady are left alone in the rain.

## Part 3 - The Funeral on the Road of Graves

Weige assassins locate the Knight and his Lady at a boarding house before they can slip out through the Gate of Green Vines. Three leather-armored killers breach the room; the Knight fights them off, but the struggle sends him crashing through a window into the inner courtyard, where a second wave of assassins waits. The Knight dispatches them using a razorshell - a conch-shaped weapon that erases a crossbowman's face - and kills the final assassin on an upper catwalk with a stolen crossbow, just in time to save his Lady.

They escape through the Gate of Green Vines and travel south along The Road of Graves. At a small village called The Cat in the Jar, a storm forces them to shelter for several days. There they meet Magpie, a consumptive young pleasuress who shows them small, genuine kindnesses - bread, cheese, a spare umbrella. The Knight dispatches a local troll; the Lady and Magpie warm to each other by a hearth fire.

On the third evening the Knight recognizes Magpie is dying. The Lady insists they return her to Ripe for burial on The Road of Graves, refusing to be left behind. Magpie dies on the fifth morning. As the Knight carries her body across the common fields toward Ripe, a Weige mancer named Birchbark raises the field's dead - a tide of rotting cats - and animates Magpie's corpse against the Lady. The Knight cuts through the horde, severs Magpie's reanimated head, and kills Birchbark, retrieving his Sorrowful Father Mask as a trophy.

The chapter closes with the Knight and his Lady departing on a riverraft, floating west past the Gate of the Ten-Plates Ghost toward the dangerous western cities, Magpie buried and the Weige threat temporarily behind them.

## Part 4 - The Graveside Inn

The Cracked Cup Knight watches over his Blue Lady at the Graveside Inn, an ancient mansion converted to lodgings. She wakes from a dream about her dead daughter. Before she can finish speaking, the Steward, a massive, soldier-built man who runs the inn alone, summons the Knight downstairs. He announces that a guest, Madam Strock, has been murdered; the doors are locked and no one is leaving. Eight other guests are present, a strange company: a white-bearded man called Timberwolfe, a walking skeleton troubadour known as The Late Poet, a hand-wringing woman called Miss Leatherworker, the robed Six-Cats-Hiding, the bald Doctor Hoof, an anxious blonde named Frida, and a girl called Melancholia. The Lady urges the Knight to investigate. They find Madam Strock's room wrecked and her body on the bed, with traces of jasmine, loose straw, and shredded deadspeech scrolls left behind. Before the Lady can speak, a scream cuts through the inn. They find Melancholia stabbed through the chest with the Steward's own breakfast knife, lying on a stone theater stage. Frida declares the inn itself cursed, haunted by the vampire Kriecher of Graveside, whose harridan lover damned the house. Timberwolfe dies next, found atop Madam Strock's body. The Knight is then ambushed in a corridor and wakes bound to a chair, accused of the murders by the Steward. Unable to break free, he waits. More guests die in random order; clues are scattered near each body. Eventually, the Knight finds The Late Poet in the Sweetmeats Room, standing over the Steward's own corpse. As the Knight reaches for his returned sword, an invisible force attacks. The Late Poet's bones are shattered. Tracking the killer to a final confrontation, the Knight finds his Lady unconscious on a table and fights a Gallowspook, a straw-cloaked wight summoned under a harridan's moon, surviving until dawn extinguishes its candles. The Lady wakes unharmed. The guests reappear, fleshy ghosts looping their deaths each week. The Late Poet wanders in, hints that the Knight once had a son, whose face he crossed in Middlemoss.

## Part 5 - The Walkers of Whiteeye

A rumbling stomach song, half-musical, half-animal, rolls through the hills as the Knight and his Blue Lady depart the Lake of Tomatoes on the Road of Graves. The Lady pauses to look back at her distant home; the Knight leads her east into the Whiteeye Waste, a vast curtain of greyblack dust decreed by the necromancer-goddess Lady Horsehair to seal the region from the civilized middle of the Road. Shelter is found in the hollow of the Last Tree of Wormapple, a coal-dark stump the width of a fort. They are immediately attacked by Stump Dugs, blind-eyed hounds with soft wriggling teeth. Overwhelmed, the Knight is knocked down; the Lady stands helpless. A band of leather-coated fighters, The Walkers of the Waste, storms in and kills the dugs. Their leader, a grey-haired, cobra-eyed captain named Vettel, stops one of her men from assaulting the Lady. In exchange for safe passage across the Waste, Vettel demands the Lady's palfrey. They accept. Over twelve days, the Knight falls into the Walkers' grim routine of dawn marches and nightly shelter-seeking. A kindly Walker named Wrathmaige explains the Waste and its navigational challenges. He describes the Hearse, a creature like a two-ton dragonfly with a hundred stinging tails and a thousand eyes on its wings, which hunts by night and sings the stomach song. A days-long dust storm blinds the sky, preventing shelter-finding; the Hearse takes one Walker per unshielded night. After the storm briefly clears, Wrathmaige takes a star-reading and forces a march south to a canyon cliff. Crossing the Waste Bridge, a narrow arc of white rock, Wrathmaige is bitten by a Nickel Beetle; its poison has no cure. Vettel abandons the ailing Walker and presses on. The Knight refuses to leave him. On a ruined birch hilltop, the Lady rings the stolen palfrey bells to sing the stomach song herself, luring the Hearse down. The Knight fights it, cutting away stingers and finally impaling it as it tries to crush him. With the creature dead, the three survivors walk into the lush Middle of the Road, the Waste falling away behind them. Wrathmaige, dying, points out Middlemoss between two white peaks before collapsing in a fit of coughs as rain begins to fall.

## Part 6 - The Three Guests

The Knight and his Lady arrive at Cobweb's Hotel in Middlemoss, a cobweb-choked lodging whose owner threatens injury to anyone who snaps a strand. In the common room they find three others already seated by the hearth: R the Killer, a scruple-free mercenary; Wrathmaige, sweating and shivering with beetle poison; and a silent woman called The Mothy Widow. The chapter unfolds as three nested stories told around the fire. R the Killer recounts how he was once hired to protect a mancer-priest clearing the undead from Castle Celadust, a fortress grown from living trees. Inside, a mummy named Nilghiri ensnared him with her onyx eyes; the priest died. R spent an indeterminate stretch as Nilghiri's willing guardian, tending her castle and killing mercenaries sent to destroy her. When Nilghiri, fearing warmth returning to her long-dead heart, released R from her spell, he stayed by choice. They spent one night together; by morning she was a pile of ash-covered linen. He left, collected her valuables, and told the village he had exorcized the place. Wrathmaige responds to R's story with a fragmented, cough-wracked account of being lost in The Old Soulwood, hunted by a Periwinkle Forest Cat, the embodied spirit of an ancient giant. He follows a fuchsia wisp through the dark forest, crosses a glade pool called a Blind Eye, and tracks the wisp into the hollow of a tree before his coughing cuts the story short. His eyes go glassy; he is dead. The Aged Widow, revealed as The Mothy Widow, breaks her silence. She narrates her own story: how she and her husband visited the House of Maze in Middlemoss, and she returned home with a man wearing her husband's face and flesh but not his soul; a Doppelganger Skeleton. Rather than confront it directly, she lured the imposter onto a moonlit moor, having secretly drunk a protective Lemon Cider beforehand, and led it into a pack of ghouls that tore it apart. She never verified the body. The chapter ends with all four, the Knight, the Lady, R, and the Widow, staring silently into the blue-flamed hearth.

## Part 7 - The House of Maze

The Knight and his Blue Lady arrive in Middlemoss, an ancient town of repurposed tombs carved into canyon walls, where a crowd is killing a swarm of thieving white rats. One escapes into a peculiar building: The House of Maze, a scaffolded, windowless guild house whose rats steal secrets for the information-hoarding Master of the Puzzle-mask. A street leech explains the guild's nature, and when the Blue Lady mentions her stepson, the objective becomes clear - seek the Master's knowledge.

The pair squeeze inside and immediately become Slaves of the Maze. The interior is a labyrinth of crawlspaces, revolving walls, floorless corridors, and nonsensical architecture designed to strip occupants of sanity. They battle dust slimes in cramped quarters, encounter a trapped Cankarnis (an undead stone predator), and endure creeping madness across multiple safe rooms including an Echo Room, a garden with a candle-lit oak, a circular stairway with a puzzle sequence, and a Scriptorium where Scroll-Armor can be found. A mad prisoner named Oldmust cryptically advises them to think like the thieves. The key turns out to be the rats themselves: their movements follow number patterns and a set schedule, always accompanied by fiddle music, which eventually leads the pair to the Master.

The final confrontation takes place in an octagonal parlor where the Master's enormous fiddler controls swarms of floor rats with his music while the dwarf Master pumps hallucinogenic musks. The Knight kills the fiddler, then goads the Master into exposing himself. Sparing his life reveals a childlike face beneath the leather mask. The Master confirms he has seen the Blue Lady's stepson in Middlemoss, offering the first concrete lead on the boy's whereabouts.

## Part 8 - The Ghost Factory

Guided by a candlecarver's tip that a ghost is present deeper in Middlemoss's ravine, the Knight escorts the Blue Lady down Albino Fish's Way, a hazardous gorge below the town where centuries of discarded magical detritus have formed a dangerous ecosystem. They fight through waves of Veyers - skull-mutilated undead that inflict debilitating maladies - and escape across a massive dead redwood spanning the gorge. The redwood harbors a spirit that communicates through its bark in prose-poem visions, and in its hollow they find a mancer's journal hinting at a ghost named Hemlock living in a nearby Ghost Factory.

The factory, entered through an iron portcullis that locks behind them, is staffed by deaf dog-slaves and patrolled by wood golems. The pair sneak through by carrying buckets or using Occultation Paint. In the factory's chambers they encounter several ghosts, and discover that Magpie, a voice the Cracked Cup Knight recognizes, has been bottled here and begs for oblivion. They press on to the Dome of the Written Stump, where Hemlock - a vast, still-individuated ghost managing a chaotic "clowder" of other spirits - speaks to them. Hemlock describes how ghosts are lured or captured, stripped of desire and motive by "The Overseer," and processed for sale in necromancy.

Their audience is cut short by the arrival of The Overseer, a lich built on spider legs. The pair flee through The Bracken Flue, a drainage shaft for defective ghosts, with the lich in pursuit. They cross one of three perilous bridges and emerge into a limestone basin. There, briefly, the ghost of the Blue Lady's dead daughter appears before the statue of a luthier. The daughter's specter radiates accusation at her mother before vanishing, leaving the Lady silent and the knight uncertain how much of what he perceived was his own feeling versus the ghost's.

## Part 9 - The Soul-Sense

Back on the Road of Graves, the Cracked Cup Knight collapses in a sudden explosion of sensory agony - every sense firing as fire - then snaps into a hallucination of the Road five thousand years in the past, during the era of the demon kings. He fights through waves of demon-slaves and defeats an effigy of the Red King before snapping back to consciousness under an oak, where the Blue Lady has pulled him from the road and is holding his hands. She has already used her enchanted necklace to call Othelmedir, the family mancer, for help.

The knight's consciousness is pulled into a featureless stone room where Othelmedir delivers a diagnosis: the soul-sense tether is broken. The knight's body, a "cracked teacup" from too many deaths and revivals, can no longer bridge soul to sense. The soul is intact but the sense is emaciated, and the condition is beyond necromancy's repair. Othelmedir's only suggestion is to find an unclaimed sense on the Road, then vanishes - with the pointed warning that the Lady will owe him a debt for answering her call.

The knight descends beneath the Road of Graves, moving through a mirror space populated by the Buried facing downward. Pursued by the Red King, he is enveloped by a mist creature and, with no other option, inhales it. He snaps back awake under the oak. His senses have returned, but they are subtly foreign - the air tastes wrong, the temperature is off for summer. He has absorbed another's sense, not recovered his own. The Blue Lady returns to his side. He rests, then rises, buckles on his sword, and feels the Steel ready. The chapter ends without resolution of what the new sense means for his identity.

## Part 10 - The Duel

The Knight arrives at Surveillance's Triumph, a monumental copper golem frozen mid-stomp over the skeleton of a slain Demon King in Middlemoss. There, he faces Wallcrest, a scarless Knight of the Leather Dragon, in a formal duel. The knight is precise and difficult to wound; the Knight ultimately wins by deliberately accepting a deep cut to open his opponent's guard. Afterward, at a mossy hollow behind a waterfall, the pair are approached by Skimcalf, a young man in wicker sandals who confesses his attraction to the Blue Lady's voice and challenges the knight out of misplaced honor. Easily bested without lethal force, Skimcalf pivots from humiliation to petition: he has a duel of his own in five days and begs to be taught. He hints that he may know something of the Blue Lady's missing stepson, and so she permits the arrangement.

The knight trains Skimcalf across four days in stances, legwork, binding, and attack combinations. The boy improves, though his guard remains slow and his temperament resists creating distance. On the final night, Skimcalf admits he has no actual knowledge of the stepson, and the Blue Lady, who has seen her dead daughter's ghost haunting the boy's shoulder throughout the day, dismisses him coldly. The next morning, amid the Deep Jain summer festival, the Knight and his Lady fight through dancing crowds and sensory chaos to reach Surveillance's Triumph in time to witness the duel's end. Skimcalf, applying the lesson of distance, almost survives, but his opponent is R the Killer, who throws his buckler to stagger the boy and then tears through his neck with a morningstar. As Skimcalf dies in the Knight's arms, apologizing for his lie, the Demon King's skeleton consumes his body. R the Killer offers no apology.

## Part 11 - The Temple of Lady Horsehair

Seeking information about her lost stepson, the Blue Lady leads the Knight to the Emerald House, a temple carved into Middlemoss's cliff face, ostensibly an art gallery serving the necromancer-goddess Lady Horsehair. The reality is grimmer: the temple's priestesses collect the souls of supplicants by having them paint their own self-portraits, which then serve as phylacteries. Upon arrival, the Lady is brought before an easel in the cylindrical sanctorium and asked by six priestesses to paint her own face in exchange for news of her stepson. She begins. The Knight watches her vitality drain into the canvas and moves to intervene. The priestesses threaten divine retribution if he interferes.

He intervenes anyway, cleaving the paint palette in two. A wax golem beats him across the chamber and he is separated from his Lady, locked in a lower exhibition hall filled with faded soul-portrait phylacteries. There he meets two figures: The Late Poet, a skeleton imprisoned years prior, who explains the trap the Lady has already walked into; and Squide, a hunched, stuttering chemist hiding in a pillar shelf, who reveals that he created an acid called the Unhue capable of breaking the soul-binding on a completed portrait. The Knight then defeats a mimic disguised as a painting, discovers a hidden stair leading to a rooftop garden, and re-enters the temple by stealth or force to locate the Unhue on a third-floor shelf.

Returning to the sanctorium, he finds the Lady slumped and drained beside her completed portrait. The six priestesses send two massive painted wax golems against him. He destroys both through sustained attrition. The priestesses, screaming from the balcony, fall with the second golem. He pours the Unhue over the portrait, the Lady's color returns, and he carries her out through the garden and up a wisteria trellis. The chapter closes with the Knight praying that Lady Horsehair neither saw his face nor learned his name.

## Part 12 - The Hypnotist of Yellowmoor

The Knight begins suffering recurring nightmares: a Road sinking into muck, a forest of honey locust trees with black thorns he compulsively bites, pain building under his skin. His Blue Lady recognizes the nightmares as a divine curse and promises to find its source. Their pilgrimages through the Middlemoss heath to four necromancer-god shrines yield nothing. A cryptically-speaking old man with two canes redirects them to Yellowmoor Manor, a sprawling, architecturally chaotic building on a mustard-colored heath slope, haunted for decades by rumors of people who began hearing its carillon chime in their dreams and then disappeared.

The pair are made to wait a full week before the hypnotist will receive them. The nightmares worsen progressively and begin bleeding into waking reality. When a tattooed servant finally arrives to escort them, the Blue Lady insists on accompanying the Knight; they ride a two-decked wagon to the manor. At the door, the old man from the heath reveals himself as the hypnotist. His wink triggers a hypnotic collapse, and the Knight falls into The Trance of Yellowmoor: a series of looping dream states involving beetle-scarabs, a road walked in reverse, a forbidden perception of his Lady, and a moonlit void. The Blue Lady's voice, repeating four sentences, provides the anchor and the means of navigating specific trances by parsing keyword phrases.

The Knight breaks the trance by descending an inverted underground campanile and destroying the silver chime at its bottom. He wakes in a dusty attic with the Lady, then navigates through the manor's thirty-seven floors to the foyer below, pursued by the hypnotist's dry laughter. The final battle runs three stages: four emotional-void thralls, then the Knight's own shadow-self made animate and covered in thorns, then the hypnotist himself encased in a hedge of frozen ice. The Knight pushes blind through the cold and laughter and drives his sword forward. The hypnotist dies. The trance ends. The chapter closes on an uneasy note: the Knight glances back to confirm his shadow has returned, then thinks he hears one last chime.

## Part 13 - The Tower of Silence

The chapter opens in Middlemoss, where the Knight watches from a boarding house as the pre-dawn streets fill with pedestrians. A procession of monks from the Tower of Silence appears, and in the chaos the Blue Lady, who stepped out alone, is seized from a bridge and dragged away. The knight gives chase but the monks vanish into the tunnels burrowing through the ravine walls.

Navigating the dense catacomb suburb of Deep Compost, the knight must obtain information about the Tower's location, either by bribing a crooked guide named Valance with a found treasure, or by paying the haruspex Joole for a spiritual consultation. The path leads to a hidden cavern containing the oil-black Tower of Silence, home to the Monks of the Lidless. The killer-for-hire R, who has his own interest in a relic mirror hidden inside, offers an alternate route through a wine crypt below the Tower.

The pair infiltrate through tunnels used to house outcast blind monks who have gorged for years on a putrid ceremonial wine. A series of brutal fights ensues: first against scattered blind monks in their sleeping quarters, then against a charging group of five, and finally against the Senseless King and Queen, two undead monarchs guarding the wine crypt's inner chamber. With those obstacles cleared, the knight and R ascend into the Tower proper.

Moving carefully through silk-carpeted corridors, the knight frees the Blue Lady from a shackled theater seat. Their escape is immediately interrupted by a Will-O-Wisp, a lethal incorporeal spirit they can only endure until it burns itself out. After regrouping, the three must navigate upward through the Tower toward the owlery. En route, R retrieves his mirror.

At the summit, the final obstacle is the Lidless, a half-demon mutated spotted owl with a face that blinds and lacerates with thrown timber. The knight and R defeat it by luring the creature beneath a loosened beam and crushing it. The chapter ends as the three escape via a skybridge onto the open heath beyond Middlemoss.

## Part 14 - The Old Soulwood

The chapter alternates between two threads: the knight navigating The Old Soulwood alone after being separated from the Blue Lady, and the Lady herself sheltering through the night with a massive Periwinkle Forest Cat. Both threads are rendered in intimate internal monologue, the Lady speaking aloud to the cat, the knight guided by conscience and oath.

After fleeing Middlemoss east, the party enters the ancient Soulwood, a forest grown from juniper berries sewn by a demon king's concubine-witch directly into Road of Graves epitaphs. The forest has swallowed the Road entirely. While the knight is momentarily transfixed by a strange music-light in a hollow log, the Lady vanishes and the log closes behind him without exit.

Separated in a driving rainstorm, the knight finds an archway of living wood leading to a protected cypress glade. There, he encounters The Tree with the Face of a Lover, a sovereign tree whose warm root-tendrils attempt seduction. The test is one of loyalty: the knight must permit the roots full contact without surrendering his devotion to his Lady. He passes. The Tree gifts him a black fox as a guide.

Following the fox into the Underpass, the root-carved tunnels beneath the forest floor, the knight navigates or fights his way past prowling Periwinkle Forest Cats, culminating in a harder fight against Spekboom, an alpha-cat with an acacia bush fused to its skull. Emerging into a moonlit clearing, he confronts Lampagogue, a sentient pool that manufactures Will-O-Wisps and forces the knight to fight one of three chosen forms before proceeding.

The chapter closes as the fox guide disappears at a leaning log shelter and the Lady's host, the mother Periwinkle Forest Cat, attacks. The knight is forced to kill her. The Lady emerges from the den, distressed by the carnage. They reunite and walk four days and three nights east, until the Soulwood ends and a new, impossibly tall forest of arch trees rises ahead.

## Part 15 - The Canopy of the Ents

The chapter opens at the base of one of the colossal Ent trees, the ancient living arch-trees that pierce the clouds. The knight and his Lady climb the rugged outer bark, hacking through dense, living thicket with sword and shield while a white mist rises fast from The Old Soulwood below, stinging the Lady's throat and pressing them upward. They break through the canopy into sunlight, standing above the clouds on a swaying platform of woven branches, the mist dissolving in the sun below.

After a period of rest on the canopy, the Ent signals dusk by trembling. The pair cross to a neighboring pine-Ent with strangely calcified, grey leaves. It is still and cathedral-quiet, clearly dying or dead. Inside its hollow trunk, moonlight fills a central chamber where they encounter the Late Poet again, a wandering Living Bones searching for his lost star. The player determines his fate with a silent judgment.

Descending deeper through the hollow trunk, the atmosphere becomes physically oppressive, the thumping of the Ent's diseased heart growing from a tick to a full-body percussion. The knight notices his hands are briefly wrong, bent backward, before correcting themselves with smoky marks where nails were. The Blue Lady notices nothing and continues walking.

At the core of the tree, the Four-Pronged Heart chamber reveals a dying stag head suspended at the apex, its corrupted right eye capable of petrification, and a chitinous man-insect servant of the heart guarding a stolen Ent's eye. The knight uses a previously obtained Left Eye of an Ent to redirect the stag's gaze, fights the insect-man to steal his gauntlets, climbs to the apex, and removes the stag's remaining eye. The heart dies and the tree collapses in a rain of sawdust.

The pair awaken in a normal, quiet forest, the towering Ents and dense Soulwood entirely gone. Guided by silent sap miners, they emerge onto a rocky tundra with distant mountains, where the Road of Graves reappears, winding through the washed-out grass. The chapter ends with the Lady remarking, gently, that the fallen tree may have outlived its seeds.

## Part 16 - The Tunnel of the Stillbreather

The knight and the Blue Lady traverse the high passes of the Meathook Mountains, pressing toward a tunnel on The Road of Graves. The journey opens quietly: the Lady confesses she glimpsed what she thought was her daughter's ghost in the glacial light, then admits it was only a trick of reflection. Her speech, the knight notes, has grown clearer since their time in the Tower of Silence.

En route, they encounter a homunculus called Hueless, riding a prehistoric Fimbulwolf named Kirk. Hueless offers to guide them to The Road in exchange for a favor: kill The Stillbreather, master of the tunnel ahead. The knight accepts.

The tunnel is a labyrinth of green-glowing ice, haunted by hallucinations and disorienting passages. Scattered throughout are pages from a warrior's journal, written roughly twenty years prior, describing that man's own siege against The Stillbreather and his servants. The Lady reads a page bearing the symbol of a Pinecone Crest and quietly concludes the journal's author must have been her stepson.

The tunnel's final chamber contains an underground castle. In its crypt, the knight locates the Stillbreather: a preserved, lipless figure lying in an open sarcophagus, breathing green-tinged air. Combat unfolds across the crypt's many stone boxes, with the knight fending off waves of firebeetles while searching for the leather egg in which The Stillbreather's breath is hidden. When the knight finally ruptures the egg, the Stillbreather's animate power dies.

The chapter closes under an aurora-lit mountain sky. The Lady, reflecting on the dream-like quality of what passed inside, reads the final journal page aloud. She confirms her conviction: the journal's warrior was family. Hueless and Kirk watch from the dark hillside as the pair chart their next course toward the distant city of Hornwater.

## Part 17 - The Coffin Rollers

Caught in a lethal bent squall east of the Meathooks, the knight, the Blue Lady, and their road-companion R the Killer take shelter inside a caravan of Coffin Rollers: itinerant freaks, mancers, and outcasts who roll the length of The Road performing circuses and, less openly, grave-robbing and smuggling. A warty gawper welcomes them in and offers a tour.

The welcome sours fast. The Rollers box the party in, and a fight through two furred enforcers drives the trio into the caravan's enormous Silk Maze. Room by room they fight their way through waiting gawpers and Wax Golems. Inside, they encounter Bonnie and Butter, a pair of eerily resilient twin girls who speak in overlapping, circular dialogue. The twins cryptically invoke their master: Patchwork, also called The Thin Man, and claim he would bring them back from death regardless.

R is eventually captured. The knight is bound to a chair in the Chemical Show wagon, where The Contortionist, a surgeon-alchemist with an impossible knot of limbs, begins preparing to make the knight into a golem. The knight uses his serrated armor couters to saw through R's bonds. R tears free, kills The Contortionist in brutal close quarters, and the two escape.

The Lady has been taken to Yellowrock Way, a glass-roofed corridor that warps space. There they face Patchwork himself: a man stitched from many skins, who can assume the appearance of a necromancer-god through fume magic. The fight is staged across three escalating phases, ending when the knight drives his sword through the loose seam at the base of Patchwork's foot, deflating the swollen master. The Strongman falls to R. The twins die a second time. The Lady wakes, alive.

The chapter closes with the knight reflecting that he hopes never to survive a fight so close to death again.

## Part 18 - The Harridan's Moon

Under a full, jaundiced Harridan's Moon, the knight carries his Blue Lady across a dark moor. She has been bitten by a Nickel Beetle, a creature whose venom is known to be fatal, and her body is failing. The Lady, composed despite her condition, tells the knight his service is finished. He ignores her.

A light appears across the moor: a squat hut, occupied by an ancient, wart-faced woman who immediately recognizes the venom and promises a cure. She demands the knight lay down his sword before she will treat the Lady. After brief internal conflict, he complies, though he keeps his Pinecone Crest shield strapped to his arm.

The old woman assigns the knight a task as payment: one of three dangerous errands inside or around the hut. Upon completion, she instructs him to fetch silver water from a pool beneath a leafless elm. When he bends over the pool, the hut rises on two giant goat-legs and kicks him into the water.

He crawls out. The hut, the witch, and his Lady are gone. He tracks the hut's goat-prints across the moor through cold and dark, fighting off a pack of Spider Wolves with only his shield, his sword still held by the witch. He eventually finds the hut again on a hill, its interior transformed. Inside, the old woman has shed her human shape and stands over the Lady in stasis: a massive, shaggy, long-armed beast, his own sword in her claw, preparing to take the Lady's body as her own.

The knight fights the witch-beast with a fire-poker, dodging her stolen sword. When she begins uttering curses, he spears her tongue with the poker to interrupt, retrieves his sword, and severs her head. The Lady's trance breaks instantly. She rises, restored.

## Part 19 - The Alsoburg Siege

The knight and his Blue Lady descend by lift into the underground cavern of Spring-Under-Fjall, their passage interrupted by hissing gas vents that fragment the lift operator's account of the cave's history. Upon arrival at Station Archer, they plunge directly into a pitched battle: the engineer-knights of the station are being overrun by golems controlled by a force called The Crawling Silk. The knight fights to rescue as many of the station's defenders as possible before the station falls.

Cornered inside a pressure tower, the survivors plan an escape through the sewer system while defending both the tower's control panel and the engineers cutting through to the drain. The control panel becomes a weapon, as the knight can selectively rupture pipes with scalding spring water to melt the golems' waxen frames. The situation deteriorates when The Crawling Silk itself appears: a once-human mancer cursed to eternal crawling madness, its deadspeech chanting causing the air to freeze and the molten wax to thicken. The knight extracts the Lady and the surviving engineers through the drain before the tower is fully breached.

Emerging by the underground lake, the group marches for the undercity of Alsoburg, choosing one of three hazardous routes across the cavern floor. On the approach, they ambush a patrol of Hides, skin-clad servants of The Crawling Silk, and use their flayed robes to infiltrate the golem army marching into the already-fallen city.

Inside Alsoburg, the knight gets close enough to The Crawling Silk to strike it, drawing the entity's attention and being pulled into a frozen dream-combat. After wounding it and breaking out of the dream, the knight forces four golems to shatter The Crawling Silk's defensive cocoon through a calculated bait, then executes the creature. With the threat ended, a local cobbler confirms he saw the Lady's stepson pass through Alsoburg long ago, heading east toward Hornwater.

## Part 20 - The Scarab Mine

The knight and Blue Lady arrive at the Scarab Mine, a lead-rich quarry steeped in accumulated dead souls. A Compass mancer warns them against entering, but the Lady's stepson lies somewhere east, and the mine offers a shorter route than the desert or woodland alternatives. Before they enter, a necromantic remote contact comes through a hanged man in a nearby tree: Othelmedir, a mancer from the knight's past, requests they shatter a specific red scarab on a white stone shelf in the Stope of the Ninth Ozone. In exchange, all the Lady's debts to him are cancelled, and a further reward awaits.

Inside the mine the chapter is structured around a relentless pursuit. An entity called The Alien Knight, accompanied by seven ghosts, hunts the pair through the mine's layered tunnels and galleries. The knight cannot look directly at this foe: a blind-spot necromancy conceals his center from unshielded eyes. After a desperate flight, the two take shelter behind a lead curtain that holds the ghosts at bay temporarily. A journal left by a prior Compass mancer who walked the same path provides fragmentary directions through the mine's interior.

The pair are driven to a river of wooden scarabs, each containing a soul. The Alien Knight and his ghosts will not enter it. The knight and Lady wade across, the scarabs rising to chin height, their polished shells finding every gap in armor. The Lady emerges wounded on the far bank.

They shelter at Toke's Crossroads while the Lady recovers and the knight searches the stairway niches, locating a Ring of Perfect Leather and using the mancer's journal as a guide to the Stope of the Ninth Ozone. There the knight shatters Othelmedir's ancestor scarab as promised. Nearby is the Shelley Stone: a relic that, placed once on the throat of someone not long dead, will return them to life. The Alien Knight attacks again in this final chamber. The knight discovers that closing one eye bypasses the blind-spot necromancy, making his enemy fully visible, and defeats him. The chain cage arrives; the pair ascend and emerge onto the eastern mountainside, The Road visible through the web of a lone dead tree.

## Part 21 - The Stone Cocoon

The chapter opens with a historical prologue: three thousand years prior, a demon king ruled this barren plain, until seven necromancer-gods destroyed the demon kingdoms and raised the dead from the despair of the dying. What remains now is the giant half-buried skeleton of one such demon king, lodged in a lone hill over an otherwise flat, snowswept plain. The land carries a residue of hallucination, dangerous to travelers crossing it in winter storms.

The knight and Blue Lady are caught in exactly such a storm. The Lady is badly affected by the visions, seeing faces of the dead she recognizes among the snowdrifts. The knight, more resistant but not immune, must navigate the hallucinations while keeping the Lady moving. They locate shelter: a cave mouth beneath the arching jaw of the demon king's buried skull, warmed by a deep hot spring. But inside, a Walker of Whiteeye, maddened by days or years of wandering the fume-filled ruins, attacks with an iron needle. The knight subdues him.

While dragging the body deeper into the tunnel, the knight discovers a silk carpet and, in the same moment, loses the Lady: she flees deeper into the cave, chased by her visions. The knight follows through a labyrinth of frozen chambers, fighting various hallucinations, until he finds the Lady in what appears to be the chapel of their ruined home. The daughter-ghost of the Lady's estranged daughter confronts them there, a silent accusation of maternal neglect. The knight cuts the ghost down and pulls the Lady away.

They arrive at a warm grotto around a glowing hot spring, the blue mushroom carpet lighting the cave. The Lady, undone by cold and grief and the visions of her daughter, turns to the knight and asks him to remove his helmet and take her hands. The knight refuses, upholding his oath, though the cost is evident in the Lady's wounded withdrawal. As they wait for the storm to pass, the pool grows still and cold, signaling that the cave is now freezing. They navigate back out through rapidly forming ice-walls, emerging into a cleared snowscape. The Lady speaks of the glass orchid her lord once gave her, left behind when they fled home, and questions what hope remains in finding a stepson she drove away. They find a small tundra shrine with paper scraps naming nearby villages, choose a direction, and return to the Road.

## Part 22 - The Sisterhood of the Yellow Smile

The knight enters the Buried Forest along the Road of Graves and cuts down three stonecats - grave robbers prying up epitaph stones. Before the bodies cool, a necromancer-driven Gonging Hearse closes in on ten animated legs. R the Killer, a mercenary, appears from the trees and joins the fight; together they destroy the construct and its singing driver, whose deadspeech had been continuously raising the slain stonecats. Afterward, R collects his coin from Hayz, the shrine mancer at the Scattered Shrine beside Hornwater Lake, and departs - the narrator noting grimly that the knight will never see him alive again.

The knight, in search of his Blue Lady, gondolas into the canals of Hornwater. After fruitless hours searching the streets under a vow of silence, a noblewoman with white hair and amber eyes - the Guildlady of Burning Shells - stops her coach. She recognizes the knight's pinecone crest and invites him to the Palace. There, the Guildlord offers him a position as weaponmaster, asking only to meet the Lady first.

Returning from the Palace at dusk, the knight receives a letter sealed in yellow wax: the Sisterhood of the Yellow Smile informs him his Blue Lady has voluntarily chosen burial at their priory, and invites him to witness her interment. He races through the Vekelhause Marina - fending off bow-wielding Gasps lurking beneath the water - and reaches the priory. The prioress, speaking with coercive witchery, drives him bodily from the gate despite his pleas.

The knight makes a choice: he returns through the priory's opposite entrance and fights his way through sisters and four increasingly dangerous glass illusions. He digs his Lady free from her coffin with a shovel. She tearfully confesses she lost faith in their quest and sought burial as a form of atonement. The chapter closes with the knight having broken his oath to the Road, his soul unmoored, dawn rising over the Scattered Shrine.

## Part 23 - The Weaponmaster

Fleeing at dawn through Hornwater's canal district with his Blue Lady, the knight is ambushed by assassins of the Guild of Long Knives in the narrow lanes beside Canal Raydwurst. He cuts down four attackers and engineers an escape before surfacing in the weaving quarter.

There, among laborers and loom-workers, the knight spots a figure whose strangely rebuilt face stirs buried memory: his old Weaponmaster, the man who trained him in Clan Fathmail's courtyard. The Lady recognizes him too, but distrusts the convenient timing. The man attaches himself to them, speaking in the remembered clipped tones of the master, and leads the knight through three Hornwater locations - an arena arcade, a skylit hall, a pond house - searching for clues to his own sworn Lady's whereabouts.

The trail leads into the Channelloft, a labyrinthine tower of wastewater tunnels and squatters. Long Knives pour in behind them. Separated from the knight, the doppelganger weaponmaster drags the Blue Lady into a park. When the knight fights free and follows the blood trail, he finds his Lady tied to a Long Knife corpse with the false master's sword at her throat.

The confrontation reveals a soul cracked by too many resurrections. The weaponmaster's necromantic tissue ignites with wrath mid-duel, shattering the knight's Pinecone Crest Shield and transforming the man into a Shambler - a mass of living tissue and dying spirit. The knight lures it into the grove's redbud trees, goading it into getting its sword lodged in the wood until he can deliver a clean killing blow. The master's body disintegrates.

Untying his Lady from the corpse, the knight hears her final words on this thread: during her captivity, she saw him - her stepson, the child she drove from her Lord's house. She knows where he is.

## Part 24 - The Court of the Leather Dragon

The knight and his Blue Lady, Lady Odette, paddle by gondola to a fortified island on Hornwater Lake - the seat of the Leather Dragon, a guild of elite warriors whose gargoyles are petrified basilisks. As they near the shore, Odette reveals the full weight of what she kept hidden: the boy she drove from her late Lord's house, and prayed would find peace, is now the Great Wyrm Grauf, master of one of Hornwater's most feared martial guilds.

They are captured on the beach without resistance - the knight surrenders rather than be cut down uselessly. Brought before Grauf in the Swidden of the First Wyrm, where five hundred foot knights, three hundred horse knights, and three hundred arbalests drill for imminent war against Hornwater's other guilds, Odette speaks aloud and claims kinship. Grauf's tactician calls for their beheading. Grauf refuses, on grounds of guild honor, and instead takes them to the Afthimmel, the keep's high tower, for a formal duel: the knight as Odette's champion against the Great Wyrm himself.

The duel is brutal and asymmetric. Grauf's enchanted leather armor is impenetrable; the knight fights without his shattered shield, parrying blind. A deep cut opens the knight's waist. He fights through it, wearing Grauf down on endurance until a narrow opening appears - the neck beneath the helmet. He takes it.

Grauf falls. The tower dissolves, and the three of them are transported by some dead-soul wind to the shore of Hornwater Lake, beside the Road of Graves. The knight, bleeding out, refuses the Shelley Stone that could revive him. Odette removes his helmet - finally seeing his face - and kisses him. He dies.

The chapter closes in three quiet codas: a sunrise over the ruined Dragon keep; Odette walking a field road beside a cart, the scarred Grauf leading the ox; and a Polished Steel Longsword resting against an oak root, touching a stone on the Road of Graves.
