# Weirmoth - Character Analysis

## Description

Weirmoth is a wiry, compact man in his mid-thirties, a road-worn Cillian Murphy. Brownish hair, distinct sideburns to the jaw, the shadow of a half-grown mutton-chop on his upper lip. A head shorter than most men, two heads shorter than Othelmedir. Cloak patched from old funeral shrouds, seams running the wrong way for ordinary tailoring. Signature spear. In an inner pocket he keeps Mister Ribbon, a preserved canary in a wire cage, whom he talks to as a confidant.

His one incongruity: immaculate hands, the nails of a notary on the body of a grave-carter. He carries a sliver of fat-soap and washes at any lull, even in combat.

## Personality

A chipper, self-mythologizing picaro: he banters, brags, and spins a saint's-tale around every object he owns. He claims he never dreams. He lies. The patter conceals a quiet warmth that surfaces in single lines, most often addressed to Mister Ribbon.

Beneath it, a fair-bargainer. He lives by the same barter logic Othelmedir does: service for service, accounts settled. He measures a man by whether the deal was held, not by who the man was. A strange, unsought mirror of Othelmedir, and one Othelmedir consistently underestimates.

In a fight, clean and unflashy with the spear. In chrypt arcana, lost, and admits it.

*He was once a seminarian of a minor road-cult; he washed out before ordination and never speaks of it. The shroud-cloak is his old vestment. Mister Ribbon was his augury bird. Othelmedir's deadspeech does not unsettle him because he was almost trained in it.*
