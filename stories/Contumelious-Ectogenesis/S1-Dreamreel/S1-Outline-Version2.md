# "Dreamreel" - Full Outline

## Premise

*Part of "Othelmedir's Black Scholastics" in the Time of Dying setting.*

In the Road-village of *Sulky Wend*, near a sealed chrypt called *The Tower of the Attercop*, residents dream the death-visions of an old cabal. They call it 'The Dreamreel'. Othelmedir built it. Years ago, under a different name, he fled the Tower when a rival cabal breached it, and his circle released a necromantic gas to buy time, sealing everyone inside as dreaming undead. Now he hires a sellsword, Weirmoth, and returns to destroy the cauldron before the villagers' memories are fully replaced. Problem: the gas preserved his old colleagues too, and they wake when he enters.

## Plot

**1. Arrival in Sulky Wend.** Othelmedir enters 'The Hooded Pillow', the village boarding house. Residents are sullen, sleep-starved. A sleepwalking child recites a funeral rite she cannot know: 'The Little Mass of Failing Lamps'. (*`lemma` blob*). The local bedmeister is occupied with another client. Othelmedir notes he lived near Sulky Wend before but rarely visited the town. His reflection does not elaborate.
- (Othelmedir) Tall as a redwood, thin as a reed; iron eyes, broad dark face, cropped curls, salt-and-pepper beard
- (Othelmedir) Oxblood velvet cloak; full white prosthetic left arm hangs lifeless
- (Othelmedir) Sees residents as material: notes a strong neck, clean teeth on a boy
- (lore) The boarding house once housed Tower staff; its ledger is still bound in attercop silk, a former gift of the tower's mancers
- (withheld) The significance of that specific funeral rite, and whose death it marks

**2. Teammates.** Bedmeister Noseman Under Eaves describes the Dreamreel to Weirmoth: like a nagging worry at sleep's edge; death-visions crowd out all other dreams. He begs for help. Othelmedir, having overheard, presents himself as a dream-mancy specialist. Weirmoth accepts without probing and takes the bedmeister's coin. They agree to ride at dawn.
- (Othelmedir) Introduces himself in precise, formal speech; fronts as a wandering scholar
- (Othelmedir) Lets Weirmoth and Noseman set terms first; states he works by barter and oath, not coin
- (Weirmoth) Roadman/grave-carter by trade; bluffs the mystic work
- (Weirmoth) Chipper, self-mythologizing picaro; banters with the bedmeister
- (Weirmoth) Cloak patched from salvaged funeral shrouds
- (Weirmoth) Spins a fabricated origin myth for his spear: 'It once belonged to a saint who lanced abscesses on a poisoned bishop's throat; it now whistles whenever sickness is near'
- (Weirmoth) Brags he "never dreams" (irony setup for scene 3)
- (lore) Dream-mancy is a dead specialism; no licensed practitioners remain
- (withheld) Noseman Under knows or suspects Othelmedir's identity but does not tell Weirmoth

**3. The Seal of Neinstitch.** The two approach the Tower on its bluff. (*`essay` or `memorial` on Road of Graves epitaphs along the way*). Weirmoth admits he slept poorly. Othelmedir disables the seal: "With a sigh came loose the sutures ('Öltesoldzo sjhaj')" (*`verse` blob*). A visible gas pours forth. They wait for the fume to dissipate. Weirmoth speaks of his background. (*`interview` blob*)
- (Othelmedir) Unsealing is modest: whispered deadspeech against the lock; imply the real strength is knowledge
- (Othelmedir) Carries no visible weapon; Weirmoth notes this uneasily
- Othelmedir explains: if the death-visions continue, villagers' memories will be wholly replaced by those of the dead
- Cobwebs quiver; spiders stir
- (Weirmoth) Slept poorly, contradicting his "never dreams" brag
- (Weirmoth) Hidden warmth surfaces under the banter during the interview
- (Weirmoth) Pulls a preserved canary from his cloak: 'Mister Ribbon'; talks to it like a confidant
- (lore) The two cabals named: tower cabal 'The Red Pew'; invader cabal 'Milkmoot'

**4. The Waking Halls.** Outer chambers. Preserved invaders rouse and attack. Weirmoth notes Othelmedir seems to know every corridor. They encounter a muscle-wall: flesh woven over the passage to the lower sanctums, blocking both further gas release and further progress.
- Gas pools knee-deep in low corners, pale green, smelling of rosewater and spoiled meat
- Every surface wears a velvet patina of spider silk; footsteps leave dark bruises
- Invaders: mail rusted into flesh, mouths working silent breach-orders: "No quarter for the elder."
- A lectern with three books open to the same page: 'On the Conduct of Sealed Houses', 'Compendium of Threshold Speech', 'A Practitioner's Almanac'
- Low sweet rot like overripe peaches, undercut by burnt hair and old wax
- A preserved ledger nailed to a column; most duty-hours long past
- One invader wears a pendant (hammered tin; lidded cauldron sigil, single open eye on lid, flat-tear shape); Othelmedir pockets it
- (Othelmedir) Internal line appraising which tissues survived the gas best; never reaches for a weapon
- Othelmedir straightens a crooked wall sconce without looking (old habit from nightly rounds)
- (Weirmoth) Spear-work clean and unflashy; banter thins by the third corpse; canary goes silent
- (lore) Rival mancer Ostrobone the Rook led the breach wearing a mask of hornet husks
- (withheld) The name the cabal used for Othelmedir, and why the corpses murmur it

**5. The Scriptorium.** They breach a library of rotted codices. Othelmedir retrieves a treatise he helped author. He does not say so.
- Othelmedir explains the gas and the cauldron: elements seep outward even though the cauldron is stoppered, producing the Dreamreel
- (Othelmedir) Loses minutes absorbed in a treatise; retrieves 'Potions and Poisons of Eleven Reagents' without claiming authorship
- (Othelmedir) Internal: these texts are worth more than the village, certainly more than Weirmoth
- Three floors with open central balcony overlooking a dead garden; gravestone plots for former cabal members (*`memorial` blob*); false sun globe dead and dim
- Books moldering, covered in a verdigris patina from the gas

**6. The Still-Room.** Weirmoth kills the guardian dead-dreamer. They begin brewing a poison to breach the muscle-wall. Othelmedir knows ten of eleven reagents: Widowwine, Priest's Quicklime, Honey, Green from the Blades of Grass, Black Lye, Glasswort, Crystalwort, Iron shavings, Dandelion root, Preserved nine-day-gnats. The eleventh, Respiration from a Silver Dream, must be retrieved from a dream-memory. Othelmedir has brewed a second potion to enter that memory. Only one unhabituated to necromancy can drink it safely. Weirmoth qualifies.
- Various new alchemical ingredients; Othelmedir takes some, making Weirmoth suspicious
- (Othelmedir) Pockets reagents like restocking a pantry, unbothered by Weirmoth's stare
- Weirmoth begins to distrust Othelmedir; Othelmedir uses deception to get him to drink (*`interview` blob*)
  - The Generic-Witness lie: Othelmedir claims the potion shows only the first death the gas preserved, an anonymous invader's. He claims any practitioner who drinks it would burn to ash (true). He omits that the dream Weirmoth will inherit is his own.
- (Othelmedir) Frames the potion as a fair exchange; speaks in precise, formal cadence
- (Weirmoth) Drops the patter for one quiet line before drinking; the picaro mask slips

**7. The Dreamer and the Watcher.** Weirmoth drinks and goes under. Othelmedir stands over him, listening to the mercenary murmur the names of tower cabal members. Othelmedir's internal monologue: the night of the breach; his hand on the lever; the colleagues he sealed inside.
- (Internal monologue) Weirmoth relives the attack through Othelmedir's eyes: cabal combat, the order to release the gas, Othelmedir pulling the lever
- (Othelmedir) Archaic, formal, self-mythologizing register; cerebral, measured, no grief; treats the act as cowardice, not crime
- While Weirmoth sleeps, Othelmedir brews another potion (foreshadow resolution)

**8. The Descent to the Cauldron.** Othelmedir has already brewed the breach potion and destroyed the muscle-wall by the time Weirmoth wakes. They descend toward the cauldron, fighting through members of both cabals. Othelmedir's old identity is named aloud. Vicious, precise fighting between the reanimating cabals and the two protagonists. Scene ends at the Cauldron Chamber entrance.
- Dining hall: food rotten and desiccate, chairs overturned, candles burnt to stubs
- They pass the ritual circle where the tower cabal formerly performed necromancy
- Two named undead: Fellow Sourbell and Fellow Alder the Lank reveal Othelmedir's betrayal and old name: 'Fellow Liddick'; Weirmoth helps slay them

**9. Resolution.** The Cauldron Chamber. Before Othelmedir speaks, Weirmoth says he will not interfere. Othelmedir empties 'The Lidd Decoction' into the cauldron; it hisses; he intones deadspeech over it until it resolves to muck. Weirmoth has his spear ready when Othelmedir turns. Othelmedir argues: his cabal committed no evil act; the townfolk's plight stems from the rivals' breach; he kept the secret to avoid persecution from ignorance; his only guilt is the original cowardice, now rectified. Weirmoth assents, but forces Othelmedir to surrender the stolen reagents. Othelmedir agrees and hands them over. He keeps the cult pendant he pocketed earlier.
- (Othelmedir) Yields the reagents (bargain names them); keeps the pendant (bargain did not)
- (Othelmedir) Closing speech in formal, archaic cadence; not pleading, stating terms
