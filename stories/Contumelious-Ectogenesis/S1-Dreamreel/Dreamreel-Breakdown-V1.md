# Dreamreel

## 1. ESSAY
- Frame the village of Sulky Wend, sleep-starved, the boarding house
- Establish the tall, prosthetic-armed scholar entering The Hooded Pillow
- Othelmedir's gaze: residents catalogued as material, strong necks, clean teeth
- Reflect obliquely on having lived nearby, never elaborating
- Note the ledger bound in attercop silk; scholar recognizes the gift
- Dreamreel introduced as rumor first, not yet named

## 2. LEMMA — The Little Mass of Failing Lamps
- Encyclopedia entry: a funeral rite performed for sealed-house dead
- Origin among the Tower mancers; rare outside the cabals
- Liturgical structure, the lamp imagery, the closing antiphon
- Note: a sleepwalking child reciting it is a known omen of the Dreamreel
- Anchors why Othelmedir twitches when the child speaks

## 3. ESSAY
- Othelmedir's reflection on what the rite means, who it marks
- Refuses to answer his own question, withholds the name
- Discursive aside on memory-replacement: the sleeper losing themselves
- Hedges, addresses the reader: "you have heard, perhaps, of such houses"
- Sets up the bedmeister's negotiation across the room

## 4. INTERVIEW (3) — Noseman Under Eaves, bedmeister at The Hooded Pillow
- Q: the trouble here? / A: residents dream the deaths of strangers
- Q: how does it feel? / A: like a worry one cannot put down before sleep
- Q: who can help? / A: any specialist; he begs Weirmoth, then Othelmedir

## 5. VERSE
- Dawn ride out from Sulky Wend, two riders, one quiet
- The Road of Graves narrows toward the bluff
- Tower of the Attercop rising; webbed crown against the sky
- Weirmoth chatters, Mister Ribbon riding in his cloak
- Othelmedir silent, recounting the path he already knows

## 6. MEMORIALS (3)
- Epitaph for a Tower-staff cook, sealed inside, marker raised by no kin
- Epitaph for an unnamed traveler taken by the gas on the road
- Epitaph addressing the reader: pass quickly, do not breathe deeply

## 7. ESSAY
- The Tower of the Attercop described from below the bluff
- Architecture, the spider-silk patina, the lidded cauldron's false chimney
- Othelmedir's discursive justification: necessary work, ugly methods
- A historian's note on dream-mancy as a dead specialism
- Closes by approaching the seal he himself once set

## 8. VERSE
- Othelmedir whispers the Seal of Neinstitch loose: 'Öltesoldzo sjhaj'
- Sutures part with a sigh; pale green gas pours knee-deep
- Both step back; Weirmoth coughs, Mister Ribbon stops singing
- Cobwebs quiver; spiders stir along the lintel
- Initial fume thins; the doorway yawns open

## 9. INTERVIEW (4) — Weirmoth, while the gas dissipates outside the chrypt
- Q: rest well? / A: confesses he slept poorly, contradicting the brag
- Q: the spear's story? / A: spins the saint-and-bishop fabrication
- Q: the canary? / A: introduces Mister Ribbon, talks to him like family
- Q: why this work? / A: hidden warmth surfaces under the picaro patter

## 10. VERSE
- Entering the Waking Halls, gas pooling in low corners
- Mailed invaders rouse, mouths working: "No quarter for the elder"
- Weirmoth's spear opens the first three; clean, unflashy
- Othelmedir straightens a crooked sconce without looking
- Pockets a tin pendant: lidded cauldron, single open eye
- Reaches a muscle-wall woven across the lower passage

## 11. LEMMA — The Red Pew and Milkmoot
- Entry on the two cabals: the Tower's Red Pew, the invading Milkmoot
- Doctrines, signature necromancies, founding members
- The breach that ended both, sealed by a colleague's gas
- Notes Ostrobone the Rook, hornet-husk mask, leader of Milkmoot
- Anchors the cabal names just spoken aloud

## 12. ESSAY
- The Scriptorium, three floors, balcony over a dead garden
- Books verdigris-patinaed; lectern with three open volumes
- Othelmedir lifts an old draught-treatise of eleven reagents
- Discursive on authorship; declines to claim it aloud
- Internal: these texts are worth more than the village
- Explains the Dreamreel's mechanism: the cauldron, the seepage

## 13. MEMORIALS (4)
- Epitaph for Fellow Sourbell, set by the cabal before the breach
- Epitaph for Fellow Alder the Lank, the same hand
- Epitaph for an unnamed initiate, marker chipped and unfinished
- Epitaph addressed to the cabal itself: we kept the lid; the lid kept us

## 14. ESSAY
- The Still-Room, alchemical diagrams crawling the walls
- The eleven reagents listed; ten in hand, one missing
- Respiration from a Silver Dream: only retrievable from a dream-memory
- Othelmedir pockets new reagents without comment, Weirmoth watches
- Reasons through the second potion: only the unhabituated may drink
- Internal: Weirmoth qualifies; the lie is already half-formed

## 15. INTERVIEW (3) — Weirmoth, in the still-room, distrust rising
- Q: what does the potion show? / A: he drops the patter, asks plainly
- Q: any practitioner? / A: the Generic-Witness lie, ash and habituation
- Q: fair exchange? / A: one quiet line before drinking, mask slipping

## 16. VERSE
- Weirmoth's body slack on the still-room floor
- Othelmedir stands over him, listens to the murmured names
- Memory rises: the night of the breach, blades and deadspeech
- His hand on the lever, the lidded cauldron unsealing
- Colleagues sealed inside, dreaming, his cowardice canonized
- The mercenary will wake knowing all of it

## 17. ESSAY
- Cerebral, measured monologue while Weirmoth dreams
- Self-mythologizing register: cowardice not crime, work not guilt
- Brews the third potion at a side-bench, foreshadowed but unnamed
- Discursive aside on the muscle-wall, already planning its dissolution
- Closes as Weirmoth stirs, the dream nearing its end

## 18. VERSE
- Muscle-wall already gone, passage open, descent begins
- Dining hall: rotted food, overturned chairs, candles to nubs
- Past the ritual circle of the Red Pew, sigils still warm
- Both cabals reanimating, vicious specific combat
- Fellow Sourbell and Fellow Alder the Lank name him: 'Fellow Liddick'
- Weirmoth's spear ends them; Othelmedir's name now naked between them

## 19. LEMMA — The Lidd Decoction
- Entry on the third potion brewed in the still-room
- Composition, the eleven reagents recombined into a smothering agent
- Application: poured into a sealed cauldron of necromantic gas
- Effect: hisses, then quiets, then turns to muck under deadspeech
- Authorship attributed to a former Red Pew alchemist

## 20. VERSE
- Cauldron Chamber: lidded vessel, slow gas spilling
- Weirmoth lowers the spear, will not interfere
- Othelmedir empties the Lidd Decoction into the cauldron
- It hisses; he intones deadspeech across the rim
- The cauldron quiets, settles, turns to a basin of muck

## 21. ESSAY
- Othelmedir turns; Weirmoth's spear ready, considering
- Closing speech in formal archaic cadence, not pleading
- Argues the cabal did no evil deed; the rivals' breach undid the lid
- Argues secrecy was avoidance of ignorant persecution
- Confesses the cowardice, claims it now rectified
- Yields the reagents on Weirmoth's terms; bargain calls for it
- Keeps the tin pendant; the bargain did not name it
