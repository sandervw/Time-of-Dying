# Dreamreel

## 1. ESSAY
- Sulky Wend's affliction: residents dream death-visions, sleep-starved
- The Dreamreel as nagging worry at sleep's edge, crowding out true dreams
- Hedge on whether memory-replacement is gradual or has crossed a threshold
- Mention the sealed Tower above the village; wave at its history
- Address reader on the tactical patience of returning to one's own crime

## 2. LEMMA — The Little Mass of Failing Lamps
- Origin: a funeral rite for cabal-members dying in seclusion
- Conditions of recital: only intoned by ranking practitioners at vigil
- Why a sleepwalking child should not know it: rite was never lay-taught
- Marks one specific death; the name withheld at this point in the entry

## 3. INTERVIEW (3) — Noseman Under Eaves, in the boarding house common room
- Asked to describe the Dreamreel; likens it to a worry that won't stop nagging
- Asked who suffers; names children, hostlers, his own wife
- Asked about a price; produces coin, then names Othelmedir's offer to barter

## 4. MEMORIALS (3)
- Road of Graves marker honoring a cartwright who never reached his appointment
- Marker addressed to riders: "Slacken your reins; the dead listen for hooves"
- Marker for the unburied of the Tower bluff, names left blank

## 5. VERSE
- Approach the bluff at dawn; cobwebs thick on the Tower door
- Whispered deadspeech against the Seal of Neinstitch
- Sutures part with a sigh; pale gas vents out slow
- Step back, wait, watch the green tide thin against the threshold

## 6. INTERVIEW (3) — Weirmoth, on the Tower steps while the gas dissipates
- Asked his trade; admits roadman, grave-carter, occasional spear for hire
- Asked about Mister Ribbon; tells the canary's whole fabricated history
- Asked about last night's sleep; mutters that he, who never dreams, dreamt

## 7. ESSAY
- Reflects on knowing this corridor, this dust, this lock; declines to say so
- The texts inside are worth more than the village; states this plainly
- Hedges on whether the rival cabal left anyone alive past the gas
- Acknowledges Weirmoth as collateral; weighs cost like a butcher weighs meat
- Names the strategic value of formal, archaic speech as armor

## 8. VERSE
- First chamber: invaders rouse, mail rusted into flesh
- They mouth silent breach-orders at the wrong target
- Spear enters a throat where mail and meat have grown one
- Othelmedir straightens a crooked sconce while Weirmoth fights
- Pockets a hammered tin pendant from a pierced cultist's neck

## 9. LEMMA — The Tower of the Attercop
- Original purpose: research-house of the cabal known as The Red Pew
- Architectural quirk: spider-silk patina on every surface since founding
- The Seal of Neinstitch and the gas: a contingency, not a defense
- The cauldron at its base: source-element of the Dreamreel

## 10. VERSE
- Lower passage: the muscle-wall, flesh woven across the corridor
- Spear-tap returns a wet drum-thump; the wall twitches in answer
- Weirmoth swears, low; Othelmedir studies it like a recipe
- Names what is needed: a poison brewed to eleven reagents
- They turn aside for the still-room

## 11. ESSAY
- Scriptorium pause; he loses minutes inside a treatise he once wrote
- Tells Weirmoth how the gas seeps even through a stoppered cauldron
- Frames the Dreamreel as overflow, not malice; technical, not cursed
- Pockets reagents and codices, ignoring Weirmoth's narrowing stare

## 12. MEMORIALS (3)
- Plot for Fellow Hain, "Last to leave the lecture, first to set the kettle"
- Plot for Fellow Vespertine, "She kept the ledgers and the silences"
- Plot for an unnamed bedwarden, marker faceless, attercop silk grown over

## 13. ESSAY
- Lays out the Generic-Witness lie in his own head before he speaks it
- Truth: any practitioner who drinks burns to ash; Weirmoth qualifies
- Omission: the dream is his own, not an anonymous invader's
- Frames the lie as fair exchange, since the brew is genuine and rare
- Hedge to the reader on whether oath-by-barter survives an omission

## 14. VERSE
- Still-room guardian: a dead-dreamer, eyeless, fingers in the kettle
- Spear-thrust; the thing folds without a sound
- Weirmoth wipes the haft on a curtain, pretends he is not shaken
- Othelmedir sets the still and starts measuring reagents

## 15. INTERVIEW (3) — Othelmedir to Weirmoth, presenting the dream-potion
- Asked what it shows; "the first death the gas preserved, an invader's"
- Asked why Othelmedir cannot drink; "I would burn to ash; you will not"
- Asked the price; "an oath, by barter, that you tell me what you saw"

## 16. ESSAY
- Weirmoth lies under; murmurs Red Pew names he should not know
- Internal: the night of the breach, the lever, the sealed-in colleagues
- Cabal combat reframed as cowardice, not crime, in formal cadence
- During the dream, Othelmedir brews the breach potion in silence
- Foreshadows the cauldron and the surrender of the stolen reagents

## 17. VERSE
- Breach potion eats the muscle-wall to threads; descent begins
- Outer cabal members rise; Weirmoth's spear keeps clean rhythm
- Pass the dining hall: rotted bread, candle-stubs, chairs on their backs
- Pass the ritual circle of the Red Pew; chalk lines still vivid

## 18. VERSE
- Fellow Sourbell rises from the alcove, speaks the name Liddick
- Fellow Alder the Lank repeats it, tongue dry as old paper
- Weirmoth glances at Othelmedir, then puts his spear through Sourbell
- Together they take Alder; the name keeps echoing in the corridor

## 19. ESSAY
- The name spoken aloud after twenty years; he treats it as data
- Reframes the breach-night choice: not betrayal, retreat under terms
- Hedge to the reader on whether cowardice can be undone by return
- Notes Weirmoth's silence; expects a confrontation at the cauldron

## 20. VERSE
- Cauldron Chamber: the Lidd Decoction poured into the stoppered mouth
- Hisses; the contents climb, then collapse to muck
- Deadspeech intoned over the muck until the room goes inert
- Turns from the cauldron to find Weirmoth's spear at low guard

## 21. ESSAY
- States his case in formal cadence: the cabal committed no evil
- Plight stems from the breach by Milkmoot, not from the Red Pew
- Secrecy was protection from ignorant persecution, not from guilt
- Yields the reagents on demand; keeps the pendant by silent omission
- Closes by naming the bargain done, the road open

## 22. MEMORIALS (3)
- For the cabal that sealed itself, "Hands on the lever, eyes on the door"
- For Sulky Wend's restored sleep, "May your dreams be your own again"
- For the kept pendant, "Some debts travel with the debtor, unpaid"
