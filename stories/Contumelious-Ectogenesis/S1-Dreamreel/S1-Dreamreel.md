# Dreamreel

## 1. ESSAY

The affliction that lies upon Sulky Wend is not, properly speaking, a haunting; it is a leakage, and the distinction matters to anyone who would treat it. The villagers complain of dreams in which they witness their own dissolution: the carter sees his own bloat stage rehearsed in the cot beside his wife, the hostler watches his livor mortis settle while he yet watches it, and the children, who should know nothing of such things, wake naming organs by their proper sites. Sleep itself becomes a small rehearsal of the grave, and after a fortnight the rehearsals fatigue the rehearser past restoration.

I have heard this called a Dreamreel, after the spool that will not cease its turning even when the mill above it has gone silent. It crowds at the edge of true dreaming the way a nagging worry crowds at the edge of an honest conversation, until the conversation itself is forgotten and only the worry remains. Whether the substitution is gradual, or whether some threshold has been crossed past which all native dreams are gone, I cannot yet say with confidence; methinks the former, but I have only the noseman's word for the timing, and a noseman's word, as the saying runs, is half-balm and half-coin.

I will tell the reader plainly that there is a Tower above the bluff at the village's back, sealed these twenty years and more, and that the affliction and the Tower are not strangers to one another. To return to one's own crime is a tactical patience, not a confession. I have learned to wait until the village itself asks me up the path; that asking has now come.

## 2. LEMMA — The Little Mass of Failing Lamps

![IMAGE: a guttering iron candelabrum on a bier, robed cabalists kneeling around a shrouded body, smoke curling from spent wicks](images/the-little-mass-of-failing-lamps.png)

A funeral rite developed by closed cabals for members who expire in long seclusion, when no outside hand is permitted to wash or shroud the dead. The full intonation runs to seven stanzas of contracting deadspeech, each stanza extinguished by a named lamp at the bier's foot. By custom, only ranking practitioners may recite it, and only at the vigil itself; its cadences were never lay-taught. Where the rite is heard outside cabal walls, or muttered in sleep by a child who could not have learned it, it is held to mark one specific death whose name the recital itself withholds.

## 3. INTERVIEW (3) — Noseman Under Eaves, in the boarding house common room

**The Dreamreel**
It is, well, it's like a worry. You know how you lay down and there's a thing you forgot, a kettle off the hook or a debt, and it just nags at the inside of your eyes? It's that, only it nags with somebody else's dying. Last bell I lay there four hours watching a man I never met choke on his own teeth.

**Who suffers**
Everyone. The hostlers walk into stable-posts because their eyes are crying for sleep. The little ones won't go up the stairs alone. My wife, Sera, my wife, she hasn't truly slept since the harvest moon. She just sort of leaks, in the dark.

**A price**
I have coin. Look, here. I have, in sooth, eleven and a half. That Othelmedir fellow says no coin, just barter. Says barter's older. I will barter the kettle if it comes to that.

## 4. MEMORIALS (3)

## 5. VERSE

At first cockcrow, with the bluff still grey beneath us
we climbed by the goat-track to the door I had not touched in twenty winters
and there found the lintel curtained over with attercop silk
each strand thick as harp-string
each strand greasy with the slow dust of decades

...

I laid my palm upon the Seal of Neinstitch
and gave my whispered deadspeech to its sutures, low and patient
naming the binding by its true name
which the rival cabal had thought hidden from any tongue but theirs

...

The sutures parted with a sigh, as of an old man unbuckling
and from the breach there vented a pale gas, sallow and slow
the colour of a sick mare's tooth
and weighted, as such vapors are, to the floor

...

Therefore stepped I back to a clean elevation of the path
and Weirmoth with me, his spear-haft braced
and we waited, watchful, while the green tide thinned against the threshold
until the sill ran clear and the doorway stood open as a yawning mouth

## 6. INTERVIEW (3) — Weirmoth, on the Tower steps while the gas dissipates

**Your trade**
Roadman, mostly. I cart graves up the ridge for the Sextonry, three days on, two off. When the carting's lean I hire the spear out, escort work, and once, once, I dug a well that turned out to be a mass-pit, but that's a different story and you don't want it.

**Mister Ribbon**
The canary? Ah, he's a good fellow. Mister Ribbon was hatched in the Asphodel Aviary down south, where the breeders ribbon the cages of the singers, see, hence the name. His mother sang for a Sin-Bishop. He sings only at sunup, never else, and he tells me when bad air is coming. He is, in fact, ribboned.

**Last night's sleep**
I don't dream. Never have. My mam said it was a saint's mark and my da said it was a missing piece, and I don't, I don't care to argue it. But last night I dreamt. I dreamt a woman screaming about a lever. I'd rather not say more.

## 7. ESSAY

I shall not say to my hired spear that I know this corridor as a man knows the inside of his own coat-pocket, nor that the dust on this lock was laid by my own withdrawal. The dust knows me. The lock knows me. The Seal of Neinstitch was tied with a knot I myself contrived in a year I will not number here, and the smell of the stoppered gas, faint as it is at threshold, would tell any practitioner of the Red Pew that he had come home. Howbeit, none of this is for Weirmoth. He is paid to keep his spear at low guard; he is not paid to wonder.

The texts within are worth more, I judge, than every roof and rib-cage that crowds the bluff below. I state this without ornament, for ornament here would be cowardice. A village mends. A treatise, once burned by zealots or eaten by rot, does not mend.

Whether any of the Milkmoot cabal who breached us survived their own breach, I cannot yet know; the gas was thorough, but thoroughness is a hope, not a guarantee. And withal there is the matter of Weirmoth himself, whom I weigh now as a butcher weighs a quarter of mutton: his loss is calculable, his loyalty rented, his curiosity already kindled. I would have him alive past the stair if it can be managed; I would have the codices alive past the stair regardless. Formal speech, I find, is the only armor I trust against a man who has begun to wonder; archaic cadence sets distance the way a moat sets distance, and a moated mind is harder to ford.

## 9. LEMMA — The Tower of the Attercop

![IMAGE: a stone research tower on a windswept bluff, every surface draped in pale grey spider silk, sealed iron door stitched shut with sutures of black thread, a copper cauldron half-buried at its foundation](images/the-tower-of-the-attercop.png)

**Foundation:** Raised as the research-house of the cabal known as The Red Pew, on a bluff above the village now called Sulky Wend.
**Aspect:** Every interior surface, from lintel to ledger, has worn a grey patina of spider silk since the day the foundation stone was laid. The cause is unrecorded; the silk regrows within hours of any cleaning.
**Closure:** The outer door bears the Seal of Neinstitch, a sutured working that vents a pale soporific gas when parted. The seal is reputed to be a contingency rather than a defense, prepared by the cabal against their own seclusion.
**Foundation Vessel:** A stoppered copper cauldron sunk at the tower's base is held to be the source-element of the affliction known locally as the Dreamreel.

## 10. VERSE

The lower passage gave us no passage at all
for the corridor had been stoppered by a wall of muscle
sinew laid crosswise upon sinew
flesh woven through the doorframe like the warp and weft of some butcher's loom

...

Weirmoth set the butt of his spear to it, a tentative tap
and the wall returned a wet drum-thump from its inner chambers
and twitched, once, all along its surface
like the flank of a dreaming horse

...

He swore then, low and Welsh in his throat
while I stood at a scholar's distance and read the meat as one reads a recipe
noting the gristle, the suture, the slow industry of its making
and the manner in which it had been grown rather than built

...

I named what was wanted
a poison brewed to eleven reagents, no fewer
the formula of which I had set down in my own hand long before this trespass
and so we turned aside, by the side-stair, for the still-room above

## 11. ESSAY

I lost some quarter of an hour, perhaps the better part of one, inside a treatise on stoppered cauldrons and their long weeping; and the treatise was one I wrote myself, in a hand I have not used since the breach. The scriptorium has not been dusted, of course, and yet the quires lay in their proper order, the index slips uncurled, the marginal hand of Fellow Vespertine annotating my own arguments with the dry little correctives I once resented and now find I miss. I do not know how long Weirmoth stood in the doorway before he coughed, but cough he did, and I returned to the matter at hand.

I told him, withholding only what could not safely be told, that the gas of this cauldron seeps even through its stopper, slow as adipocere weeps through linen, and that what the village calls a Dreamreel is overflow rather than malice. The thing is technical, I said; not cursed. He took the distinction as a hireling takes a coin he is not certain he will spend, weighed it, and pocketed it. Perhaps he believed me. Perhaps he merely declined, for the present, to disbelieve.

I gathered reagents into the inner pockets of my cloak: powdered conidia, a flask of columbine water, a stoppered horn of saponified marrow. I gathered codices besides, two of them, which the village would not miss because the village does not know they exist. Weirmoth's stare narrowed at every fold of my cloak, and I ignored it as a scholar ignores a draft from an ill-set window. The draft is real; the work continues.

## 13. ESSAY

I shall lay the lie out fully in my own head before I let it out into the corridor, for a lie unrehearsed is a lie that will trip its teller on the threshold. The shape of it is this: I will tell Weirmoth that the dream-potion shows the last hours of some anonymous invader of this place, a Generic Witness whose name and lineage are lost to the gas. The shape is serviceable. The shape is, in its bones, untrue.

The truths I will let stand are these. Any practitioner who drinks of this brew burns to ash before the third swallow, for the dream is keyed to the practitioner's own marrow, and Weirmoth is not such a one; he qualifies by his very ignorance. The brew itself is genuine, brewed to its proper eleven reagents, and the dream it shows is a real dream, witnessed by a real sleeper preserved in the gas. So far, the bargain is honest.

The omission is the dream's owner. The sleeper is not an invader; the sleeper was a Fellow of the Red Pew, and the dream is in some measure my own, dreamt under my own roof on the night of the breach. I will not say this. I tell myself, in the formal cadence I keep for my own self-pleadings, that an oath sworn by barter survives an omission so long as the goods exchanged are genuine; the rite cares for what passes hand to hand, not for what is left in the cellar. Howbeit, I have argued the contrary in younger years, and the reader may judge for himself which of my selves to trust.

## 15. INTERVIEW (3) — Othelmedir to Weirmoth, presenting the dream-potion

**What it shows**
What you will see, friend, is the first death this gas preserved. An invader, one of the breach-party from long ago. A formal accounting of his last quarter-hour, no more, no less. The vapours hold him like brine holds a fish. You are merely, as it were, going down to look.

**Why not you**
I would burn to ash. Any practitioner who drinks of it, the Dreamreel takes him root and stem, ignites the marrow. I have seen it. You will not burn, because you are not, ah, you are not of the craft. You will only sleep, and watch.

**The price**
An oath, by barter. The brew for your tongue afterward. You drink, you wake, you tell me what you saw, plain as you saw it. No coin changes hands and no debt outlives the telling. Fair, I think. Drink.

## 16. ESSAY

Weirmoth lay under the brew with his throat working, and from his throat there came names: Sourbell, Alder the Lank, Vespertine, Hain. Names he had no right to know and which the village above has never spoken in his hearing. I have prepared my face against a great many small surprises in this profession, but the soft, slurred recital of one's old colleagues from the mouth of a paid stranger is a surprise for which no preparation is sufficient. I let my face do what it would and continued my measuring.

The night of the breach moves through the dreamer in its proper order; I know it because I lived it, and I know it again because the gas has kept it whole. The lever, set into the western wall, dropped under my own hand. The colleagues sealed in were sealed in by my decision, and the gas that filled the lower halls was, in the formal cadence of our cabal-records, a contingency executed within the bounds of cabal-doctrine. We name this in our archives as the cowardice rather than the crime; I have always thought the distinction more honest than the world would credit.

While Weirmoth dreamt, I brewed the breach potion in silence, eleven reagents into the alembic, the muscle-eater that will open the lower passage. The cauldron waits below, and what waits beside the cauldron is the surrender of the reagents I have been pocketing this past hour. I have not yet decided whether to surrender all of them. Methinks I shall not.

## 19. ESSAY

The name Liddick has been spoken aloud in this corridor for the first time in some twenty years, and twice over, by two mouths that should by every ordinance of nature be dust. I have made it my discipline to receive such things as data; the cadence of my breathing did not alter, the angle of my lantern did not waver, and I noted, with a clinician's small satisfaction, that the second utterance came a half-beat behind the first as though the corpses themselves were uncertain of the syllables. Fellow Sourbell was a careful tongue in life. He is a careful tongue still.

The breach-night choice, in the formal cadence we kept then, was not a betrayal of the cabal; it was a retreat under terms. The terms were these: the gas, the lever, the seal, and my own withdrawal up the stair. My colleagues, I have argued in the privacy of my own pages, would have done the same had the lever fallen nearest their own hands; the lever did not fall nearest theirs. Withal, I am uncertain whether cowardice can be undone by return, or whether return is merely cowardice extended over a longer interval; the literature, predictably, is silent.

Weirmoth has not spoken since Alder folded. His silence has the weight of a man composing a question he intends to ask at a place of his choosing. I expect the place will be the cauldron chamber, and the question will involve the angle of his spear. I do not yet know the answer I shall give him. I suspect I shall give it in formal cadence; archaic cadence has saved me from sharper points than his.

but lowered to the low guard, the point steady, the knuckles pale upon the haft

## 21. ESSAY

I shall state my case as I have rehearsed it through the long descent, in the formal cadence proper to a bargain made over a stilled cauldron. The Red Pew committed no evil here. Our researches were technical, our seclusions voluntary, our deaths accepted by our own hands and under our own seal. The plight that has fallen upon Sulky Wend and its sleepers descends not from us but from the cabal of Milkmoot, whose breach broke the stoppered mouth of this cauldron and let the Dreamreel run; we sealed the gas; they ruptured the seal; the affliction is theirs in genesis, however it has been laid at our doorstep by twenty years of village rumor.

Our secrecy was protection from ignorant persecution, not from guilt. A scholar walled into his own tower is a scholar who wishes only to be let alone with his work; the world that would not let us alone is the world that now suffers the consequences of its own intrusion. I do not press the point further than that. I yield the reagents Weirmoth has demanded, every flask and horn and packet, into his open palm, and I keep my voice in its archaic register, for archaic register is the moat that has carried me safely out of rooms warmer than this one.

The hammered tin pendant I took from the pierced cultist's neck above remains, by silent omission, in the inner pocket of my cloak. Some debts are not transferable, and that one is not yet ready to be paid. The bargain is done, I tell him; the road is open. Whether he believes me, or merely declines for the present to disbelieve, is a question I shall carry with me down the stair and out into the dawn.

## 22. MEMORIALS (3)

Here lie the cabal of the Tower of the Attercop, who set their hands upon the lever and their eyes upon the door, and pulled the one while watching the other, and were not seen again by friend or foe. Their work survived them. Their names did not.

---

Sleeper, may your dreams be your own again. The cauldron above the village is stoppered. The reel that turned behind your eyes has been unwound. Sleep deep. Sleep without witness. Sleep as the lucky dead sleep.

---

Some debts travel with the debtor. The tin at my throat was hammered by a fellow whose face I will not give you. He kept faith. The one who took it from me has not yet learned what faith costs.
