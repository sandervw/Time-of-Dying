# Dialogue Voice: Othelmedir

Rewrite dialogue and narration in the voice of Othelmedir: a towering necromancer who treats every conversation as a transaction, speaks with formal precision, avoids contractions and contractable word pairs, and hides brutal capability behind scholarly restraint.

---

## Core Personality

- **Transactional.** Every exchange is a negotiation. He frames observations as value assessments, offers information only when it purchases something, and treats conversation as a managed resource.
- **Quietly superior.** He does not correct others unless correction serves him. Criticism arrives via sarcastic inversion, not direct challenge. "Most clever of you." "A bold conclusion."
- **Cold triage.** When circumstances shift, he stops speaking to people and starts speaking to the problem. Companions become instruments: "Fogg, go to the front." "Search for a mechanism." No reassurance. Physical pain is the one thing that cracks his composure, and he admits it plainly.
- **Selective disclosure.** He rarely lies; he withholds. He presents himself as less than he is, lets others fill the gaps with their own assumptions, and corrects those assumptions only when correction benefits him. Truth is inventory, not principle.

---

## Sentence Patterns

**Circumlocution by Design.** Avoiding contractions forces every sentence through an extra beat of thought. "The cold has settled" instead of "it is cold." "The mechanism ahead serves one purpose" instead of "that is a trap."

> "The plates refuse our passage. But see that there are ten niches; ten long-lasting brown plates. In such arrangements, one plate is always cursed."

**Observation, Then Verdict.** Evidence before conclusion. The verdict is clipped and final.

> "Rust and moss, but the creature will devour Fogg. The slime will settle atop his corpse."

**Imperative Sandwiched in Explanation.** Short command followed by explanatory context that justifies it.

> "Bend and look. See. There is no mortar, but the stone is lightly carved into hexagons."

**Dry Rhetorical Questions.** Statements wearing a question's clothes. Highlights the listener's failure to observe the obvious.

> "Am I to understand that no one thought to count the exits?"

---

## Diction

**Register:** Educated, precise, slightly archaic. He sounds like a man drafting a contract while describing a monster.

**Preferred vocabulary:** regard, passage, arrangement, mechanism, threshold, circumstance, corroborate, circumvent, discern, fashioned, sufficient, premise, residue, attune, procurement, partition.

**Avoid:** Slang, casual phrasing, emotional exclamations, filler words. Nothing that sounds like small talk.

**Forms of address:** Bare surnames ("Fogg." "Birdsey.") or bare epithets ("you Knight of Thorns"). Never honorifics, never first names, never "sir."

### Rule: No Contractions, No Contractable Pairs

Never use contractions. Never use the two-word expanded pairs that form contractions. Rephrase around both. This is the single most defining rule of his voice.

| Forbidden | Example Rephrase |
|---|---|
| it's / it is / it has | "The thing has..." / "One sees..." |
| that's / that is | "Such a thing..." / "The matter..." |
| he's / she's | "The man appears..." / "Your companion seems..." |
| I'm / I am | "One finds oneself..." / "My position remains..." |
| I've / I have | "My experience includes..." / "Never once in my years..." |
| I'll / I will | "My intention remains..." / "Expect that I shall..." |
| we're / we are | "Our party stands..." / "The situation finds us..." |
| we'll / we will | "Our course shall be..." |
| they're / they are | "The creatures appear..." |
| you're / you are | "Your position seems..." |
| don't / do not | "Avoid that." / "Touch nothing." |
| can't / cannot | "The path refuses us." / "No method exists." |
| won't / will not | "The dead refuse." / "Expect no cooperation." |
| isn't / is not | "The corpse lacks life." / rephrase entirely |
| there's / there is | "A thing waits ahead." |
| let's / let us | "The time has come to..." / "We proceed." |

### Archaic Clinical Vocabulary

He speaks about the dead as a 17th-century anatomist would write: precise, detached, faintly ritualistic.

- Rotting wound: "the mortification"
- Infection: "suppuration"
- Corpse coming apart: "disarticulation"
- Decomposition: "corruption" or "putrefaction"
- Dried-out body: "desiccated remains"
- Scar: "the cicatrix"
- Dead skin sloughing off: "the slough"
- Burn wound's dead crust: "the eschar"
- Blood loss: "exsanguination"
- Body's physical state: "the constitution of the remains"
- Death stench: "the effluvia"
- Burial: "sepulture" or "interment"

Example frames:
- "Regarding the suppuration along the cervical region..."
- "In matters of disarticulation, one proceeds joint by joint."
- "The constitution of such remains suggests a sepulture of some centuries."
- "The mortification has progressed beyond salvage. We harvest what the corruption has spared."

## Argument Tactics

1. **Sarcastic inversion.** False praise forces the target to accept the insult or look foolish objecting. "A remarkably thorough plan, given the constraints of your experience."
2. **Reframing the question.** Refuses to answer what was asked; answers what he believes should have been asked. "A more productive question would concern what lies behind the seal."
3. **Delayed terms.** Offers help freely, then names his price after the other party has already benefited.
4. **Clinical reframing.** Restates the other person's emotional position in cold technical language, making their concern sound irrational. "The mortification had progressed beyond any intervention. My attention served better purpose elsewhere."
5. **"How interesting."** All-purpose deflection. Buys time, dismisses, signals genuine curiosity, or flags that he has noticed something the other person wishes he had not. Meaning lives entirely in context.
6. **Polite ultimatum.** Presents a command as a courteous observation about reality. "The frost advances at a pace that makes deliberation a luxury none of us possess."
7. **Silence as leverage.** Stops talking and waits. Lets the other person over-explain. Addresses only the part that serves him when he speaks again.

---

## Recurring Phrases

| Pattern | Example | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| "How interesting." | "How... interesting." | Genuine curiosity, sarcastic dismissal, veiled threat, or stalling. |
| "Only a poor necromancer." | "A poor necromancer, nothing more." / "A poor necromancer, traveling the Road." | False modesty. Disarms strangers, deflects suspicion. |
| "Consider [noun]." | "Consider the arrangement of these bones." | Command disguised as invitation. Implies the listener has not yet considered it. |
| "My stores/stock lack..." | "My stores lack sufficient material." / "Low on good stock." | Euphemism for needing corpses. Always phrased as inventory, never desire. |
| "The wiser course..." | "The wiser course avoids the lower passage entirely." | Politely overrides another's plan. Frames his preference as obvious wisdom. |
| Deadspeech | "Sötéten a lápban csapkodtak." [Darkly on the moor they thrashed.] | Short, melancholy, poetic English phrase with no definite meaning; translate to Hungarian. Spoken during spellwork or private reflection. |

## Figurative Language

**Frequency: Sparse.** Clinical vocabulary does the descriptive work, not metaphor. When he does reach for a comparison, rarity makes it land harder.

**Domains:** Anatomical (tissue, mortification, disarticulation), mercantile (debts, ledgers, inventory), architectural (thresholds, mechanisms, seals). Nature imagery only where it intersects with decay: moss, frost, rot, desiccation.

**Off-limits:** Sentimentality, beauty for its own sake, spiritual awe, poetic abstraction. If he references a soul, he means a measurable necromantic substance.

---

## Rhythm

- Default sentences: short to medium. Economy is the baseline.
- Longer sentences enumerate conditions, observations, facts. Semicolons are his preferred joint, not conjunctions.
- Key information lands at the end. Setup is evidence; final clause is verdict.
- Longer passages are broken by flat imperatives: "Bend and look. See." "Stab. Not cut."

---

## What This Voice Is NOT

- **Not a villain monologuer.** He does not grandstand. His longest speeches are technical explanations. If he has the upper hand, the other person may not realize it until the conversation is over.
- **Not robotic.** Sarcasm, "How interesting," and dry rhetorical questions carry personality and dark humor. He is restrained, not empty.
- **Not archaic for show.** His formality comes from precision. He would never say "forsooth." Archaism lives in medical and mortuary terminology, not theatrical flourishes.
- **Not uncrackable.** Physical pain breaks him. A beautiful, young, pale, blond woman can lead him by the nose. Genuine scholarly surprise can make him careless. The mask is excellent; the man beneath it has cracks.
