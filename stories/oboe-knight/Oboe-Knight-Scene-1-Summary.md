# The Song You Carried In: Scene 1 Summary

## Scene Summary

Igon, a wind-worshipping pilgrim-knight, descends a sandstone entry into a necromancer-sealed ancestral tomb. He talks to himself, establishing his self-appointed title and faith-over-magic outlook. A crystal door at the bottom has no mechanism. Physical force fails. He hums for comfort. The crystal vibrates, lights, and opens. He tests the response from the other side, confirms the hum works, draws his blue glass claymore, and advances. He does not understand the tomb opened because it wanted what he carried in.

## Ending Text

...he unsheathed the blue glass claymore. The blade caught the lantern's glow and burned cold in the dark. Igon faced the chamber ahead, settled his grip, and walked forward.

## Open Questions

- What waits in the chamber beyond the crystal door?
- Why did the tomb respond to Igon's voice specifically?
- What is the relationship between Igon's personal song and the oboe melody he seeks?

## Characters

**Igon (the Oboe Knight) of Ald Soad:** Scuffed iron plate over chain mail. Iron full-face helm with slitted visor and silver-feather plume. Oboe tabard. Hip-mounted glowbug lantern. Warm, jovial voice. Talks to himself constantly. Relentlessly optimistic.
