# The Song You Carried In: Scene 2 Summary

## Scene Summary

Igon enters an L-shaped shrine chamber with ten stone shrines holding desiccated offerings: dried apricots, a knotted cord, a clay thumb-ring. At the far end, a Worm Locker contains a pinned skeleton whose jaw works without tendons, emitting a sustained note. The melody is familiar; he cannot place it. He hums along. The corpse awakens. A voice speaks through it: "what does it feel like," delivered without inflection. The skeleton tears free and attacks. Igon bisects it at the spine with his blue glass claymore and crushes the skull underfoot. The jaw clicks twice and stops. He uses his hum to open one of two crystal resonance doors. A new note begins deeper in the tomb. He walks through.

## Ending Text

...the crystal answered. Green light pooled in the seams and the door slid wide, and somewhere deeper in the tomb another note began, faint and sustained, waiting for him to match it. Igon walked through.

## Open Questions

- Why does the tomb respond to Igon's hum specifically?
- Is the tomb echoing his own song back to him?
- What waits behind the second crystal door?

## Characters

**Igon (the Oboe Knight) of Ald Soad:** Scuffed iron plate over chain mail. Iron full-face helm with slitted visor and silver-feather plume. Oboe tabard. Hip-mounted glowbug lantern. Blue Glass Claymore. Warm, jovial voice. Talks to himself. Relentlessly optimistic; treats combat with casual confidence.

**Guilliam the Unthinking (via corpse):** First appearance. Speaks through a pinned skeleton in a thin, reedy, musical voice. Asks clinical questions without inflection. No physical form; presence conveyed through sound and animated dead.
