# The Song You Carried In: Scene 6 - Post-Scene Summary

## Scene Summary

Igon enters the final shrine room and collapses halfway to the platform where Guilliam's desiccated bones lie in a ring of stone. He sits cross-legged, silent, drained of his personal song. Guilliam speaks through the pipes with clinical gratitude, revealing the oboe was a lure seeded in the wind decades ago. He dismisses his lost servants, probes Igon about the surviving necromancer-gods, and declares himself nearly whole. The narrator reveals what Guilliam cannot grasp: his bones will never move. The intelligence swells bodiless through the pipes, vast and bright, a mind without a body. The scene ends abruptly with Guilliam singing through the tohmb in Igon's stolen voice, certain of a revival that will never come.

## Ending Text

...Every surface of the tohmb sang. The melody climbed the stairs. It poured through the obelisks. It washed over the bones that would never rise. And Guilliam sang on.

## Questions Posed

- Does Igon survive the tohmb? Can he leave, or is he trapped with the singing intelligence?
- What becomes of Guilliam, swelling forever in pipes but never restored to flesh?
- Can the personal song be reclaimed, or is it permanently lost?

## Characters

**Igon (the Oboe Knight):** Iron plate over chain mail, full-face helm with silver-feather plume, oboe tabard torn to shreds. Blue Glass Claymore across his knees. Physically devastated, bleeding, silent. The relentless optimist reduced to stillness.

**Guilliam the Unthinking:** No physical form. Desiccated bones on the platform, immobile. Voice issues from pipes throughout the tohmb, musical and clinical. Speaks with borrowed warmth. Certain of imminent revival. Cannot conceive of his own impossibility.

## Reflection

**What worked:** Image-sound counterpoint lands hard. Guilliam's clinical warmth. The abrupt cut. "And Guilliam sang on" as final line. The bones that never move.

**What was cut in review:** Trimmed Guilliam's oboe-lure speech for clipped pacing.

**What's missing:** Igon's interior is entirely implied. Intentional, but a single line of thought could deepen.
