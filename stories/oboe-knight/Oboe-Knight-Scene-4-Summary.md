# The Song You Carried In: Scene 4 Summary

## Scene Summary

Igon descends a narrowing passage devoid of dead. He finds thin pipes cut into the walls and understands the tohmb's mechanism: his voice was channeled through these pipes to animate the corpses above. Every sound fed the demon. He goes silent. The passage ends at a double door sealed with necromancer runes in black wax. Someone sealed this deliberately. Igon drives his claymore through the seal and steps through without a word.

## Ending Text

He lifted his Blue Glass Claymore, unmarred despite every corpse it had cut, and drove it through the seal. The wax cracked. The wood splintered. He stepped through.

## Questions

- Does Igon's silence protect him, or has the demon already harvested enough?
- Who sealed the door with necromancer runes, and why leave rather than destroy what lay below?
- What waits in the vestibule beyond?

## Characters

**Igon (the Oboe Knight) of Ald Soad:** Scuffed iron plate over chain mail. Iron full-face helm with slitted visor, silver-feather plume. Oboe tabard. Blue Glass Claymore. Previously jovial; now entirely silent. Understanding the mechanism has not stopped him, only quieted him.

**Guilliam the Unthinking:** No direct appearance. Present as a sustained note vibrating through the stone, growing stronger. Patient, ambient, inescapable.

## Reflection

**Worked:** Pipe-discovery paragraph carries the scene's weight. Bellows simile ties mechanism to the organ metaphor. Silence-vs-vibration counterpoint sustains tension without combat.

**Cut in review:** "clean and bright as the day it was forged" replaced with "unmarred despite every corpse it had cut."

**Missing:** Guilliam has no direct voice. Intentional; saves it for Scene 5.
