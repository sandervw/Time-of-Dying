# Named Locations

## Regions / Lands
- **Tveland** — central land containing Galgenbeck and Sarkash
- **Kergüs** — frozen northern expanse
- **The Western Kingdom / Wästland** — western kingdom, once prosperous
- **Südglans** — southern empire that sank into the sea (referenced in Wretched Royalty backstory)

## Cities & Settlements
- **Galgenbeck** — greatest city, seat of the Two-Headed Basilisks, in Tveland
- **Grift** — city-state on eastern peninsula, reached by three cyclopean bridges
- **Alliáns** — storm-piercing spire-city of black glass in northern Kergüs; seat of Blood-Countess Anthelia
- **Schleswig** — "sad-but-gaudy" city in Wästland, seat of King Fathmu IX

## Wilderness & Natural Features
- **Sarkash** — dark forest in Tveland
- **Bergen Chrypt** — massive mountain/catacomb complex bounding the world to the west (also referenced as "the Spears" in Psalm II)
- **Endless Sea** — surrounds the world
- **Lake Onda** — in Wästland, once rich with fish
- **The Múr** — bottomless chasm cutting Grift off from the mainland
- **Terion** — thousand-meter vertical cliff at Grift

## Landmarks & Structures
- **Cathedral of the Two-Headed Basilisks** — in Galgenbeck
- **Graven-Tosk** — ancient cemetery deep in Sarkash
- **Palace of the Shadow King** — gothic black castle rising over Graven-Tosk
- **Valley of the Unfortunate Undead** — lethal valley where the basilisk HE is coiled
- **Thel-Emas' lost temple** — source of divining stones (Heretical Priest gear)
- **Urilian crypts** — source of rare anti-obsidian (Basilisk demand)
- **Tvelandian orphanarium** — referenced in Gutterborn Scum origins

## Islands
- **Crëlut** — heretic isle, two nautical miles east of Grift

## Otherworldly / Metaphysical
- **Shimmering Fields** — the afterlife/paradise
- **Realm of the Dead** — where the Valley's black soil drags wanderers
- **Refva** — dimension/boundary through which one falls to reach Cube-Violet
- **Cube-Violet** — esoteric dimension of mythical obscurity

## Establishments (named)
- **The Paunchy Swine** — tavern (adventure spark table)

# Vermis

## Cities & Settlements
- **Agerutt** (II) — desert city, heart of the old world's civilization; rare pocket of human life surrounded by silent, wounded lands
- **Family house** (III) — unnamed; cited as a new "everyday" location type introduced in book III

## Wilderness & Natural Features
- **Deadman's Garden** (I) — peaceful starting clearing of ferns and mosses, protected by a sleeping giant's skeleton
- **Greengrave** (I) — vast graveyard region; "a garden for the dead"
- **The Silver Swamp** (I) — cursed swamp hexed by witches into a death trap; the one region in book I without hostile skeletons
- **The Flutewood Forest** / **Flutewood** (I) — forest of whistling, hole-ridden trees that produce an unrelenting melody
- **Returning swamps** (III) — reviewers describe a return to book I's bogs and wetlands (specific names not yet documented online)

## Landmarks & Structures
- **The Isolated Crypt** (I) — first dungeon; exits via a cliffside
- **The Pauper's Catacombs** (I) — catacomb dungeon reached from Greengrave
- **The Cursed Cabin** (I) — shelter deep in Flutewood; its fireplace hides a ring that turns your weapon into a flaming sword
- **A House Without Windows** (I) — locked structure past Flutewood, gated by Helmet's Beast (also rendered "House Without Shadows")
- **The Goblin Princess's Tomb** (I) — ancient tomb guarded by the Goblin Knight
- **The Pestilent Mines (Upper)** (I) — diseased mine region
- **Green Sun Ruins** (I) — ruined area; site of the necromancer-and-skeleton-horde encounter and the Hemlet's Beast pit
- **The Colossal Thrones** (I) — late-game region
- **The Drowned Labyrinth** (I) — final area, deep underground
- **The Whispering Fountain** (II) — cloaked statue pouring a lulling stream; a lure/trap

## Otherworldly / Metaphysical
- **The Glass Purgatory** (II) — the book's central labyrinth; a place divided between reality and illusion, floating in eternal limbo
- **Inner Purgatory** (II) — inner sub-region of the Glass Purgatory, haunted by restless souls
- **Heart of the Purgatory** (II) — innermost chamber, guarded by the Conjoined Warrior
- **Glass Nest** (II) — resting place of the Sword/Scepter of the Primordial Glass
