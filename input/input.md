## Verse

- **verse** - like a series of modern text messages, but written by a learned necromancer in Ashton-Smithian dialogue; *replacement for ACTION*


# Verse-Blob Rules (General ideas)

## Hard Rules

1. **Split at clause-level punctuation.** Semicolons, colons, and commas that separate independent clauses or stack fronted adverbials are bubble boundaries. Parenthetical commas (around appositives, inline qualifiers, mid-clause asides) stay INSIDE their bubble and are not split points. Use judgment: if a comma joins two ideas, split it; if a comma wraps a modifier around one idea, keep it.

2. **Drop terminal punctuation on each bubble.** The line break replaces the comma, semicolon, colon, or period that originally ended the clause. Internal punctuation (parenthetical commas inside a bubble) is retained. The only exception is proper-noun or phrase-internal punctuation that belongs to the content itself.

3. **Stack bubbles within a sentence with no blank line between them.** Bubbles that belong to the same source sentence appear as adjacent lines, back to back, no spacing.

4. **Separate sentences with a blank line, an ellipsis on its own line, and another blank line.** Structure: blank line / `...` / blank line. The ellipsis stands in for the period between sentences and signals the drawing of breath before the next barrage.

5. **Preserve the base voice verbatim where possible.** Do not rephrase clauses for brevity or punch. A bubble may be a single word ("when") or a long qualifying clause; do not pad short ones or trim long ones.

6. **Preserve the preamble / development / revelation arc across the stacked bubbles of a single sentence.** The key information still lands on the LAST bubble of the sentence, not the first.

7. **No texting-native tics.** No emoji, no lowercase drift, no contractions introduced by the format, no stray fragments that aren't organic to the source sentence, no timestamps, no typing indicators, no greetings, no sign-offs.

8. **Per-bubble length: 3 to 25 words.**

9. **Per-group word count: 40 to 90 words.** A "group" is the set of stacked bubbles belonging to one source sentence.

10. **Per-group bubble count: 3 to 8 bubbles.**

## Formatting

- **Within sentence:** one bubble per line, no blank lines between.
- **Between sentences:** blank line / `...` on its own line / blank line.
- **Tense:** match the source passage. Past-tense stays past-tense; the narrator is recounting, not livetexting mid-crisis.
- **No em-dashes, no exclamation marks, no ellipses as filler** (ellipses are reserved for the sentence-break separator only).

## Quick Test

A clause-split texted passage passes if:

1. Restoring the original terminal punctuation at the end of each bubble and concatenating produces the source prose verbatim (or near-verbatim — parenthetical commas inside bubbles should survive the round trip).
2. Each sentence's FINAL bubble carries the key revelation of that sentence.
3. No bubble has been invented, condensed, or rephrased beyond the source clause.
4. No bubble contains a texting-native tic (emoji, lowercase, contraction, fragment, sign-off) the base voice would refuse.
5. The visual rhythm clearly distinguishes within-sentence stacking from between-sentence separation; a reader can count sentences by counting ellipsis lines plus one.

## Example

```
Then sprang I forth unto the table
and against its edge sprawling
for the great Chrypt-Keep of Carcë was shaken anew as one shaketh a dice-box
and the lightnings rent the heavens
and the thunder rolled without cessation
and the sound of subterraneal waters stunned the ear of that chamber
and above the whole tumult there pealed yet the charnel laughter of the King

...

And I, the adept of deadspeech, knew then that it was with the King even as it had been with his predecessor in cycles long gone
when sovereign strength forsook him
and the spirit within tare at him
and plastered those chamber-walls with his lifeblood

...

Yet was I mindful, even amid that hideous storm of terror, of the ninety-seventh page whereon the King had shown me the word of dismissal
and so, in that selfsame breath, I wrenched the tome from his palsied grasp
and turned unto the page

...

Scarce had mine eye found the syllable
when there swept into the chamber a whirlwind of hail and sleet
and the candles were snuffed out in an instant
and the tables overset
and the whole place reduced unto a clamor of blind motion

...

And in the plunging darkness
beneath the ceaseless crashing of the thunder
I, pitching headlong from my place, felt claws of no earthly mensuration clasp fast upon my head and body
and bear me downward

...

Howbeit in that extremity
and through the agony of those claws
I cried aloud the word which had been vouchsafed me, the word TRIPSARECOPSEM
and therewith fell a-swooning into a darkness whence I knew not whether I should return
```
